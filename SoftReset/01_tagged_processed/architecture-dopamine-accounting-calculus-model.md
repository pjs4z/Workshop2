## Dopamine Accounting Calculus Model

The model establishes a running balance of dopamine by treating every daily behavior as a credit or debit that rises or falls back toward baseline at a predictable half-life. Each action is assigned a standardized nanomole shift, graded by research quality, and entered with a timestamp and sign. The cumulative effect of these time-stamped impulses, once passed through exponential decay, yields a continuously updated reserve that starts at one hundred percent and drifts above or below homeostasis depending on the mix of boosts and leaks.

At the end of each twenty-four-hour cycle the remaining reserve is converted to a five-point fitness score, turning complex neurochemical dynamics into an intuitive daily metric. A companion analytics engine graphs the reserve curve, highlights the biggest positive and negative contributors, and invites users to experiment with lifestyle adjustments by revealing how sleep, exercise, stressors, and substances interact in real time.

To ground the framework in physiology, a two-week pilot will enroll healthy adults who log behaviors while providing blood, wearable, and mood data. Correlations between modeled reserves, dopamine metabolites, and subjective vitality will test the system’s validity; discrepancies will be used to recalibrate behavior coefficients and decay constants. Successful alignment would justify larger studies and position the model as a practical dashboard for managing dopaminergic health.
