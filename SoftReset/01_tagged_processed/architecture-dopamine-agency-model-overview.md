## Dopamine Agency Model Overview

The framework defines a unified, research-anchored map of factors that shape both dopaminergic drive and a person’s capacity to act with intention. It catalogues measurable signals spanning internal states, biological rhythms, environmental conditions, habitual loops, self-narratives, and social safety nets, classifying each by its directional influence on agency and by evidential weight. These signals are linked in a causal graph that clarifies how changes in one domain propagate through the system, while a rule engine continuously scans incoming data to launch just-in-time prompts—exercise, breathing, digital boundaries, social outreach, focus protection—aimed at nudging the most leverageable variables toward agency-positive zones. A curated evidence library underwrites every link and intervention, maintaining scientific traceability.

Several forces still elude reliable capture. Creative cognition, deep narrative coherence, fine-grained emotion regulation, direct neurochemical activity, and broader cultural or neighbourhood context sit outside current sensor reach. The programme mitigates this by pairing behavioural or linguistic proxies with emerging biosensors and public data feeds, positioning the model to absorb richer signals as technology matures. Through iterative closure of these gaps, the system seeks to furnish an ever more complete, sovereignty-preserving substrate for personalised agency optimisation.
