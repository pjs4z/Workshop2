## Dopamine Resilience Recovery Framework for ADHD

The framework sets out to restore a healthy, self-sustaining dopamine rhythm in adults with ADHD so that prescription stimulants become optional tools rather than daily crutches. Grounded in contemporary neuroscience and behavioral science, it treats dopamine regulation as a trainable skill: compulsive reward loops are dismantled, natural receptor sensitivity is rebuilt, and the prefrontal cortex is re-engaged through deliberate stressors, structured rest and calibrated rewards.

Progress is governed by a weekly resilience score derived from continuous logging of sleep, movement, nutrition, digital exposure, medication use and social engagement. An AI engine converts these signals into personalized insight, highlighting cause-and-effect patterns and prescribing targeted adjustments—whether a cold plunge, breathwork session, meal tweak or media fast—to smooth spikes, fill troughs and raise baseline motivation. Key performance indicators on sleep regularity, physical vitality, stimulant consumption and real-world productivity appear in a live dashboard that guides users, coaches and peer cohorts; sustained improvements unlock deeper interventions such as immersive retreats. By fusing data, coaching, community and a library of evidence-based protocols, the system aims to deliver “stimulant indifference”: steady focus, mood and drive emerging from the brain’s own chemistry rather than pharmacological peaks and crashes.
