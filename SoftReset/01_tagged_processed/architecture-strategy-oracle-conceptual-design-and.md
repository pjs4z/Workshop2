## Strategy Oracle Conceptual Design and Schema Overview

The system presents a person-centric advisory model that treats an individual’s ambitions, underlying drivers and decision style as first-class data, then situates this profile inside a detailed portrait of the organization that surrounds it. By pairing personal motive with structural and cultural realities, the design rejects abstract, one-size guidance and instead frames choice as bounded, context-embedded action; the strategic rationale is that advice carries weight only when it accounts for both what someone wants and what their environment will permit.

To generate such guidance, the architecture unifies three information streams—a semantic record of goals and motivations, a contextual map of organizational objectives, rules, norms and external pressures, and a library of cause-and-effect reasoning patterns that translate motive-plus-context into viable moves. Dedicated extraction and transformation pipelines continuously draw data from surveys, documents and other sources, reconcile it into this shared representation, and feed a reasoning engine that matches conditions to strategy templates, enriches them with lessons from prior cases, and produces transparent recommendations.

The intended result is a dynamic companion that helps users navigate careers, projects or personal endeavours with advice that is simultaneously personalized and organizationally realistic, closing the gap between private aspiration and institutional constraint while adapting effortlessly across domains and over time.
