## The Reset Mind Database Schema Design

The data model arranges our knowledge system around four concentric layers that mirror how purpose turns into action.  At the core, the DNA layer safeguards the mission, values, and positioning statements, then continuously gauges every downstream asset against those principles through an automated alignment tracker.  Surrounding that, the Brain layer catalogs the scientific foundations of our work—research summaries, evidence links, methodological blueprints, and the network of dependencies that tie one insight to the next—so each intervention is both traceable and empirically grounded.

The Aura layer translates that intellect into experience.  It codifies voice personas, visual guidelines, and reusable content patterns, then records each creative output alongside its originating voice, template, and quality metrics.  In doing so, brand expression stays consistent, measurable, and instantly adjustable.

The outer Services layer turns ideas into deliverables.  A job ledger captures every request, drives it through ordered execution steps, validates it against predefined output schemas, and returns a final artifact whose provenance, quality scores, and mission-alignment are stored for later review.  Shared utilities knit the layers together: lineage tables map how one piece of content begets another, semantic tags provide cross-domain searchability, and usage analytics expose real-world engagement.

Vector references point each record to its embedding in an external store, enabling high-speed semantic search while keeping operational data inside the database.  Row-level security, realtime change feeds, and orchestration functions are wired in from the start, ensuring the system remains multi-tenant safe, observable, and ready for automated scaling.  A staged migration path—core tables first, integration surfaces next, guardrails before production—completes the blueprint, positioning the platform for rapid yet disciplined evolution.
