## Color System Principles

The interface lives on a deep, cinematic canvas. Everything begins with Midnight Navy, a near-black blue that quiets the screen and primes the eye for focus; Charcoal Gray slips in as structure, easing strain without breaking the nocturnal spell. Against this disciplined darkness, a single burst of color carries the story: Forge Orange, our molten signature, ignites calls to action and signals transformation. When the stakes escalate, Phoenix Red enters—a precise flash of danger used only for genuinely critical moments. Volt Gold tempers the palette with optimism, offering warm highlights that guide without shouting.

Neutrals keep the narrative readable and restrained. Light Silver or pure white lettering cuts cleanly through the night, while Slate Gray whispers secondary information. Bone, a soft off-white, appears rarely as daylight in marketing scenes, never inside core product flows. Every bright pixel has purpose; superfluous hues are rejected on sight.

Subtle states round out the system. A low-opacity amber halo breathes around focused objects, hinting at interactivity without adding new chroma. When data declares success, a measured stroke of Steel Teal suffices—present but never competing with the fire of Forge Orange.

The rules are simple and absolute: dark mode is the only mode; one dominant accent per view preserves hierarchy; red is reserved, never punitive; all text meets or exceeds WCAG contrast thresholds. Color is mission, not decoration—each tone chosen to sharpen attention, convey emotion, and leave nothing extraneous on the screen.
