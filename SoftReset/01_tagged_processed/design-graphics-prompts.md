## Graphics Engine Prompt Schema

Our graphics engine runs on a disciplined JSON blueprint that binds story, format, and brand into a single cinematic pulse. Each file opens with a terse, evocative storyline, then locks in a publishing format that dictates aspect ratio and framing nuances. A standing mantra—trauma-informed hero’s journey rendered in gritty Villeneuve realism, punctuated by Forge-Orange, Phoenix-Red, and Volt-Gold—fuses tone, palette, and narrative before the first pixel is dreamed.

Every image lives in its own object. Design modality sets the visual technique in film language, always beginning with “cinematic realism.” A lean scene directive follows, written in present tense with placeholders like {Protagonist} or {Golden_Child} so character updates ripple system-wide without rewrite. Colour palette text anchors two or three dominant hues and the required accent, preventing drift from signature colors. Atmosphere layers sensory grit—fine grain, humming neon, drifting dust—while a mood phrase crystalizes the emotional note in a few decisive words. Character visual references sharpen posture, expression, or props; aspect ratio seals the frame.

Vocabulary discipline is uncompromising. Cinematic descriptors stay; cartoon, anime, celebrity likeness, or off-brand terms never cross the prompt. Phrases that prove reliable—gritty Villeneuve realism, fine film grain, Forge-Orange glow—repeat intentionally to tutor the model toward a stable visual lexicon. After generation, each render is audited against the schema; any variance triggers prompt adjustment or subtle post-processing—crop, grade, or grain—to realign the final image with the directive.

This schema is our narrative assembly line, enabling many creators to produce cohesive, emotionally resonant visuals at scale. Every field serves a precise function, every word earns its place, and together they deliver a library of images that share one cinematic heartbeat across websites, carousels, blog covers, and reels.
