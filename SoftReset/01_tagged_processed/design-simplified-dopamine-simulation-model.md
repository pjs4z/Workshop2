## Simplified Dopamine Simulation Model

The model translates the brain’s complex reward dynamics into a daily accounting exercise: chemical stimulants and instant-gratification behaviours are treated as withdrawals, while sleep, exercise, meditation and similar practices are deposits. Each input is pre-assigned a small integer weight that reflects its acute effect on dopamine balance, so a day’s ledger collapses into three subtotals—substances, spikes and restoratives—and a single net figure. That figure is mapped to a five-point “dopamine fitness” rating whose extremes signal either a surplus of sustainable motivation or a deficit likely to produce craving, lethargy and mood volatility.

Because the weights are coarse and rule-based, the entire flow—data capture, arithmetic, score translation and even an hourly mood sketch that depicts brief highs followed by compensatory lows—can run on lightweight, no-code tooling. The outcome is a feedback instrument that lets users see, at a glance and without specialist knowledge, how daily choices compound into neurochemical resilience or exhaustion, and therefore guides them toward consistently higher baseline wellbeing.
