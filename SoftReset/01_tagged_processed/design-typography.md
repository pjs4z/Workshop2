## Typography System Overview

Our typographic system balances sovereign boldness with coach-like warmth by pairing two distinct families. Federo and Libre Baskerville deliver the heroic, editorial voice: Federo, set in commanding uppercase, leads major headlines and taglines, while Libre Baskerville sustains long-form narratives, captions, and refined italics that lend gravitas and credibility.

For conversational or instructional moments we shift to Solway and Lato. Solway’s rounded slab-serif shapes humanize secondary headings, typically in title or sentence case, while Lato carries the body text of interfaces, buttons, and explanatory copy with quiet clarity at even the smallest sizes.

Every composition begins by choosing a persona. Epic, aspirational screens unite Federo with Baskerville; practical, supportive screens rely on Solway with Lato. The headline fonts never appear together, nor do their body counterparts, except when clearly separated sections demand different tones. Hierarchy follows suit—H1 in Federo or Solway, H2 adjusting weight or family for contrast, tertiary headings often rendered in Lato Semi-Bold or Baskerville Bold Italic. Short, decisive calls to action may stay in Federo all caps, but longer labels revert to Lato for legibility. Quotes lean on Baskerville italics unless voiced by the coach, who speaks in Solway. Throughout, expressive type is reserved for moments that deserve the spotlight, while the supportive sans ensures effortless reading everywhere else.
