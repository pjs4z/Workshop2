## Reclaim Your Throne

A celebrated prodigy leaves university convinced of limitless promise yet quietly props his performance on stimulants that soon demand nightly sedation by alcohol. The substances erode his identity, career prospects, and relationships until he is isolated, unemployed, and consumed by shame. At his bleakest moment he contemplates ending his life, but a visionary encounter with a gruff, compassionate mentor figure reframes the crisis: the chemicals that once delivered quick euphoria are exposed as false prophets of dopamine, while the enduring symbol of his true purpose—a vacant throne—still stands, waiting to be earned.

Through this mythic intervention he is invited into “compassionate inquiry,” a discipline that faces pain without self-condemnation and converts it into deliberate agency. The mandate is not instant victory but the right to fight: one sober step after another sustained by honesty, community support, and self-respect. Awakening the next morning, he accepts the summons, reaches out for real help, and anchors his recovery to the simple decree “Reclaim your throne,” a daily reminder that his worth, potential, and responsibility remain intact and recoverable.
