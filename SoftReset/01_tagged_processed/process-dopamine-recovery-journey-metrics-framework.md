## Dopamine Recovery Metrics Framework

The framework defines a nine-month recovery path that tracks the cognitive, behavioral, and emotional evolution of each participant with only the most instructive metrics. Subjective check-ins on cravings, confidence, mood, and identity combine with objective signals such as sleep consistency and physiological calm to create a single Dopamine Resilience Score that is refreshed daily and summarized monthly. This composite indicator condenses complex inputs into one intuitive measure of progress, allowing participants and facilitators to see at a glance whether resilience is strengthening or slipping.

As the program advances, the weighting of component metrics shifts: early stages focus on extinguishing stimulant use and stabilizing basic habits; middle stages emphasize physiological restoration, self-efficacy, and routine adherence; later stages validate automaticity, stress-proof consistency, and the solidification of a non-addict identity. A digital coach reads the score in real time, offers tailored nudges when risk rises, and amplifies encouragement when milestones are reached, turning data into immediate behavioral guidance. Community engagement and mentorship gradually enter the score to reinforce social accountability and leadership.

By graduation the participant is expected to show months of abstinence or controlled use, reliable health and performance indicators, high self-reported sovereignty, and a written maintenance plan backed by a personal support network. The framework’s disciplined minimalism makes progress unmistakable, sustains motivation through visible gains, and leaves each graduate with both the evidence and the confidence that their new habits and identity can endure without external scaffolding.
