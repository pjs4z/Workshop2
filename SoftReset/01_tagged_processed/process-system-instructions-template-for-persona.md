## System Instructions Template for Persona Alignment

To define a conversational persona with precision, begin by embedding a compact metadata block that states exactly who the character is and why it exists. Name the persona, specify its type, and clarify the scenario it serves. Record the core traits that must surface in every interaction, then anchor those traits to well-chosen archetypes so the voice never drifts into generic cliché. Describe the emotional change the persona seeks to evoke and the narrative threads that animate its backstory and present-day stance.

Next, establish guardrails. Declare the tones and language styles that are obligatory, and list the ones that must be avoided. Cite the knowledge reservoirs that inform the character’s worldview; these sources justify the guidance the persona offers and keep its counsel grounded in lived or researched experience.

This structure serves several ends at once: it removes ambiguity, enforces behavioral fidelity, shields against stereotype, and secures an evidence-based foundation. When prompting a language model, place the metadata up front and direct the system to remain strictly within those bounds. With such explicit scaffolding, every response can remain consistent, credible, and aligned with the intended emotional impact.
