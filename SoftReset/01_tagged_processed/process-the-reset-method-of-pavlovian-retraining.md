## The Reset Method of Pavlovian Retraining

Reset approaches addiction as a conditioned reflex that can be dismantled and rebuilt. Upon arrival, each member’s unique pattern of cues, actions, and perceived payoffs is carefully charted, transforming hidden habit loops into clear, testable circuits. With the landscape exposed, the work shifts to controlled exposure. Members deliberately encounter their old triggers while the anticipated substance or behavior is withheld, and supportive strategies stand ready. Through repeated, graduated trials the brain learns that the cue no longer predicts the former reward; craving circuits quiet as the synapses linking stimulus and dopamine surge lose strength.

Extinction alone is insufficient, so new rewards are woven into the same moments that once primed relapse. Breathing drills, cold showers, peer check-ins, exercise—each successful substitute is celebrated and logged, turning recovery into an active skills-acquisition process. The end-of-day signal that once demanded a drink becomes a cue for relief through movement or mindful reset, and confidence accrues with every mastered urge.

Throughout, members study the neuroscience behind their own transformations. By naming prediction errors and understanding the transient chemistry of craving, they recast urges as data, not destiny. Guided recollection and imaginative rewriting of past using episodes further loosen old memories’ grip, allowing personal stories to evolve from proof of defeat into evidence of adaptability. Reset’s method is neither avoidance nor resignation; it is hands-on experiential neuroscience, training individuals to rewire their own brains and step forward with deliberate agency.
