## Dopamine State Variables

The model defines ten moment-to-moment state variables—energy, focus, positivity, tension, drive, overwhelm, autonomy, competence, connection, and clarity—each captured through a five-point self-report prompt and corroborated by passive behavioural or physiological cues. Together they offer a comprehensive yet non-overlapping snapshot of a knowledge worker’s physical arousal, attentional control, emotional tone, motivational force, perceived workload, psychological needs, social support, and goal understanding.  

For every variable an “if-too-low, if-too-high” playbook links real-time readings to brief, low-friction interventions: movement or stimulants to lift energy, mindfulness or brief breaks to dial down excess arousal, structured timers to recover focus, reframing or negotiation to restore autonomy, micro-planning to regain clarity, and so on. By design, the ten variables distill further into three core axes—energy, agency (autonomy plus competence), and focus—whose combined optimisation most strongly predicts sustained performance and well-being. Continuous measurement and responsive micro-adjustments are expected to keep each axis within its optimal band, translating raw effort into productive output while mitigating fatigue, anxiety, and disengagement.
