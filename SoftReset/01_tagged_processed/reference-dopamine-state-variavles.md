## Dopamine State Variables

The framework delineates ten momentary psychological states that together capture the full landscape of day-to-day work experience. Each state is anchored in an established academic construct and defined so that no dimension overlaps with another, creating a mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive map of energy, attention, affect, stress, motivation, cognitive load, and the classic needs for autonomy, competence, relatedness, and goal clarity.  

Every state can be surfaced through a single, plain-language prompt that invites an immediate self-rating, while parallel behavioral and physiological signals offer a silent corroborating channel. This dual-path measurement makes the model lightweight enough for frequent use yet rich enough for continuous sensing, ensuring that moment-to-moment shifts are both felt and detected.  

By unifying these elements, the approach furnishes teams and platforms with a real-time diagnostic of human capacity. Targeted nudges, workload adjustments, or social supports can then be deployed precisely where the data reveal friction, converting raw awareness into adaptive practices that sustain well-being and elevate performance.
