## Dopamine Variables Definition

The model maps a knowledge worker’s real-time experience onto ten distinct constructs—energy, focus, positivity, tension, drive, overwhelm, autonomy, competence, connection, and clarity—so the full terrain of arousal, attention, emotion, motivation, workload, agency, skill confidence, social support, and goal comprehension is continuously visible. Each construct is tracked through brief self-checks supplemented by behavioral and physiological cues, producing a concise dashboard whose dimensions are intentionally non-overlapping yet collectively exhaustive.

Every metric feeds a paired micro-intervention script designed to nudge the variable back toward its productive zone: arousal is raised or calmed, attention sharpened or relaxed, mood lifted, stress tempered, motivation rekindled, load resized, agency restored, mastery affirmed, relatedness refreshed, and goals clarified. The framework treats energy, agency (autonomy plus competence), and focus as the primary levers; their stability governs the efficacy of all others. By coupling granular measurement with rapid behavioral adjustments, the system creates a closed-loop engine that preserves well-being, prevents burnout, and steadily amplifies knowledge-work output while remaining flexible enough to merge or reprioritize dimensions as data dictate.
