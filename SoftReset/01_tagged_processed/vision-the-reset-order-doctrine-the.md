## The Reset Order Doctrine: The Origins of ADHD

ADHD emerges when early-life stress and inconsistent attachment interrupt the formation of neural systems that govern attention and impulse control. The infant brain, wired to read caregiver cues for survival, adapts to chronic tension or emotional unavailability by becoming hyper-vigilant and easily distracted; over time this coping style solidifies into the behaviour set labelled ADHD. Biology supplies a vulnerable template, yet the decisive factor is the relational environment: prolonged exposure to stress hormones and lack of attuned regulation dampen prefrontal and dopamine pathways, making scattered focus and impulsivity functional responses rather than innate defects.

The Reset Order converts this insight into practice by repairing the developmental injuries that fuel members’ stimulant reliance. Coaches offer stable routines, consistent responsiveness and guided mindfulness so adults can experience the attunement and predictability their nervous systems originally missed. As safety and self-understanding grow, hyperactivity and distractibility recede, allowing members to taper medication while cultivating intrinsic regulation skills and productive focus. The programme thereby replaces symptom management with lasting neural and relational recovery, enabling participants to carry forward healthier patterns in their families and communities.
