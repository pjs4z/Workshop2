# Abstinence Violation Effect

**Definition:** A cognitive-emotional reaction to a minor lapse wherein guilt, shame, and a perceived total loss of control precipitate a catastrophic relapse. It is fueled by a fatalistic belief in the binary disease model.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The **AVE** acts as a critical reinforcing feedback loop (The Marlatt Loop) that converts a mistake into a disaster.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Rigid adherence to [[Somatic Pathology]] or [[Dual Pathology]] which demands perfection.
- **Downstream (Drives):** A collapse in [[Self-Efficacy]] and immediate return to heavy use to escape the shame of the lapse.
- **Mitigation:** The **Marlatt Protocol** (Relapse Prevention) reframes lapses as learning data rather than moral failures.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Automatic):** The emotional crash following a slip triggers automatic coping mechanisms (more drugs).
- **Leverage Point:** Cognitive Re-Engineering of Failure—teaching patients to distinguish between a "slip" (error) and a "relapse" (collapse).
