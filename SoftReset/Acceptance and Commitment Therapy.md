# Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

**Definition:** A therapeutic framework that promotes **psychological flexibility** by teaching individuals to accept difficult feelings as part of a healthy emotional landscape, rather than attempting to eliminate them. Unlike [[Cognitive Behavioral Therapy]], which focuses on changing the content of thoughts, ACT changes the relationship to thoughts through acceptance and value-aligned action.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
ACT functions as a regulatory system for [[Dopaminergic Motivation]] by inhibiting the [[Discrepancy-Based Mode of Mind]]—the counterproductive habit of treating internal discomfort as a problem to be "solved."
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[The Observing Self]], [[Cognitive Defusion]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Value Identification]], [[Committed Action]]
- **Feedback Loop:** By accepting the "pole in the basement" (structural discomforts of life) rather than trying to remove it, the system reduces the secondary stress of struggle, allowing energy to be redirected toward [[Value Identification]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Builds the skill of "psychological flexibility"—the ability to stay in the present moment despite unpleasant sensations.
- **Motivation:** Re-orients motivation from "feeling good" (hedonism) to "living well" (meaning), leveraging the [[Will to Meaning]] described in [[Logotherapy]].
- **Leverage Point:** Shifts focus from "symptom reduction" to "valued living," making it highly effective for chronic dopamine-related issues where total elimination of urge is impossible.
