# Addiction Cycle

**Definition:** A composite, repeating three-stage process—comprising the [[Binge/Intoxication Stage]], [[Withdrawal/Negative Affect Stage]], and [[Preoccupation/Anticipation Stage]]—that interactively intensifies to drive the pathological state of substance dependence.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Addiction Cycle]] is composed of three interconnected sub-stages that feed into multiple reinforcing and balancing loops, such as the *Dark Side / Negative Reinforcement Spiral* and the *Incentive Sensitization Loop*. The cyclical nature ensures that relief from one stage inevitably triggers the onset of the next, driving structural [[Neuroplasticity]] over time. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Peer Pressure]] (catalyzes the initial [[Binge/Intoxication Stage]]), [[Preoccupation/Anticipation Stage]] (re-initiates the cycle).
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Neuroplasticity]] (via repeated substance exposure), [[Mesolimbic Dopamine System]] (acute activation followed by long-term downregulation).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of the COM-B model, the [[Addiction Cycle]] fundamentally shifts the locus of **Motivation** from positive reinforcement (impulsive reward-seeking) to [[Negative Reinforcement]] (compulsive relief-seeking). Breaking this cycle requires interrupting the transition between stages, mapping leverage points to specific behavioral phenomena like [[Compulsive Drug-Seeking and Taking]].
