# Addiction Process

**Definition:** A complex, relapsing neurobiological and psychological process characterized by compulsive engagement in a behavior or substance use despite negative consequences. It originates not as a moral failure, but as a coping mechanism for emotional pain and neurobiological impairment, often rooted in early developmental trauma or stress.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The addiction process functions as a self-reinforcing feedback loop involving biological, psychological, and social factors. It is an attempt to regulate internal states that the individual cannot manage autonomously due to impaired [[Self-Regulation]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Hungry Ghost]] (the inner void), [[Dislocation]] (social isolation), and [[Implicit Memory]] of early trauma.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Salience Attribution]] (overvaluing the substance), [[Brain Lock]] (compulsive behavior), and further [[Dislocation]] via stigma and the [[War on Drugs]].
- **Mechanism:** The process hijacks the brain's [[Dopamine]] (incentive-motivation) and [[Endorphins]] (attachment-reward) systems, altering the function of the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Acts as a powerful automatic motivator (Habit) to soothe emotional distress (painkilling) and generate a sense of vitality.
- **Capability:** Relies on the impairment of executive function ([[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]]), reducing the "Free Won't" or capacity to inhibit impulses.
- **Leverage:** Can be interrupted by [[Compassionate Curiosity]] and structural interventions like [[Harm Reduction]].
