# Addiction

**Definition:** An ingrained habitual response and source of gratification or security used to cope with internal feelings and external pressures, characterized by subjective benefits that are eventually outweighed by destructive costs to health, work, and relationships.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Addiction is the central variable in the **Cycle of Dependence** feedback loop. It functions as a short-term coping mechanism that ultimately degrades the user's environment, reinforcing the need for the behavior.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Low [[Self-Efficacy]], negative emotions (anxiety, pain), and the breakdown of [[Value System]]s.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreases [[Value System]] integrity and [[Self-Efficacy]]. It triggers [[Withdrawal]] when stopped and is the primary subject of the [[Disease Model of Addiction]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Learned Helplessness]] (lack of internal direction), [[Social Dependency Constellation]] (cultural pressure), and the [[Opponent-Process]] (fear of withdrawal).
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Withdrawal]] (fear of absence), [[Tolerance]] (need for more), and the creation of a [[Private World]] to validate the behavior.
- **Inhibited by:** [[Maturing Out]] and [[Controlled Use]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Often driven by the immediate "high" or relief from pain, overriding long-term values.
- **Leverage Points:** The [[Life Process Program]] targets Addiction by replacing the narrative of powerlessness with one of agency and skill acquisition.
