# Addiction

**Definition:** A pathological state in which the dopamine desire circuit is intensely stimulated and hijacked by a substance or behavior, severing the neurological link between wanting and liking to create an insatiable craving.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Addiction]] is a systemic failure of [[The Executive Control System]] and [[The Dopamine-H&N Homeostasis]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed by the [[Mesolimbic Pathway]] and artificial spikes in [[Reward Prediction Error]]. It is characterized by the "Addiction Hijack" reinforcing loop, where intense [[Dopamine]] surges bypass natural satiety limits.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Heavily inhibits [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]] and overpowers the [[Mesocortical Circuit]], causing the individual to lose the ability to engage in [[Mental Time Travel]] to forecast long-term negative consequences.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Addiction]] represents a profound deficit in psychological Capability, as the [[Mesolimbic Pathway]] overrides reflective thought. Leverage points require multi-faceted interventions: 
1. Modify Opportunity: Remove environmental cues that trigger artificial [[Reward Prediction Error]].
2. Modify Capability: Utilize Cognitive Behavioral Therapy to strengthen the [[Mesocortical Circuit]] and restore [[Mental Time Travel]].
3. Modify Motivation: Cultivate [[Affiliative Relationship]]s to boost [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]], filling the "relief from the low" deficit that drives the compulsive loop.
