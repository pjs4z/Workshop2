# Addictive Cycle

**Definition:** A self-perpetuating feedback loop where [[Addiction]] temporarily reduces negative feelings, but its destructive consequences degrade the individual's life (work, family), increasing the negative feelings that drive the addiction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This is a **Reinforcing Loop**.
- **Mechanisms:** Short-term relief $\to$ Long-term degradation $\to$ Increased need for relief.
- **Intervention:** The [[Life Process Program]] intervenes here by building [[Self-Efficacy]] and [[Coping Skills]] to handle the negative feelings without substances.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Driven by the desire to escape negative affect (Emotional Resilience).
- **Leverage Points:** Skill acquisition (stress management) breaks the link between stress and the addictive response.
