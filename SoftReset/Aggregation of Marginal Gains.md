# Aggregation of Marginal Gains

**Definition:** The strategy of searching for tiny margins of improvement (1 percent) in every aspect of a process. While individually imperceptible, these small improvements compound geometrically over time to produce massive results.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A focus on Systems rather than Goals.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Exponential growth in the [[Lagging Measure]] (outcomes).
- **Mathematics:** 1% better every day for a year = 37x better. 1% worse every day = near zero.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Lowers the barrier to entry. It is easier to improve a process by 1% than to achieve a massive breakthrough instantly.
- **Opportunity:** Applicable to any domain (Health, Business, Relationships).
- **Leverage Point:** Deconstruct a complex task into component parts and optimize each by a small fraction (as seen in the British Cycling team example).
