# Atomic Habit

**Definition:** A small, regular practice or routine that is not only easy to perform but serves as the fundamental unit of a larger system, compounding over time to produce remarkable results.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Atomic habits operate on the principle of compounding. Just as money multiplies through compound interest, the effects of habits multiply as they are repeated.
- **Stock:** The accumulation of small improvements leads to a massive transformation in the **Stock** of personal potential.
- **Delay:** Results are often delayed, leading to the [[Plateau of Latent Potential]] where progress seems invisible despite the effort (The Valley of Disappointment).
- **Feedback Loop:** A reinforcing loop where small wins build confidence (evidence for [[Identity]]), which encourages further repetition.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Implementation Intention]], [[Environment Design]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Identity]], [[Automaticity]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Relies on the **Psychological Capability** to focus on the system rather than the goal.
- **Motivation:** shifts focus from outcome-based (results) to identity-based (becoming the type of person who does X).
- **Leverage Point:** Reduce the barrier to entry using the [[Law of Least Effort]] to ensure the habit is small enough to be sustainable.
