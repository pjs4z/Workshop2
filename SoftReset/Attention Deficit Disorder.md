# Attention Deficit Disorder

**Definition:** A developmental impairment—rather than a genetic disease—characterized by poor attention skills, deficient impulse control, and hyperactivity. It originates from the interaction between an innate [[Sensitivity]] and environmental stresses (the [[Invisible Environment]]) during critical brain development, resulting in a defensive mechanism of [[Dissociation]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
ADD functions as a coping mechanism turned maladaptive trait. It is the result of inhibited [[Neural Darwinism]] in specific brain regions.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The collision of a highly reactive [[Sensitivity]] with a stressful [[Invisible Environment]] (lack of [[Attunement]]).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Chronic [[Dissociation]] (tuning out), [[Counterwill]] (resistance), and a reliance on [[Implicit Memory]] rather than conscious regulation.
- **Biological Basis:** Underdevelopment of the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex]] and irregularities in [[Dopamine]] reception.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Impaired by the automaticity of "tuning out" when emotional pain arises.
- **Motivation:** Driven by an automatic need to escape distress (tuning out) or seek stimulation (hyperactivity) to counteract [[Shame]].
- **Leverage Point:** Reframing ADD traits not as flaws, but as desperate, archaic defense mechanisms that protected the infant self.
