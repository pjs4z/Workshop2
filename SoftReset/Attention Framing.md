# Attention Framing

**Definition:** A cognitive filtering process that artificially bounds the decision space to elevate the salience of critical objectives while actively suppressing distracting alternatives. It serves as a countermeasure to decision fatigue and analysis paralysis.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Attention Framing functions as a "Filter" in the system, reducing the volume of inputs the [[Executive Function Deficit]] must process.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Necessitated by the overwhelming volume of "shallow work" requests.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Enables entry into [[Deep Work]] by creating a clear "signal" amidst noise.
- **Mechanisms:** Utilizes heuristics like the *Eisenhower Matrix*, *Rule of 3*, or the *OODA Loop* to force decisive selection over mathematical optimality.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Reduces the cognitive load required to start working. By treating attention as a scarce resource, framing prevents the depletion of willpower.
- **Opportunity:** Alters the "Choice Architecture" of the workday. Instead of a 20-item to-do list, the environment is structured to present only the "Vital Few."
- **Leverage Point:** Apply framing *before* the work session begins to prevent the brain from slipping into [[Meta-Optimization]].
