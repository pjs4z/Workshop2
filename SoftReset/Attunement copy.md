# Attunement

**Definition:** The process by which a parent or caregiver creates a resonant emotional bond with an infant, accurately reading and responding to the child's internal states. It is the essential environmental input required for the healthy development of the brain's [[Self-Regulation]] circuits.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Attunement acts as the architect of the developing brain.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The caregiver's own capacity for presence and lack of chronic stress.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Proper development of the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]], regulation of [[Dopamine]] and [[Endorphins]] receptors, and the capacity for [[Self-Regulation]].
- **Failure State:** Lack of attunement ("Proximal Separation") leads to chronic physiological stress, [[Epigenetics]] changes, and susceptibility to the [[Addiction Process]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** A lack of attunement in childhood results in a structural capability deficit in adulthood—specifically, the inability to self-soothe without external aids.
- **Opportunity:** Therapeutic environments (like [[Harm Reduction]] centers or therapy) aim to provide "re-attunement" to repair these deficits.
