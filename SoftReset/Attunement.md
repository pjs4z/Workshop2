# Attunement

**Definition:** A shared emotional state where the caregiver aligns with the infant's rhythms and feelings, creating a dialogue essential for the development of the brain's self-regulation circuitry. It is the primary vehicle for healthy [[Neural Darwinism]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Attunement is the "nutrient" for the developing emotional brain.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A secure [[Invisible Environment]] (parental calm and presence).
- **Downstream (Drives):** The release of [[Dopamine]] and endorphins, promoting the growth of the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex]].
- **Inhibition:** Stress or depression in the caregiver disrupts attunement, triggering the infant's [[Dissociation]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** The deep biological hunger for attunement drives much of the ADD adult's "relationship seeking" or "people pleasing."
- **Leverage Point:** **Active Attunement**—Using eye contact and safe connection with a therapist or partner to co-regulate the nervous system, repairing the "interpersonal bridge."
