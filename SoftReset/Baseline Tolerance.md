# Baseline Tolerance

**Definition:** The biological threshold of stimulation required to elicit a neurochemical response. This "set point" is dynamic: it shifts upward with overexposure (requiring more wind to feel the breeze) and downward with abstinence (making a breeze feel like a gale).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Baseline Tolerance is the stock variable in the **Tolerance Trap** (Reinforcing Loop):
1.  High stimulation leads to [[Homeostatic Downregulation]].
2.  Downregulation raises **Baseline Tolerance**.
3.  Higher tolerance makes moderate pleasures feel "empty," driving a pursuit of extreme [[Societal Superabundance]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Chronic [[Future-Oriented Anticipation]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Dissatisfaction with ordinary life and the compulsion for intensity.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** The goal of [[Dopamine Revitalization]] is not to stop enjoying life, but to lower this baseline so that "a piece of toast can be more satisfying."
- **Insight:** Recovery is the process of lowering the baseline. The initial pain of "fasting" is simply the gap between the current high baseline and the low stimulation level; this gap closes as [[Homeostatic Upregulation]] occurs.
