# Behavioral Change

**Definition:** The active process through which individuals alter their habits, actions, or psychological responses over time, heavily mediated by cognitive beliefs, planned attitudes, and external conditions.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Behavioral Change]] is an action-oriented flow initiated by high levels of [[Self-Efficacy]]. It does not occur in a vacuum; rather, it is deeply embedded as a temporal part of the overarching [[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]] system. The momentum of this process can be heavily inhibited by rigid or unfamiliar [[Situational Specificity]]. Successful modifications in behavior feed directly into the [[Contextual Adaptation Homeostasis]] and [[Efficacy-Behavior-Environment Reinforcement]] loops.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Self-Efficacy]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Targeting **Sustained Behavioral Change** requires a holistic alignment of Psychological **Capability** ([[Self-Efficacy]], [[Social Cognitive Neural Processing]]), Social/Physical **Opportunity** ([[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]], [[Situational Specificity]]), and Reflective **Motivation** ([[Self-Efficacy]], [[Intrinsic Motivation]]).
- **Leverage Point:** Design structured, graded mastery experiences and micro-habits to incrementally elevate [[Self-Efficacy]], thereby expanding the foundational psychological capability and intrinsic drive required to maintain [[Behavioral Change]] against environmental friction.
