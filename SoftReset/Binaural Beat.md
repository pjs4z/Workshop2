# Binaural Beat

**Definition:** An auditory illusion created by presenting two slightly different pure tone frequencies to each ear (e.g., 132 Hz and 78 Hz). The brain integrates the difference (54 Hz) as a perceived third "beat," which entrains [[Neural Oscillation]] to match that frequency.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** The frequency differential forces the brain to synchronize its electrical activity (brainwaves) to the "beat."
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Auditory input, [[Neural Oscillation]] physics.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Theta Waves]] (Creativity), [[Gamma Waves]] (Processing), [[Dopamine]] (indirectly via state change).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
A tool for **Opportunity** (Physical/Environmental).
- **Application:** Used to artificially induce states conducive to [[Divergent Thinking]].
- **Nuance:** Research suggests this is most effective for individuals with *low* baseline dopamine; for those with normal/high dopamine, it may actually decrease creative capacity due to over-stimulation.
