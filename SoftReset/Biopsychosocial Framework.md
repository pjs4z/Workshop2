# Biopsychosocial Framework

**Definition:** A trans-disciplinary meta-model asserting that addiction pathology emerges from the reciprocal interaction of biological substrates (genetics/neurobiology), psychological states (cognition/trauma), and social environmental contexts (dislocation/poverty).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The synthesis of the dialectic between [[Somatic Pathology]] (Thesis) and [[Psychosocial Dislocation]] (Antithesis).
- **Integrates:**
    - Bio: [[Neuroadaptation]] and [[Alcohol Dependence Syndrome]].
    - Psycho: [[Self-Efficacy]] and [[Abstinence Violation Effect]].
    - Social: [[Harm Reduction]] and environmental factors.
- **Mechanism:** [[Reciprocal Determinism]]—each domain influences the others continuously.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Leverage Point:** Holistic intervention. Treating biology (medication) without treating sociology (housing/connection) is destined to fail due to system feedback loops.
