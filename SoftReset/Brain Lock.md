# Brain Lock

**Definition:** A neurological state, observed in both OCD and addiction, where the switching mechanism between thought and action is stuck. This causes compulsive behaviors to follow obsessive thoughts automatically, bypassing the executive control of the cortex.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Brain Lock represents the failure of the "clutch" in the brain's transmission.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** High [[Salience Attribution]] and impaired [[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]] function.
- **Downstream (Drives):** The repetitive, unstoppable nature of the [[Addiction Process]].
- **Mechanism:** The impulse moves from the emotional brain to the motor centers without successful veto by the prefrontal cortex.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Reduces the addict's physical capability to stop an action once the impulse has arisen.
- **Leverage:** [[The Four Steps]] (Re-label, Re-attribute, Re-focus, Re-value) are designed specifically to manually override Brain Lock by engaging conscious attention.
