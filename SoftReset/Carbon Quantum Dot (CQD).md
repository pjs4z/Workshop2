# Carbon Quantum Dot (CQD)

**Definition:** Nanoscale carbon particles (comparable to a tennis ball on a scale of football fields relative to a human hair) capable of crossing the [[Blood-Brain Barrier]]. They possess unique quantum properties that allow them to fluoresce in proportion to the presence of specific neurotransmitters, such as [[Dopamine]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
CQDs represent a phase shift in diagnostic capability, moving from macro-biological proxies (like blood tests) to micro-molecular observation.
- **Upstream (Created by):** Advances in [[Nanotechnology]].
- **Downstream (Enables):** [[Molecular Diagnostics]], Real-time [[Dopamine Measurement]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Functions as a potential **Capability** enhancer for medical practitioners.
- **Current Limitation:** Traditional blood tests fail to measure [[Dopamine]] because it functions at the cellular level/synapse, not in the bloodstream. CQDs solve the "observer effect" problem by being small and stable enough to enter the brain without altering its function ("keeping to themselves").
