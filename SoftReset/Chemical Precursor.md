# Chemical Precursor

**Definition:** A compound (such as L-dopa or L-tyrosine) that the body metabolically converts into a target neurotransmitter (like dopamine). These are used therapeutically because they can often cross the [[Blood-Brain Barrier]], whereas the neurotransmitter itself cannot.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** The [[Blood-Brain Barrier]] blocks dopamine but admits L-dopa. Once inside the brain, enzymes convert the precursor into active dopamine.
- **Constraint:** "The Flour Bag Fallacy"—adding more precursor (flour) does not guarantee the brain will synthesize more dopamine (cake) if the metabolic machinery is working at capacity or is regulated by homeostasis.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** Dietary intake, Supplementation.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Dopamine synthesis (potentially), [[Neuro-enhancement]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability (Physical):** Precursors are the only viable physical mechanism for increasing raw dopamine stocks from outside the body.
- **Leverage Point:** Understanding the distinction between a *precursor* and the *neurotransmitter* itself prevents wasted effort on supplements that cannot cross the BBB.
