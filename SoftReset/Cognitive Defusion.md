# Cognitive Defusion

**Definition:** The psychological process of separating raw emotional stimuli or thoughts from immediate behavioral reactions. It involves seeing thoughts as "events in the mind" rather than literal truths or commands to act.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Cognitive Defusion breaks the "fusion" that typically occurs in a [[Discrepancy-Based Mode of Mind]], where a negative feeling immediately triggers a fix-it response.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Mindfulness]], [[The Observing Self]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Psychological Flexibility]], Reduced [[Dopaminergic Motivation]] (intensity)
- **Process:** It transforms "I am anxious" (fusion) to "I am noticing a feeling of anxiety" (defusion). This reduction in immediacy prevents the hijacking of the behavioral loop by the amygdala or dopamine reward system.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** A trainable mental skill that reduces the "stickiness" of [[Cue]] triggers.
- **Leverage Point:** Critical for interrupting addiction loops; allows the user to "surf" the urge rather than drown in it (see [[Urge Surfing]]).
