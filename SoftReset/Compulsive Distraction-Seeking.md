# Compulsive Distraction-Seeking

**Definition:** The behavioral manifestation of yielding to open systems and digital notifications, driven by overwhelmed cognitive bandwidth and a high biological tolerance for stimulation.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Compulsive Distraction-Seeking]] is the target behavior that traps individuals in [[The Procrastination Escape Cycle]]. It serves as an escape mechanism from the discomfort of a stimulation wavelength mismatch when faced with difficult tasks.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Overstimulation]], [[Stimulation Tolerance]], [[Scarcity Mindset]], and the presence of an [[Open System]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Exacerbates [[Procrastination]], triggers [[Dopamine Relapse]], and actively blocks the capacity for [[Executing Deep Work]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** The user's cognitive bandwidth is overwhelmed by [[Overstimulation]] and a high [[Stimulation Tolerance]], making deep focus impossible. The [[Scarcity Mindset]] further erodes capacity through false urgency (FOMO).
- **Opportunity:** Enabled by frictionless access to an [[Open System]] and the pervasive reality of [[Attention Hijacking]].
- **Motivation:** Driven by automatic [[Dopamine]] anticipation and the reflective avoidance of hard work ([[Procrastination]]).
- **Leverage Points:** To dismantle this behavior, one must architect [[Environmental Friction]] (e.g., device blocking) to limit Opportunity, and execute a [[Dopamine Detox]] to reset Capability. Cultivating [[Here-and-Now Neurotransmitters]] can also provide alternative Motivation.
