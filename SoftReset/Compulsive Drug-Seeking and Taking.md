# Compulsive Drug-Seeking and Taking

**Definition:** The target behavioral phenomenon where an individual automatically and compulsively seeks and consumes drugs, driven by neurobiological adaptations and deeply ingrained stimulus-response habits.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This behavior is the ultimate output of the [[Addiction Cycle]]. It represents the behavioral manifestation of the shift from impulsive action to compulsivity, deeply intertwined with the *Dark Side / Negative Reinforcement Spiral* and the *Habitual Automaticity Loop*.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Incentive Salience]], [[Negative Reinforcement]], [[Habitual Drug-Seeking]], and [[Opponent Process]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Restarts the [[Binge/Intoxication Stage]], perpetuating the cycle.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Severely degraded by [[Neuroplasticity]] (loss of executive function) and the downregulation of the [[Mesolimbic Dopamine System]] (anhedonia).
- **Opportunity:** Facilitated by [[Peer Pressure]] and the presence of drug-associated contextual cues that trigger the [[Preoccupation/Anticipation Stage]].
- **Motivation:** Driven by an inescapable combination of [[Incentive Salience]] (craving) and [[Negative Reinforcement]] (alleviating the distress generated by the [[Extended Amygdala]]).
- **Leverage Points:**
  1. *Memory Reconsolidation Disruption:* Use cue-exposure therapy coupled with beta-blockers or GABAergic stimulation to uncouple environmental cues (Opportunity) from [[Incentive Salience]] (Motivation).
  2. *Social/Environmental Restructuring:* Actively remove the individual from [[Peer Pressure]] and known triggers to eliminate the Opportunity loop.
