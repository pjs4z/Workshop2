# Conditioned Withdrawal

**Definition:** A physiological response where environmental cues (people, places, paraphernalia) or internal emotional states associated with prior drug use elicit actual withdrawal symptoms and craving in an abstinent individual via associative learning pathways.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A mechanical expression of [[Reciprocal Determinism]], linking environment to biology.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Repeated pairing of substance use with specific environments (classical conditioning).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Relapse even after physical detox is complete, often triggering the [[Abstinence Violation Effect]].
- **Mechanism:** The environment acts as a "Conditioned Stimulus" (CS) triggering a "Conditioned Response" (CR) of sickness.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity (Physical):** The physical environment itself becomes a trigger for biological incapacity.
- **Leverage Point:** Exposure therapy and changing the "Setting" (as per [[Harm Reduction]] principles) to break associative bonds.
