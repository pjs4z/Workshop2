# Controlled Usage

**Definition:** The regulation of consumption or engagement in an activity (such as drinking or eating) to moderate, non-problematic levels, demonstrating that loss of control is not inevitable.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A behavioral outcome of high [[Self-Efficacy]] and a strong [[Value System]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Self-Efficacy]], self-monitoring.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Reinforces the belief that [[Addiction]] is not an irreversible disease. inhibited by [[Set-Point Theory]] in the context of extreme weight loss.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Target Behavior:** Controlled Usage (Moderation).
- **Capability:** Requires self-monitoring and cognitive control.
- **Leverage Points:** Protocol design (e.g., specific rules for consumption) and data-driven feedback.
