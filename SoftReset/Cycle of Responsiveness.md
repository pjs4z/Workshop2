# Cycle of Responsiveness

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop in workplace culture where high expectations of availability create anxiety (**[[Internal Trigger]]**), leading employees to constantly check devices (**[[Distraction]]**), which reinforces the norm that immediate response is required.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This cycle is a symptom of dysfunction, specifically a lack of **[[Psychological Safety]]**. It transforms technology from a tool into a source of job strain.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Toxic workplace culture, lack of **[[Psychological Safety]]**.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Burnout, "Fake Work," overuse of **[[External Trigger]]**.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** Can only be broken by collective agreement (Schedule Syncing) and leadership modeling the right to disconnect.
- **Motivation:** Driven by fear (of missing out, of punishment).
