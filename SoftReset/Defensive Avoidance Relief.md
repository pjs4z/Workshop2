# Defensive Avoidance Relief

**Definition:** A self-sustaining feedback mechanism where the anticipation of threats generates high emotional arousal, triggering defensive or avoidant behavior. This behavior provides immediate, short-term relief from arousal, thereby powerfully reinforcing the avoidance while preventing long-term behavioral mastery.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop explains the intractability of phobic and avoidant behaviors. 
1. Anticipation of a threat spikes [[Emotional Arousal]].
2. High arousal provides physiological feedback of vulnerability, lowering [[Self-Efficacy]] and triggering [[Defensive Behavior]] (avoidance/escape).
3. Executing [[Defensive Behavior]] temporarily removes the threat, causing an immediate homeostatic decrease in [[Emotional Arousal]] (a balancing effect).
4. This relief strongly reinforces the defensive maneuver in the short term, but because it actively inhibits [[Coping Behavior]], it deprives the individual of the [[Performance Accomplishments]] needed to permanently alter their baseline [[Self-Efficacy]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Low [[Self-Efficacy]], high [[Emotional Arousal]], and negative [[Outcome Expectancy]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Chronic maintenance of [[Defensive Behavior]] and stagnation of skill acquisition.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
To break this maladaptive loop, one must target **Opportunity** and **Automatic Motivation**. The primary leverage point is to systematically remove immediate escape pathways (disrupting the opportunity for avoidance) while simultaneously providing guided [[Participant Modeling]]. This forces the individual to remain in the situation long enough to realize the threat is manageable, extinguishing the avoidance drive and altering their [[Outcome Expectancy]].
