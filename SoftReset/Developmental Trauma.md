# Developmental Trauma

**Definition:** The enduring neuroregulatory and physiological alterations resulting from Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) that function as a primary etiological driver for adult addictive behaviors, viewing addiction as self-medication for a dysregulated nervous system.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Forms the **Felitti Loop**: Trauma -> Biological Dysregulation -> Addiction -> Social Instability -> Retraumatization.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Abuse, neglect, and household dysfunction during critical developmental windows.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Somatic Pathology]] (obesity, addiction) and [[Psychosocial Dislocation]].
- **Shift:** Moves clinical inquiry from "What is wrong with you?" to "What happened to you?"

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability (Physical/Psychological):** Trauma reduces the baseline capacity for emotional regulation, making chemical regulation necessary for survival.
- **Leverage Point:** Trauma-Informed Care—treating the underlying nervous system injury rather than punishing the adaptive behavior (addiction).
