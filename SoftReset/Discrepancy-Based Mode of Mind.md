# Discrepancy-Based Mode of Mind

**Definition:** A cognitive paradigm, reinforced by technological culture, that views every discomfort, negative emotion, or internal "gap" as a problem requiring an immediate solution.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This mode creates a pathological feedback loop when applied to internal states. By treating natural pain or [[Dopaminergic Motivation]] (withdrawal/craving) as "errors," it generates secondary suffering (stress about the stress).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Technological ubiquity, Cultural intolerance for discomfort.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Avoidance behaviors, Anxiety, Suppression of [[Acceptance and Commitment Therapy]].
- **Inhibition:** It inhibits [[The Observing Self]] by demanding immediate closure/resolution rather than observation.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Driven by the desire for immediate relief (low dopamine tolerance).
- **Leverage Point:** Recognition of this mode is the first step in ACT; shifting from "solving" feelings to "having" feelings.
