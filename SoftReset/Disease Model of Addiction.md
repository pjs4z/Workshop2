# Disease Model of Addiction

**Definition:** A conceptual framework positing that [[Addiction]] is a biologically determined, irreversible, and progressive medical condition characterized by loss of control and requiring lifelong [[Abstinence]] and medical or spiritual treatment.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This model creates a reinforcing feedback loop known as **The Disease Model Trap**. By teaching that individuals have no control, it lowers [[Self-Efficacy]], which increases the likelihood of failure in self-regulation, thereby "proving" the disease concept.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Genetic Determinism]] and biological essentialism.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreased [[Self-Efficacy]]; promotes [[Abstinence]] as the only solution; stigmatizes [[Relapse]] as a catastrophic failure rather than a learning opportunity.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Reduces psychological capability by instilling a belief in inherent powerlessness.
- **Leverage Points:** Shifting from this model to the [[Life Process Program]] allows for cognitive reframing that boosts agency.
