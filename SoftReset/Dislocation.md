# Dislocation

**Definition:** The loss of psychological, social, and economic integration into family, community, and culture. It creates a profound sense of exclusion, isolation, and powerlessness that acts as a primary precursor to addiction in modern free-market societies.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Dislocation creates the vacuum that addiction attempts to fill.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Systemic poverty, trauma, and policies like the [[War on Drugs]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The [[Hungry Ghost]] state and the need for the [[Addiction Process]] as a substitute for genuine connection.
- **Feedback Loop:** Addiction leads to further dislocation (jail, stigma), which fuels the pain that requires more addiction to soothe.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** A physical and social environment defined by Dislocation deprives the individual of alternative sources of [[Endorphins]] (connection), making drugs the only available option.
- **Leverage:** [[Harm Reduction]] and community integration are attempts to reverse Dislocation.
