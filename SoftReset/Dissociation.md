# Dissociation

**Definition:** An automatic psychological defense mechanism that separates conscious awareness from overwhelming emotional pain or sensory stimuli. In [[Attention Deficit Disorder]], this manifests as chronic "tuning out" or absentmindedness, originally developed to survive a painful [[Invisible Environment]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Dissociation is a survival tool that becomes a hindrance.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** High [[Sensitivity]] encountering chronic stress or lack of [[Attunement]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inattention, memory gaps, and the underdevelopment of [[Self-Regulation]].
- **Feedback Loop:** **The Dissociation Defense Spiral**—Tuning out prevents the processing of reality, leading to more chaos, which requires more tuning out.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Acts as a barrier to learning and intimacy.
- **Leverage Point:** Recognizing "zoning out" not as laziness, but as an anxiety response. The intervention is safety and grounding, not forced concentration.
