# Diversion and Misuse Escalation

**Definition:** A reinforcing system loop where rising ADHD diagnoses increase stimulant availability, leading to greater diversion, which fuels nonmedical misuse and drives continued peer demand.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This reinforcing feedback loop drives the current epidemic of stimulant abuse among young adults.
- **Mechanism:** Rising diagnoses of [[Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)]] lead to a higher volume and availability of [[Prescription Stimulants]]. This surplus in the population increases opportunities for [[Medication Diversion]], which directly increases [[Nonmedical Stimulant Misuse]] among young adults. The normalization and perceived academic benefits of misuse drive continued demand, further encouraging [[Medication Diversion]] in a vicious cycle.
- **Interventions:** This loop is structurally inhibited by systemic balancing loops incorporating [[Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs]], [[Screening, Brief Intervention, and Referral to Treatment]], and [[Harm Reduction Intervention]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop thrives on the massive expansion of **Opportunity** (ubiquitous access to surplus pills) colliding with an environment of high **Motivation** (cultural/academic pressures to perform). Breaking the loop requires severing the node of [[Medication Diversion]] through regulatory friction, and mitigating [[Nonmedical Stimulant Misuse]] by targeting psychological **Capability** via treating [[Psychiatric Comorbidity]].
