# Dopamine Detox

**Definition:** A structured process of intentionally reducing exposure to highly stimulating activities to prevent overstimulation and restore the mind's baseline focus capability.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Dopamine Detox]] acts as the primary balancing mechanism (see: [[The Detox Homeostasis]]) against the modern attention economy. By leveraging a [[Closed System]] and introducing strict [[Environmental Friction]], it breaks the reinforcing loop of constant stimulation. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed by the implementation of a [[Closed System]] and the explicit desire to escape chronic [[Overstimulation]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreases [[Stimulation Tolerance]] and [[Overstimulation]]. Directly inhibits [[Procrastination]] by re-aligning the brain's baseline stimulation requirements with the realities of hard work. 

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
A [[Dopamine Detox]] serves as a macro-level intervention to reset **Capability**. It restores the physiological and cognitive capacity needed for [[Executing Deep Work]]. It requires high **Opportunity** control (blocking all access to an [[Open System]]) and aims to shift **Motivation** away from frantic [[Dopamine]] anticipation toward calm, goal-oriented action supported by [[Here-and-Now Neurotransmitters]].
