# Dopamine Revitalization

**Definition:** A behavioral strategy involving the total elimination of specific high-stimulation habits (digital, dietary, etc.) coupled with the active engagement in [[Here and Now (H&N) System]] sensory experiences. Unlike "fasting"—which implies simple deprivation—revitalization aims to restore neurochemical sensitivity through [[Homeostatic Upregulation]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Dopamine Revitalization functions as an intervention in the biological feedback loop of addiction. By removing the stimuli provided by [[Societal Superabundance]], the brain is forced to stop "closing windows" (receptors) and begin reopening them.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A conscious rejection of [[Future-Oriented Anticipation]] and the recognition of the [[Tolerance Trap]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes [[Homeostatic Upregulation]], lowers [[Baseline Tolerance]], and strengthens the [[Here and Now (H&N) System]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Relies on **Differentiation**—the ability to distinguish between the urge for *more* (Dopamine) and the experience of *pleasure* (H&N).
- **Motivation:** Leverages the **All-or-Nothing Rule**. Since moderation keeps the craving alive, total abstinence (Binary Elimination) is easier than incremental reduction because it eventually extinguishes the automatic drive.
- **Leverage Point:** **The H&N Substitution Strategy**. You cannot just remove dopamine; you must replace it with present-moment savoring (e.g., social connection, nature) to prevent the failure mode of "feeling nothing."
