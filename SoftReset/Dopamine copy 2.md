# Dopamine

**Definition:** A neurotransmitter governing the brain's incentive-motivation circuitry (Ventral Tegmental Area to Nucleus Accumbens). It is essential for survival drives (food, sex) but is hijacked by addictive substances to create artificial, intense reward signals that trigger craving and seeking behaviors.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Dopamine creates the "wanting" (as opposed to the "liking" of [[Endorphins]]).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Stimulants (cocaine, crystal meth), novelty, and anticipation. Regulated by early [[Attunement]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Salience Attribution]] (making the drug feel like a survival need) and [[Brain Lock]].
- **Feedback Loop:** Chronic overstimulation leads to receptor downregulation (tolerance), increasing the [[Hungry Ghost]] void and necessitating higher doses.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Acts as the automatic motivation engine. High dopamine release reinforces the [[Addiction Process]] via operant conditioning.
- **Leverage:** Pharmacological stabilization (e.g., methadone) attempts to normalize this system to break the cycle of frantic seeking.
