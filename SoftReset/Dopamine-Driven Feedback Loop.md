# Dopamine-Driven Feedback Loop

**Definition:** A neurological cycle where the anticipation of a reward (not the reward itself) causes a spike in dopamine, creating the motivation (craving) to act.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** The brain allocates more neural circuitry to "wanting" (craving) than "liking" (pleasure).
- **Spike:** Dopamine spikes when a [[Cue]] is recognized, predicting a reward. This drives the **Craving** phase of [[The Habit Loop]].
- **Relationship:** [[Variable Reward]] schedules keep dopamine levels high, preventing boredom.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Supernormal Stimulus]], [[Prediction]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Action/Response

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Automatic):** This is the biological engine of motivation.
- **Leverage Point:** Use [[Temptation Bundling]] to artificially spike dopamine for a difficult habit by pairing it with a high-reward activity.
