# Dopamine

**Definition:** A neurotransmitter that motivates the pursuit of future possibilities, rewards, and control over the extrapersonal environment, driving behaviors ranging from creativity to addiction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Dopamine]] is the biological engine of the [[Extrapersonal Space]], driving humans to seek out resources that are distant in time and space. It exists in a mutually inhibitory relationship with [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]], forming the core regulatory loop known as [[The Dopamine-H&N Homeostasis]]. 

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Reward Prediction Error]] (surges when actual rewards exceed expected rewards).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes orientation toward the [[Extrapersonal Space]], [[Passionate Love]], and [[Low Latent Inhibition]]. It operates through two distinct pathways: the [[Mesolimbic Pathway]] (which drives raw desire and [[Addiction]]) and the [[Mesocortical Circuit]] (which drives calculation, planning, and [[Agentic Relationship]]s). [[Dopamine]] is eventually inhibited by the achievement of [[Mastery]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Dopamine]] provides the intense automatic and reflective Motivation for goal-directed behaviors, ranging from [[Addiction]] to long-term achievement. Because its drive wanes as familiarity sets in, it can be engineered for positive outcomes. A key leverage point is to gamify long-term tasks by introducing unpredictable milestones; this manufactures organic [[Reward Prediction Error]]s to sustain [[Dopamine]] without relying on sheer willpower or the [[Mesocortical Circuit]] alone.
