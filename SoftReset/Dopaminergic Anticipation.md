# Dopaminergic Anticipation

**Definition:** The psychological process whereby focus is projected entirely into the future via desire. It is the specific feeling of "wanting" that is distinct from, and often stronger than, the experience of "liking" or "having."

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This phenomenon is the active phase of [[Dopamine]] release. It creates a tension that seeks resolution through acquisition.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Dopamine]], [[Evolutionary Mismatch]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Acquisition behaviors, eventually leading to [[Hedonic Adaptation]].
- **Regulation:** Can be suppressed or redirected via [[Executive Modulation]].
- **Feedback Loop:** Part of the **Hedonic Treadmill**—anticipation leads to action, which leads to boredom, which leads to new anticipation.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** The primary barrier to "being present." It devalues the current state in favor of a hypothetical future.
- **Leverage Point:** Recognition of the "Glory of Maybe" allows an individual to pause and apply [[Virtue Ethics]] before acting on the impulse.
