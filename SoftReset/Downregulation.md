# Downregulation

**Definition:** The physiological process wherein the brain reduces the number or sensitivity of [[Dopamine]] receptors in response to chronic overstimulation. This leads to tolerance (needing more to feel less) and diminished capacity for pleasure (anhedonia).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This is a balancing feedback loop designed to protect the brain from over-excitement.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Chronic overstimulation of the [[Desire Circuit]] (e.g., drugs, porn, sugar).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Tolerance, Cravings (without pleasure), Addiction, Depression.
- **Reversal:** Reversed by [[Dopamine Revitalization]] (abstinence/fasting), which allows upregulation (receptor recovery).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Reduces the biological capability to experience pleasure from normal stimuli.
- **Leverage Points:** The only effective intervention is the removal of the super-normal stimulus for a sustained period to allow the brain to re-calibrate (Upregulation).
