# Environment Design

**Definition:** The practice of shaping physical spaces to make cues for good habits obvious and cues for bad habits invisible.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** "Behavior is a function of the Person in their Environment" (B = f(P,E)). Visual cues are the strongest catalyst for [[The Habit Loop]].
- **Flow:** Reduces **Friction** for desired behaviors (making them compliant with [[Law of Least Effort]]) and increases friction for undesired ones.
- **Relationship:** Context becomes the cue; habits are associated with locations/objects, not just time.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Commitment Device]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Automaticity]], [[Atomic Habit]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity (Physical):** The most powerful lever for behavior change because it persists without willpower.
- **Leverage Point:** "Prime the environment" for future use (e.g., setting out gym clothes).
