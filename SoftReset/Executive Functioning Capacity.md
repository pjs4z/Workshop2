# Executive Functioning Capacity

**Definition:** The cognitive capability governing self-restraint, emotional sensitivity, and self-regulation, the limitation or deficiency of which can lead to academic struggles and elevate the risk of substance use disorders.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Executive Functioning Capacity]] serves as the primary cognitive defense mechanism against substance abuse. In the Therapeutic Homeostasis Pathway, medications are introduced specifically to balance deficits in this capacity.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increased by [[Monoamine Neurotransmission Upregulation]] (which is catalyzed by [[Prescription Stimulants]]); Decreased by [[Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)]] and [[Psychiatric Comorbidity]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[Nonmedical Stimulant Misuse]] and [[Medication Diversion]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Executive Functioning Capacity]] is the core **Capability** driver in preventing substance use disorders. When an individual lacks this capacity, they experience an impaired psychological ability to resist impulsive behaviors, worsening their long-term decision-making. Restoring this capability—through appropriate therapeutic use of [[Prescription Stimulants]] to achieve [[Monoamine Neurotransmission Upregulation]], or by treating underlying [[Psychiatric Comorbidity]]—strengthens the individual's resilience against the reflective motivation to self-medicate or divert medications.
