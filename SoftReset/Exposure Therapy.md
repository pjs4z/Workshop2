# Exposure Therapy

**Definition:** A clinical intervention that involves systematically confronting feared stimuli or cravings ([[Cue]]) without performing the habitual avoidance or compulsive response, aiming to reset the body's fight-or-flight baseline.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Operates via the mechanism of **Habituation**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Committed Action]], [[The Observing Self]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Extinction of the Conditioned Response, Reduced [[Dopaminergic Motivation]].
- **Variations:**
    - **Flooding:** Immediate, intense exposure (e.g., crossing the longest bridge).
    - **Gradual Exposure:** Step-by-step increase in intensity.
    - **Urge Surfing:** Riding out an addictive craving until it naturally subsides.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Requires the physical and mental capacity to endure high physiological arousal.
- **Opportunity:** Often requires a "safe container" or therapeutic setting to prevent actual harm during the exposure.
- **Leverage Point:** Breaks the link between the **Cue** and the **Action**, proving to the brain that the "threat" (or the inability to satisfy a craving) is survivable.
