# Goldilocks Rule

**Definition:** The principle that humans experience peak motivation when working on tasks that are right on the edge of their current abilities (not too hard, not too easy).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** Tasks that are roughly 4% beyond current ability induce **Flow**.
- **Threat:** The greatest threat to success is boredom, which occurs when a habit becomes too easy (high [[Automaticity]]).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Need for novelty and mastery.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Variable Reward]], [[Deliberate Practice]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Sustains motivation after the initial excitement fades.
- **Leverage Point:** Once a habit is established, you must incrementally increase the difficulty to stay in the "Goldilocks Zone."
