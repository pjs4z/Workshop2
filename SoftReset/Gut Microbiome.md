# Gut Microbiome

**Definition:** An ecosystem of microorganisms residing in the gastrointestinal tract that influences brain function and motivation through the production of psychoactive metabolites (fatty acid amines) and direct nerve signaling.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The primary component of the **Microbiome-Motivation Cycle**.
- **Key Agents:** Strains *Eubacterium rectale* and *Coprococcus eutactus*.
- **Mechanism:** These bacteria produce fatty acid amines during exercise -> stimulates the brain -> increases dopamine -> boosts motivation to exercise.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Prebiotics (fiber, chia seeds), Fermented foods, Probiotics.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Sustainable Motivation without [[Dopamine Downregulation]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity (Physical):** The most accessible "hack" for healthy dopamine regulation. Unlike [[Reuptake Inhibition]] drugs, this system does not trigger the "Homeostatic Brake."
- **Leverage Point:** **Microbial Environmental Engineering.** Shift from the medicine cabinet to the pantry. Ingesting fiber acts as "fertilizer" for the specific bacteria that manufacture motivation.
