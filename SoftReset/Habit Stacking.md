# Habit Stacking

**Definition:** A strategy that pairs a new habit with a current habit (e.g., "After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]"), leveraging the neural connectedness of existing behaviors.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** Utilizes the synaptic strength of an established **Cue** to trigger a new **Response**.
- **Feedback:** Can lead to a positive **Diderot Effect**, where one good behavior naturally cascades into the next.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Implementation Intention]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[The Habit Loop]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** Uses the existing routine as a reliable prompt.
- **Leverage Point:** Highly effective when combined with [[Temptation Bundling]] (stacking a need after a want, or vice versa).
