# Habit Tracking

**Definition:** The practice of visually recording the execution of a habit (e.g., crossing off days on a calendar) to provide immediate evidence of progress.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** Makes the habit **Obvious** (visual cue), **Attractive** (progress is motivating), and **Satisfying** (crossing it off feels good).
- **Rule:** "Never Miss Twice." If you break the chain, restart immediately to prevent the formation of a new (bad) habit.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[The Habit Loop]] (specifically the Reward phase).
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Identity]], Motivation during [[Plateau of Latent Potential]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Provides immediate **Reinforcement** in a delayed-return environment.
- **Leverage Point:** Automate tracking where possible to reduce friction.
