# Harm Reduction

**Definition:** A public health philosophy and policy approach focused on mitigating the negative consequences of drug use (e.g., disease, overdose, stigma, violence) rather than demanding immediate abstinence. It accepts the reality of the [[Addiction Process]] and prioritizes the preservation of life and dignity.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Harm reduction acts as an "Island of Relief."
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Compassion and scientific evidence regarding the failure of the [[War on Drugs]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Reduced [[Dislocation]], lower stress/cortisol levels for addicts, and increased opportunity for connection and eventual recovery.
- **Examples:** Supervised injection sites (Insite), methadone maintenance, needle exchanges.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** Changes the physical and social environment to remove barriers to health (like fear of arrest or infection), creating a space where [[Self-Regulation]] can potentially be rebuilt.
