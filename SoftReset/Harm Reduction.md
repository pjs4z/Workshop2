# Harm Reduction

**Definition:** A pragmatic clinical and social strategy focused on mitigating the negative consequences of drug use (legal, medical, social) without requiring abstinence as a prerequisite for care. It accepts [[Reciprocal Determinism]] and prioritizes survival over ideology.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Acts as a stabilizing loop against the "War on Drugs" and [[Psychosocial Dislocation]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The failure of the penal/abstinence models (HIV/AIDS crisis).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increased [[Self-Efficacy]] and trust in the medical system, often serving as a bridge to the [[Biopsychosocial Framework]].
- **Key Concept:** "Set and Setting"—acknowledging that the environment influences the drug effect more than the chemical alone.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity (Physical/Social):** Provides safe environments (needle exchanges) that reduce lethality.
- **Leverage Point:** Meeting the user "where they are," reducing the shame that drives the [[Abstinence Violation Effect]].
