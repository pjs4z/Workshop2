# Hedonic Adaptation

**Definition:** The rapid recalibration of emotional baselines where familiarity with a stimulus leads to a cessation of dopaminergic response. It manifests as boredom or "the letdown" immediately following the acquisition of a desired object or goal.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This is the reset mechanism of the dopamine system, ensuring that no single achievement provides permanent satisfaction, thus keeping the organism hunting for resources.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Successful acquisition following [[Dopaminergic Anticipation]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Nihilism]] (if misunderstood as a failure of life) or renewed [[Dopaminergic Anticipation]].
- **Mitigation:** Can be countered by shifting focus to [[Eudaimonic Fulfillment]] and the "Here and Now" neurotransmitters (Serotonin/Oxytocin).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Negative Driver):** The discomfort of boredom acts as a prod to seek new stimuli.
- **Leverage Point:** Reframing boredom not as a signal to consume, but as a signal to engage in high-effort, skill-based activities ([[Virtue Ethics]]).
