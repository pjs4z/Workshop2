# Here and Now Neurotransmitters

**Definition:** A collection of present-oriented neurotransmitters, including serotonin, oxytocin, endorphins, and endocannabinoids, that mediate the experience of sensory pleasure, emotion, and satisfaction in the immediate present.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]] ground human experience in physical reality, serving as the neurological counterbalance to [[Dopamine]]. This opposing dynamic is described by [[The Dopamine-H&N Homeostasis]].

- **Upstream (Driven by):** The depletion of [[Reward Prediction Error]] (which reduces [[Dopamine]] suppression) and the achievement of [[Mastery]] (which triggers satisfaction).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes interaction with the [[Peripersonal Space]], the transition into [[Companionate Love]], and the formation of deep [[Affiliative Relationship]]s. By naturally inhibiting [[Dopamine]], these chemicals quiet the drive for "more" and facilitate present-moment contentment.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]] are critical for sustaining long-term behavioral change by providing reflective Motivation and psychological Capability. In the context of [[Addiction]] recovery, cultivating [[Affiliative Relationship]]s (e.g., support groups) boosts [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]] to naturally inhibit [[Dopamine]] cravings. Additionally, engineering routines that prioritize sensory-focused "microbreaks" (e.g., exposure to nature, woodworking) intentionally spikes these chemicals, resetting psychological balance and preventing dopaminergic burnout.
