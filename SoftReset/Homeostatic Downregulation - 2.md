# Homeostatic Downregulation

**Definition:** A biological protection mechanism where the [[Internal Dopamine Factory]] reduces the number of active receptors in response to frequent, high-intensity stimulation. This is the brain's attempt to maintain equilibrium (homeostasis) amidst a storm of neurotransmitters.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This process is the core mechanic of the [[Tolerance Trap]]. When the brain detects a "windstorm" of dopamine, it "closes the windows" to protect itself.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** High-frequency triggers from [[Societal Superabundance]] and [[Future-Oriented Anticipation]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Baseline Tolerance]] (requiring more stimulus for the same effect) and inhibits the [[Here and Now (H&N) System]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Understanding this biological reality helps reframe "boredom" or "withdrawal" not as a personal failure, but as the physical sensation of a system that has been stripped of its receptors.
- **Opportunity:** Modern environments (cheap calories, infinite scrolling) are designed to trigger this downregulation automatically, bypassing conscious consent.
