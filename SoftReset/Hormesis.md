# Hormesis

**Definition:** The biological phenomenon where small to moderate doses of noxious or painful stimuli (like cold water immersion or intense exercise) stimulate the body's adaptive healing response and upregulate dopamine.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Hormesis essentially hacks the [[Pleasure-Pain Balance]] by pressing on the **pain** side first.
*   **The Inverse Mechanism:** Unlike drugs (which tip to pleasure -> resultant pain), Hormesis tips the scale to pain. The brain's homeostatic response is to release endogenous [[Dopamine]], Serotonin, and Norepinephrine to restore balance.
*   **Result:** A sustained, natural elevation of mood ("The Natural High") rather than a crash.
*   **Neurogenesis:** Extreme cold and exercise can promote the growth of new neurons.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Pain-Seeking Behavior]], [[Exercise]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Dopamine]], [[Resilience]], [[Homeostasis]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
*   **Caveat:** One can become addicted to pain (e.g., overtraining, cutting). The dose must be moderate to be hormetic; too much pain leads to toxicity or a new [[Dopamine Deficit State]].
