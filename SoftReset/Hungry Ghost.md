# Hungry Ghost

**Definition:** A Buddhist metaphor describing a psychological state characterized by an insatiable inner void and a desperate, perpetual foraging for external relief or fulfillment. It represents the spiritual and emotional core of the [[Addiction Process]], where the individual seeks to fill a sense of deficiency with substances, objects, or behaviors.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The Hungry Ghost is the experiential manifestation of developmental deficits.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Lack of early [[Attunement]], [[Dislocation]], and [[Implicit Memory]] of abandonment or distress.
- **Downstream (Drives):** The compulsive seeking of [[Dopamine]] hits and [[Endorphins]] through the [[Addiction Process]].
- **Interaction:** It creates a state of chronic stress that keeps the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]] offline, preventing rational assessment of consequences.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Provides the deep, reflective (and often unconscious) drive to escape the present moment, which is experienced as painful or empty.
- **Leverage:** Can be addressed through [[Compassionate Curiosity]] and reconnecting with authentic values (Sobriety vs. Abstinence).
