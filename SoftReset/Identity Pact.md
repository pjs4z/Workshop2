# Identity Pact

**Definition:** A type of **[[Precommitment]]** involving a change in self-image or moniker (e.g., "I am Indistractable") to influence future behavior. It leverages the psychological tendency for humans to act in ways consistent with their beliefs about themselves.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Unlike **Effort Pacts** (which add friction) or **Price Pacts** (which add penalty), an **Identity Pact** alters the internal decision matrix by changing the default answer from "I can't" (deprivation) to "I don't" (empowerment).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Rituals, mantras, teaching others.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Consistent **[[Traction]]**, resilience against **[[Internal Trigger]]**.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Shifts motivation from extrinsic/willpower-based to intrinsic/identity-based.
- **Leverage Point:** Teaching the concept to others creates the strongest Identity Pact (the "protégé effect").
