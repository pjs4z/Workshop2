# Identity-Based Habits

**Definition:** A behavior change strategy that focuses on changing *who* you are (beliefs and self-image) rather than *what* you want to achieve (outcomes). It posits that behavior is a reflection of identity, and lasting change only occurs when the identity itself is upgraded.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This operates as a reinforcing feedback loop between behavior and belief.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The accumulation of evidence provided by performing an [[Atomic Habit]]. Every action is a "vote" for the type of person you wish to become.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Intrinsic motivation. Once a habit becomes part of your [[Identity]] (e.g., "I am a runner"), the friction to perform the habit disappears.
- **Contrast:** distinct from **Outcome-Based Habits**, which focus on a [[Lagging Measure]] (goals) and often fail once the goal is reached.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Reflective):** Shifts motivation from external rewards to internal alignment. It transforms "I have to do this" into "This is what someone like me does."
- **Leverage Point:** "Fake it 'til you make it" works by consciously casting votes for a new identity until the evidence outweighs the old self-image.
