# Identity

**Definition:** The emergent set of beliefs and self-image that acts as the deepest layer of behavior change. It is not fixed but formed and reinforced through the evidence of one's actions (habits).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Identity and habits form a bidirectional **Reinforcing Feedback Loop**. Your habits shape your identity, and your identity shapes your habits.
- **Mechanism:** Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you wish to become.
- **Conflict:** Behavior that is incongruent with the self will not last. True behavior change is identity change.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Atomic Habit]], [[Social Norms]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Prediction]], [[The Habit Loop]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Reflective):** Shifting from "I want to run a marathon" (Outcome) to "I am a runner" (Identity) utilizes **Reflective Motivation**.
- **Leverage Point:** "Fake it till you make it" works by accumulating small wins ([[Atomic Habit]]) that serve as proof to update the self-image.
