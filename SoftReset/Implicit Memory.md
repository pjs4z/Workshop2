# Implicit Memory

**Definition:** A memory system that encodes the emotional content and somatic sensations of early experiences without conscious recall. In ADD, it drives adult reactions to intimacy and authority, often triggering [[Shame]] or [[Counterwill]] based on past dynamics rather than present reality.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Early experiences recorded in the body/brain stem before the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex]] was fully formed.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Sudden mood shifts, "flashbacks" of feeling small/helpless, and the compulsion to avoid [[Attunement]] (fear of rejection).
- **Mechanism:** It bypasses the explicit memory (recall) centers, causing the person to *feel* they are reacting to the present, when they are reliving the past.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Implicit memories of abandonment sabotage motivation by triggering anxiety during tasks or relationships.
- **Leverage Point:** Separating past from present—identifying a sudden drop in mood as a "memory" rather than a current truth.
