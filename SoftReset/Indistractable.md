# Indistractable

**Definition:** A character trait and state of being wherein an individual strives to do exactly what they say they will do. It is not merely the absence of distraction, but the presence of integrity and conscious control over one's attention and intent.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Being "indistractable" acts as a stabilizing stock in the system of personal productivity. It is achieved by mastering the causal chain of behavior: managing the **[[Internal Trigger]]** that prompts action, ensuring that action leads to **[[Traction]]** rather than **[[Distraction]]**, and fortifying these behaviors against the **[[External Trigger]]**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** **[[Identity Pact]]**, **[[Psychological Safety]]** (in workplaces).
- **Downstream (Drives):** **[[Traction]]**, **[[Timeboxing]]**, Deep Work.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Functions as a powerful **[[Identity Pact]]**. By labeling oneself as "indistractable," one creates cognitive dissonance when acting out of alignment with that self-image.
- **Capability:** Requires the skill of "surfing the urge" to manage discomfort.
