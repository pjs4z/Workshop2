# Inference Substrate

**Definition:** A persistent macroscopic objective maintained as an ambient background context within the attention frame. It serves as a navigational anchor against which localized micro-actions are continuously verified for accuracy during tactical execution.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The **Inference Substrate** acts as the "North Star" residing in working memory. Its presence allows the brain to calculate "inference accuracy"—the verification that a specific sub-task actually serves the global mission.

*   **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Vesting Period of Salience]] (Requires sufficient neurochemical gravity to remain in the attention frame).
*   **Downstream (Drives):** Prevention of [[Tangential Unspooling]]; Maintenance of [[Involuntary Neurological Decoupling]] (prevents the separation of thought and action).
*   **Failure Mode:** When the transition from planning to doing occurs, this context often undergoes [[Inference Evaporation]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
*   **Capability (Psychological):** Acts as the cognitive bridge between "Meta-Appraisal" (planning) and "Tactical Execution" (doing). Without it, the individual becomes structurally blind to mission drift.
*   **Opportunity Leverage:** When internal generation fails, an **Externalized Inference Substrate** (e.g., [[Forced Superposition]] via biological necessity or physical signage) must be engineered to replace the internal deficit.
