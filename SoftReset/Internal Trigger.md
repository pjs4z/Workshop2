# Internal Trigger

**Definition:** An uncomfortable emotional or somatic state (e.g., boredom, anxiety, loneliness, fatigue) that prompts a behavioral response intended to escape that discomfort. It is the root cause of all motivation and distraction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The **Internal Trigger** initiates the feedback loop of behavior. If the individual lacks coping mechanisms, the trigger leads to **[[Distraction]]** (e.g., checking phones to soothe anxiety). If managed, it can be channeled into **[[Traction]]**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Homeostatic imbalance, **[[Cycle of Responsiveness]]** (stress), negative affect.
- **Downstream (Drives):** **[[Distraction]]** (default), **[[Traction]]** (if reimagined).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** The primary leverage point is "Reimagining the Trigger." Techniques include the "10-minute rule" and "surfing the urge" (observing the sensation without acting on it).
- **Motivation:** "Time management is pain management."
