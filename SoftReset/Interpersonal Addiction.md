# Interpersonal Addiction

**Definition:** A sterile, ingrown dependency relationship where another person is utilized as an external source of security to compensate for a lack of personal integrity. It results in a contraction of the individual's world and is structurally identical to drug addiction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This phenomenon creates the [[The Égoisme à Deux Spiral]], a reinforcing feedback loop where mutual isolation increases dependence.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Social Dependency Constellation]] (middle-class upbringing) and [[Learned Helplessness]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The construction of a [[Private World]] that excludes external reality; suppression of individual growth; eventual volatile [[Withdrawal]] upon separation.
- **Opposite of:** [[Genuine Love]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Driven by "External Validation"—the need for another to define the self.
- **Opportunity:** Thrives in the [[Nuclear Family Isolation]] or environments lacking broad social networks.
- **Leverage Points:** Disrupt the [[Private World]] by enforcing time apart; diversify emotional investments across a network (friends, community).
