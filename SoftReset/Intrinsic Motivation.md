# Intrinsic Motivation

**Definition:** An enduring psychological drive to engage in behaviors for their inherent satisfaction and the fulfillment of foundational self-determination needs, rather than for external reinforcement.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Intrinsic Motivation]] functions as a deep psychological stock that catalyzes active, effortful processes like [[Self-Regulated Learning]]. In turn, the successful execution of these learning processes generates competence and autonomy feedback, which loops back to further increase the stock of [[Intrinsic Motivation]]. This bidirectional relationship forms the core of the [[Intrinsic Regulation Spiral]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Self-Regulated Learning]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Self-Regulated Learning]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In behavioral design, [[Intrinsic Motivation]] serves as a highly potent Reflective and Automatic **Motivation** driver for key phenomena like [[Sustained Behavioral Change]], [[Self-Regulated Learning]], and [[Technology Acceptance]].
- **Leverage Point:** Trigger the [[Intrinsic Regulation Spiral]] by architecting platforms and learning environments that grant users high autonomy and provide immediate competence feedback. This converts otherwise extrinsic tasks into engines of [[Intrinsic Motivation]].
