# Invisible Environment

**Definition:** The emotional atmosphere and relationship dynamics within the family—specifically parental stress, depression, or anxiety—that shape the developing child's brain more profoundly than material circumstances. It is the context in which [[Sensitivity]] becomes [[Attention Deficit Disorder]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Multi-generational trauma, societal stress, and the parents' own [[Implicit Memory]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Disruptions in [[Attunement]], forcing the child into [[Dissociation]].
- **Mechanism:** The infant's brain scans this environment for safety; if it detects anxiety, it downregulates growth systems to focus on survival.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** Healing requires changing this environment (or removing oneself from a toxic one) to allow the nervous system to settle.
- **Leverage Point:** Shifting focus from fixing the "child/symptom" to healing the "parent/context."
