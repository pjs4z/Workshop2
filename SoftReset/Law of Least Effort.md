# Law of Least Effort

**Definition:** A principle stating that humans naturally gravitate toward the option that requires the least amount of work. The brain is wired to conserve energy.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** Habits are obstacles to getting what you want. The higher the friction (effort), the less likely the habit.
- **Flow:** By reducing **Friction** (via [[Environment Design]]), you increase the likelihood of the **Response** in [[The Habit Loop]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Biological evolution/efficiency.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Decisive Moment]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability (Physical):** Reduce the physical steps required to perform an action.
- **Leverage Point:** Prime the environment so the "lazy" choice is the "good" choice.
