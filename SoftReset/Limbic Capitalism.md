# Limbic Capitalism

**Definition:** A global economic system dedicated to providing ubiquitous, high-potency, and low-effort access to addictive goods and services that exploit the brain's primal reward drives.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
We have transformed the world from a place of scarcity to one of overwhelming abundance. Our brains, evolved for scarcity ("cacti in the rainforest"), are now drowning in [[Dopamine]].
*   **The Modern Hypodermic Needle:** The smartphone acts as a digital delivery system for dopamine, removing the friction between desire and consumption.
*   **Access and Potency:** The risk of addiction is amplified by three factors: *Access* (availability), *Potency* (engineered strength, e.g., high-THC cannabis), and *Novelty*.
*   **Supply-Induced Demand:** In this system, supply creates its own demand by triggering the [[Pleasure-Pain Balance]] and inducing [[Craving]].

- **Upstream (Driven by):** Technological Innovation, Market Forces
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Compulsive Overconsumption]], [[Dopamine Deficit State]], [[Prefrontal Cortical Atrophy]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
*   **Opportunity (Physical):** This concept represents the "Environment" in behavioral models. It necessitates the creation of artificial friction (see [[Self-Binding]]) because willpower is insufficient against an environment engineered to bypass it.
