# Logotherapy

**Definition:** A therapeutic philosophy developed by Viktor Frankl asserting that the primary human motivation is the **Will to Meaning**—the drive to find significance in life, particularly through the endurance of suffering.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Logotherapy provides the "Why" that enables the "How" of bearing dopamine deprivation or pain. It stands in contrast to the "Will to Pleasure" (Freud) and "Will to Power" (Adler).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Existential need, Spiritual capacity.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Value Identification]], Resilience, [[The Observing Self]].
- **Interaction:** It enhances [[Acceptance and Commitment Therapy]] by providing the narrative structure necessary to accept the "pole in the basement." It converts "useless suffering" into "meaningful sacrifice."

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Reflective):** The highest form of reflective motivation; it can override powerful automatic [[Dopaminergic Motivation]] triggers (addiction/lust) by linking restraint to a higher identity.
- **Leverage Point:** "He who has a why to live for can bear almost any how."
