# Mastery-Efficacy Cycle

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where high perceived self-efficacy increases the initiation and persistence of coping behavior, which in turn generates successful performance accomplishments that further elevate self-efficacy.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop represents the primary engine of successful psychological and behavioral adaptation. 
1. High [[Self-Efficacy]] increases the initiation and persistence of [[Coping Behavior]].
2. Sustained [[Coping Behavior]] directly generates [[Performance Accomplishments]].
3. Through the subjective filtering of [[Cognitive Appraisal]], these authentic successes further increase [[Self-Efficacy]], creating a positive upward spiral of behavioral change and perceived competency.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Initial injections of [[Participant Modeling]], [[Vicarious Experience]], or [[Verbal Persuasion]] to kickstart the loop.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Long-term behavioral resilience, generalized mastery, and the permanent extinction of [[Defensive Behavior]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
To engineer this loop, practitioners must align **Opportunity** and **Motivation**. The leverage point is not simply to persuade the user they can do it, but to manipulate the physical/social opportunity (e.g., scaffolding the environment) so that [[Coping Behavior]] is easily executed. Once the first cycle of the loop is completed, the resulting motivation becomes self-sustaining.
