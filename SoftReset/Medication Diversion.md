# Medication Diversion

**Definition:** The illicit transfer of prescribed medications within a population, specifically the practice of selling, giving, or exchanging prescribed stimulants for nonmedical use.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Medication Diversion]] acts as the critical bridge transferring therapeutic agents from medical contexts into unregulated, high-risk environments. It fuels the [[Diversion and Misuse Escalation]] loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Prescription Stimulants]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Nonmedical Stimulant Misuse]]
- **Inhibited by:** [[Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs]], [[Executive Functioning Capacity]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Impaired [[Executive Functioning Capacity]] correlates with poor long-term decision making and susceptibility to peer pressure regarding prescription management.
- **Opportunity:** The physical surplus of [[Prescription Stimulants]] obtained through legitimate prescriptions creates the means. The systemic absence or underutilization of [[Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs]] creates regulatory blind spots allowing diversion to occur unnoticed.
- **Motivation:** High environmental peer demand for [[Nonmedical Stimulant Misuse]] creates powerful social and financial motivation to share, sell, or exchange medication.
- **Leverage Points:** Modify opportunity by mandating rigorous, cross-jurisdictional utilization of [[Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs]] to restrict the physical accumulation of surplus pills. Modify motivation by instituting standardized medication contracts and random urine screens to make the clinical consequences of diversion highly salient.
