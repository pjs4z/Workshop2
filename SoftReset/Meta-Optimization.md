# Meta-Optimization

**Definition:** A pathological behavioral loop involving the obsessive, endless tweaking of workflows, software configurations, and organizational systems. It acts as a sophisticated form of procrastination that mimics productivity while delaying the onset of actual cognitive execution.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Meta-Optimization is a **Reinforcing Feedback Loop** driven by affect regulation.
- **The Trigger:** Anxiety or ambiguity regarding a complex [[Deep Work]] task.
- **The Behavior:** Organizing files, downloading apps, or color-coding calendars.
- **The Reward:** A false dopamine hit (feeling productive without risk), which reinforces the behavior and inhibits [[Task Initiation]].
- **The Cure:** Must be interrupted by a strict [[Minimum Viable Context]] or [[Implementation Intention]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Driven by the desire to avoid negative affect (fear of failure). The brain substitutes a high-control, low-risk task (organizing) for a low-control, high-risk task (creating).
- **Leverage Point:** Recognize "preparing to work" as a distinct state from "working," and strictly limit the preparation phase using time-boxing.
