# Neuro-enhancement

**Definition:** The pursuit or practice of using biomedical interventions (drugs, supplements, diet) to improve cognitive, affective, or behavioral performance beyond the species-typical healthy baseline, rather than treating a pathology.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Attempts at Neuro-enhancement interact heavily with biological constraints.
- **Pharmacological Approach:** Uses [[Reuptake Inhibition]] (e.g., Adderall). Often fails long-term due to [[Homeostatic Downregulation]].
- **Nutritional Approach:** Uses [[Chemical Precursor]]s (L-tyrosine). Often limited by metabolic rate-limiting steps.
- **Microbial Approach:** Manipulates the [[Gut-Brain Axis]]. Shows promise for sustainable motivation boosting.
- **Psychological Approach:** Leverages the [[Placebo Effect]], where belief in the enhancer provides the "robust" boost.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Automatic):** Driven by the "Desire Dopamine" circuit seeking "more."
- **Capability (Psychological):** Often hampered by the "More is Better" fallacy. Users fail to distinguish between *restoring* deficits (where Adderall works) and *boosting* healthy baselines (where it causes dysfunction).
