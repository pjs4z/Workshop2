# Neuroadaptation

**Definition:** A biological process where chronic substance use physically alters brain circuitry (specifically the basal ganglia, extended amygdala, and prefrontal cortex), shifting motivation from positive reinforcement (pleasure) to negative reinforcement (relief from dysphoria).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
**Neuroadaptation** is the central mechanism of the **Koob Cycle** (The Neurobiological Cycle of Compulsion).
- **The Mechanism:** Chronic use downregulates dopamine receptors (anhedonia) and upregulates stress systems (dynorphin/CRF).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Somatic Pathology]] paradigms and high-frequency substance administration.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Conditioned Withdrawal]] and the biological incapacity to resist impulses, leading to [[Alcohol Dependence Syndrome]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability (Physical):** Represents a tangible reduction in the brain's capacity for executive control and natural reward processing.
- **Leverage Point:** Pharmacological interventions to stabilize neurochemistry, allowing [[Psychosocial Dislocation]] interventions to take root.
