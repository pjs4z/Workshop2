# Neuroplasticity

**Definition:** The cascade of sequential neuroadaptive changes in brain circuitry, such as long-term potentiation and receptor downregulation, that mediate the transition from occasional, controlled drug use to chronic addiction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Neuroplasticity]] is the mechanical bridge between voluntary drug use and the pathological [[Addiction Cycle]]. It heavily influences the *Habitual Automaticity Loop* by shifting neural control from the ventral striatum to the dorsal striatum.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Binge/Intoxication Stage]] (repeated supra-physiological stimulation catalyzes structural changes).
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Habitual Drug-Seeking]] (increases automaticity), [[Mesolimbic Dopamine System]] (decreases its baseline baseline function).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Neuroplasticity]] severely compromises psychological **Capability** by impairing prefrontal executive control and altering hedonic set points. This leaves the individual defenseless against the **Motivation** to consume drugs.
- **Leverage Point:** Glutamatergic and Cognitive Restoration. Utilizing agents like N-acetylcysteine can reverse drug-induced neuroplasticity by restoring cystine-glutamate exchange. Combined with cognitive training, this can rebuild prefrontal inhibitory control and restore the capability to resist impulses.
