# Opponent-Process

**Definition:** A motivational dynamic where a stimulus producing a strong initial sensation (pleasure/relief) triggers a counter-reaction (pain/craving). Over time, the initial effect weakens while the counter-reaction intensifies, driving the cycle of [[Addiction]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The biological engine behind [[Tolerance]] and [[Withdrawal]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Exposure to strong affective stimuli (drugs, intense romance).
- **Downstream (Drives):** The shift from positive reinforcement (pleasure) to negative reinforcement (avoidance of pain) in [[Addiction]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Explains why addicts persist despite diminishing returns; leverage involves breaking the cycle through "Exposure Therapy" (enduring the counter-reaction until it subsides).
