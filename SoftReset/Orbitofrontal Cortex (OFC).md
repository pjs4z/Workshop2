# Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)

**Definition:** A region of the prefrontal cortex responsible for decision-making, emotion regulation, impulse inhibition ("Free Won't"), and assigning emotional value to stimuli. It is often structurally and functionally impaired in addicts due to developmental trauma or chronic drug use.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The OFC is the "mission control" of the emotional brain.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Shaped by early [[Attunement]] and [[Epigenetics]]. Damaged by chronic stress and neurotoxic substances.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Self-Regulation]] and conscious choice.
- **Dysfunction:** In the [[Addiction Process]], the OFC is overridden by [[Brain Lock]] and fails to inhibit impulses generated by the [[Dopamine]] system. It also misassigns value via [[Salience Attribution]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** This is the primary physical capability bottleneck in addiction. The hardware for "just saying no" is compromised.
- **Leverage:** Can be strengthened through [[The Four Steps]] (neuroplasticity) and [[Compassionate Curiosity]].
