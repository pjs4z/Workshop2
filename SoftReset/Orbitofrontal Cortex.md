# Orbitofrontal Cortex

**Definition:** The area of the right prefrontal cortex responsible for impulse control, social-emotional intelligence, attention allocation, and the inhibition of the autonomic nervous system. In [[Attention Deficit Disorder]], this area is often structurally or functionally underdeveloped due to lack of early [[Attunement]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The OFC is the hardware of self-regulation, sculpted by social interaction.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The process of [[Attunement]] and [[Neural Darwinism]] (use-dependent development).
- **Downstream (Drives):** The capacity for [[Self-Regulation]] and the inhibition of primitive drives like [[Counterwill]] and [[Shame]].
- **Regulation:** It acts as the "brake" on the amygdala and limbic system; when underdeveloped, emotional impulses flood the system without check.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** This is the physical limitation in ADD; the "muscle" that needs strengthening through neuroplasticity.
- **Leverage Point:** Activities that stimulate OFC growth (meditation, mindfulness) and external scaffolding (lists, structure) until internal circuitry strengthens.
