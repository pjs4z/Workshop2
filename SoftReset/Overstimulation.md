# Overstimulation

**Definition:** A heightened state of cognitive and neurological arousal caused by continuous exposure to external triggers, rendering regular, low-stimulation tasks unappealing and difficult to initiate.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Overstimulation]] is the central bottleneck preventing deep work. It creates a "wavelength mismatch" where the brain's current highly stimulated state cannot engage with lower-stimulation tasks, leading directly to [[Procrastination]]. It is a primary component of [[The Hedonic Treadmill of Stimulation]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Stimulation Tolerance]] (as tolerance grows, overstimulation increases), [[Dopamine Relapse]], and [[The Procrastination Escape Cycle]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Heavily increases [[Procrastination]] and decreases [[Long-Term Perspective]]. Inhibited by a [[Dopamine Detox]] and the cultivation of [[Here-and-Now Neurotransmitters]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavior design, [[Overstimulation]] acts as a negative **Capability** driver. It overwhelms cognitive bandwidth, making it mentally impossible to initiate low-stimulation tasks (like reading or writing). To counteract this, one must architect higher [[Environmental Friction]] against distractions and design a [[Closed System]] to restore baseline focus.
