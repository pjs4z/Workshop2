# Participant Modeling

**Definition:** A therapeutic and instructional procedure where the environment is deliberately structured with external aids, guided tasks, and joint mentor/therapist performances to ensure a subject successfully executes feared or highly challenging activities. 

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Participant Modeling]] functions as a highly effective behavioral catalyst by artificially bridging the gap between an individual's current capability and the task requirements. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Environmental structuring, presence of a model/mentor, and progressive task graduation.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Instantly catalyzes [[Performance Accomplishments]], provides rich [[Vicarious Experience]], and rapidly elevates [[Self-Efficacy]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In behavioral design, [[Participant Modeling]] is a high-leverage intervention targeting **Physical Opportunity** and **Psychological Capability**. By structuring the environment so that the subject *cannot* fail (using protective aids, joint performance, and graduated time intervals), the designer forces the generation of early enactive mastery. This directly hacks the subject's **Reflective Motivation**, rapidly collapsing the need for [[Defensive Behavior]] and instating durable [[Coping Behavior]].
