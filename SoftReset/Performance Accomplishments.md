# Performance Accomplishments

**Definition:** Direct experiences of behavioral mastery and successful execution of tasks. Because they are based on authentic, personal enactive mastery, they represent the most dependable and potent source of efficacy information.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Performance Accomplishments]] ground psychological beliefs in physical reality. When a person successfully engages in [[Coping Behavior]] despite fear, the resulting accomplishment provides undeniable evidence of capability, which is internalized via [[Cognitive Appraisal]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Participant Modeling]] (catalyzes early successes), [[Coping Behavior]] (sustained effort).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Radically increases [[Self-Efficacy]]; indirectly decreases anticipatory [[Emotional Arousal]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
From a COM-B perspective, generating [[Performance Accomplishments]] targets **Motivation** (by validating capability) and **Physical/Psychological Capability** (by building habit strength). The highest-leverage intervention to drive behavior change is to design environments with progressive, graduated tasks and external aids (e.g., [[Participant Modeling]]) that *guarantee* early performance accomplishments. This experiential success durably increases [[Self-Efficacy]] and sustains long-term motivation.
