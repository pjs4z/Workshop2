# Plateau of Latent Potential

**Definition:** The temporal gap between the initiation of an [[Atomic Habit]] and the visibility of tangible results. It is the phase where efforts accumulate (store potential) without providing immediate feedback, often leading to discouragement.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This phenomenon explains the non-linear nature of success.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The natural delay in complex systems where inputs ([[Aggregation of Marginal Gains]]) do not produce 1:1 immediate outputs.
- **Downstream (Drives):** The "Valley of Disappointment"—a psychological state where expectations of linear progress clash with the reality of exponential compounding.
- **Critical Threshold:** Results only become visible after crossing this threshold (like ice melting only after reaching 32°F).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** This is the primary killer of motivation.
- **Leverage Point:** Shift focus from the **Goal** (outcome) to the **System** (process). Trust that the work is not wasted, but "stored."
- **Strategy:** Use artificial/immediate rewards (4th Law) to sustain the [[The Habit Loop]] while traversing this plateau.
