# Plateau of Latent Potential

**Definition:** The temporal gap between the effort invested in a habit and the visible results of that habit, often characterized by a period where progress feels nonexistent despite hard work.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This phenomenon explains why many fail to sustain new behaviors; they expect linear progress, but results are often exponential and delayed.
- **Stock:** Effort is "stored" like energy heating an ice cube; the phase change (melting) only happens after a critical threshold is crossed.
- **Relationship:** Outcomes (weight, wealth, knowledge) are **Lagging Measures** of habits.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Atomic Habit]], [[Time Inconsistency]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Motivation dips, potential abandonment of [[The Habit Loop]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** This plateau destroys **Automatic Motivation** because the immediate reward is missing.
- **Leverage Point:** Bridge the gap using [[Habit Tracking]] or immediate rewards ([[Reinforcement]]) to provide visibility of progress while the long-term result is still incubating.
