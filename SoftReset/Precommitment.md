# Precommitment

**Definition:** A decision made in the present to restrict choices in the future, designed to overcome impulsivity and bind oneself to a specific course of action (Traction).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
**Precommitment** is the "fail-safe" or last line of defense in the Indistractable model, used only after mastering **[[Internal Trigger]]** and **[[Timeboxing]]**. It increases the cost or friction of **[[Distraction]]**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Forethought, recognition of weakness.
- **Downstream (Drives):** **[[Traction]]**, adherence to goals.

## Varieties (Mechanisms)
1.  **Effort Pact:** Increasing the friction to perform a distraction (e.g., locking a phone in a box).
2.  **Price Pact:** Attaching a financial penalty to unwanted behavior (leveraging loss aversion).
3.  **[[Identity Pact]]**: Aligning behavior with a self-image (e.g., "I am a vegetarian" or "I am Indistractable").
