# Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs

**Definition:** Statewide and territorial regulatory databases designed to assist health care providers in verifying patient prescriptions, thereby reducing the progression of nonprescription stimulant use and diversion.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs]] act as a systemic balancing mechanism (within the Clinical and Regulatory Feedback Loop) designed to interrupt the unchecked flow of controlled substances into unregulated environments.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** State/territorial regulatory and clinical healthcare systems.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[Medication Diversion]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
These programs act as an **Opportunity** modifier. Systemic absence, fragmentation, or underutilization of these databases creates regulatory blind spots. By mandating their use, the physical opportunity to accumulate surplus [[Prescription Stimulants]] is heavily restricted. They flag early refills and duplicate prescriptions, effectively altering the environmental architecture that permits [[Medication Diversion]] and subsequently curbing [[Nonmedical Stimulant Misuse]].
