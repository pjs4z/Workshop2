# Psychosocial Dislocation

**Definition:** A state of societal fragmentation and lack of meaningful environmental integration that creates a profound psychological void. In this context, addiction is not a disease, but an adaptive coping mechanism to an uninhabitable environment.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Part of the **Alexander Loop**, where isolation drives use, and use drives further isolation.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Systemic marginalization, poverty, and cultural fragmentation.
- **Downstream (Drives):** The need for [[Neuroadaptation]] as a substitute for natural social rewards (dopamine/oxytocin).
- **Inversion:** Challenges [[Somatic Pathology]] by proving that enriched environments ("Rat Park") can override chemical dependency.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity (Social):** The primary driver of addiction in this model is the *lack* of social opportunity for connection.
- **Leverage Point:** Environmental Enrichment—shifting focus from detoxifying the individual to "re-toxifying" their environment with connection and purpose.
