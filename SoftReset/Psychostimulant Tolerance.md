# Psychostimulant Tolerance

**Definition:** The physiological adaptation to exogenous stimulants (like amphetamines) where the brain negates euphoric and energetic effects over time. This forces the user to increase dosages to achieve previous states, often leading to addiction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A **Reinforcing Loop (The Tolerance-Dosage Trap)**.
1. User takes stimulant -> Euphoria/Focus increases.
2. Brain triggers [[Dopamine Downregulation]] to protect itself.
3. Euphoria fades (Tolerance).
4. User increases dosage to overcome tolerance.
5. Brain downregulates further.
- **Exception:** Uniquely, this tolerance loop does *not* appear to occur in ADHD brains regarding the "focus" effects, only the "euphoria" effects in healthy brains.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Reflective vs. Automatic):** The user consciously seeks the "Edge" (Reflective), but the body automatically neutralizes it.
- **Leverage Point:** Acknowledging that "overclocking" is a finite strategy. The behavioral cost is the loss of "casual social interaction" abilities (arrogance/irritability) and eventual crash.
