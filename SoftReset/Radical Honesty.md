# Radical Honesty

**Definition:** The practice of truthful disclosure about one’s behaviors and intent, used as a mechanism to strengthen neural pathways for self-control and generate intimacy.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
*   **Prefrontal Activation:** Lying is associated with scarcity; honesty activates the [[Prefrontal Cortex]], strengthening the brain's "brakes" and future-planning capabilities.
*   **Intimacy as Dopamine:** Honest disclosure, particularly of shame, releases Oxytocin and [[Dopamine]], serving as a high-quality alternative to cheap dopamine sources.
*   **The False Self:** Lying creates a "False Self" leading to derealization. Honesty integrates the narrative, reducing the cognitive load of maintaining deception.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Prosocial Shame]], [[Accountability]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Intimacy]], [[Plenty Mindset]], [[Prefrontal Cortex]] Activation

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
*   **Motivation (Reflective):** Moves the individual from a "Scarcity Mindset" (I must hide to survive) to a "Plenty Mindset" (I am safe to reveal myself).
*   **Prosocial Shame:** Honesty allows for *prosocial shame*—using social friction to correct behavior and find acceptance—rather than *destructive shame* which leads to isolation.
