# Reactance

**Definition:** A motivational state triggered by a threat to behavioral freedom (e.g., being told what to do, scarcity of options). It triggers a [[Dopamine]] surge to regain autonomy, often leading to irrational or rebellious behavior.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Perceived threat to autonomy, Scarcity (e.g., "closing time").
- **Downstream (Drives):** Impulse activation via the [[Desire Circuit]], Lowered standards, Rebellion.
- **Mitigation:** Can be mitigated by [[The Observing Self]] or reframing "have to" as "get to" (restoring autonomy).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** A powerful, often negative, motivator.
- **Leverage Points:** In relationships or negotiation, avoiding direct commands prevents triggering Reactance in others. Internally, recognizing Reactance allows one to avoid "spiteful" behaviors that hurt oneself.
