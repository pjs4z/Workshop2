# Relapse

**Definition:** A temporary return to addictive behavior often triggered by stress or negative emotion, viewed in the [[Life Process Program]] as a learning opportunity rather than a catastrophic failure of recovery.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
In the [[Disease Model of Addiction]], relapse confirms the disease and restarts the cycle. In the [[Life Process Program]], it initiates the **Life Process Learning Loop**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Withdrawal]], stress, environmental triggers.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Can lead to skill acquisition if analyzed correctly, or shame and bingeing if viewed through the disease lens.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Leverage Points:** Analyzing the trigger (Who? What? Where?) to improve [[Coping Skills]].
