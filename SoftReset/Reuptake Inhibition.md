# Reuptake Inhibition

**Definition:** A neurochemical mechanism where compounds (DRIs or SSRIs) prevent the recycling (clearance) of neurotransmitters from the synaptic cleft. This causes the molecule to remain at the receptor site longer, extending its signal.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This is the primary mechanism of action for drugs like Wellbutrin and Ritalin.
- **Mechanics:** Unlike a direct injection of dopamine (blocked by the [[Blood-Brain Barrier]]), this mechanism maximizes the *existing* supply.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Pharmaceutical interventions (DRIs).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increased [[Dopamine]] activity (temporarily) and eventual [[Dopamine Downregulation]] if overused.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability (Physical):** This represents the current limit of medical "enhancement"—we cannot add more fuel, we can only make the engine burn the existing fuel more slowly.
- **Leverage Point:** Effective for those with *deficits* (ADHD, Depression) to reach "normal," but ineffective for healthy brains trying to reach "super-normal" due to the onset of [[Psychostimulant Tolerance]].
