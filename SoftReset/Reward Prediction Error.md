# Reward Prediction Error

**Definition:** A neurochemical response triggered when an actual reward exceeds an expected reward, producing the thrill of novelty and anticipation rather than the pleasure of the reward itself.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Reward Prediction Error]] is the primary catalyst for dopaminergic activation. It relies on unpredictability and novelty. 

- **Upstream (Driven by):** Unpredictable environmental stimuli or unexpected windfalls in the [[Extrapersonal Space]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Directly increases [[Dopamine]]. As a stimulus becomes familiar, [[Reward Prediction Error]] depletes, which in turn causes [[Dopamine]] to drop. This depletion is a structural requirement for [[The Passion-to-Companionship Transition]], allowing [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]] to rise and facilitate [[Companionate Love]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This occurrent is heavily targeted in both the formation and breaking of habits. In the context of [[Addiction]], ubiquitous environmental triggers generate artificial [[Reward Prediction Error]]s, overriding natural satiety. A key leverage point is modifying Opportunity by conducting "search-and-destroy" missions in the physical environment to remove cues and triggers. Conversely, to sustain positive behaviors, engineering organic [[Reward Prediction Error]]s through unpredictable rewards or gamified milestones can sustain Motivation.
