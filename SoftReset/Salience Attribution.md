# Salience Attribution

**Definition:** The neurological and psychological process by which the brain assigns excessive, supreme value to specific stimuli (such as drugs or behaviors). In addiction, the substance is tagged as being as critical to survival as food or oxygen, overriding rational judgment and long-term goals.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Salience attribution is a malfunction of the evaluation systems in the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]] and amygdala.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Surges of [[Dopamine]] triggered by drug use or cues.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Brain Lock]] and the compulsive pursuit inherent in the [[Addiction Process]].
- **Effect:** It creates a "tunnel vision" where the addict cannot perceive the value of anything else (family, health, safety) in comparison to the drug.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Shifts the target behavior (drug use) from a "want" to an existential "need" in the automatic motivation system.
- **Leverage:** [[The Four Steps]] (specifically Step 4: Re-value) aims to consciously dismantle false salience.
