# Self-Efficacy

**Definition:** A central cognitive mechanism representing an individual's enduring belief in their capacity to exercise control over their own motivation, behavior, and social environment.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Self-Efficacy]] acts as a fundamental stock of psychological capability. It is biologically catalyzed by [[Social Cognitive Neural Processing]] and dynamically influenced by environmental interactions. As this cognitive stock grows, it directly increases the occurrence of [[Behavioral Change]] and [[Technology Acceptance]]. It is a critical node within both the [[Efficacy-Behavior-Environment Reinforcement]] loop and the [[Contextual Adaptation Homeostasis]] loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Social Cognitive Neural Processing]], [[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]], [[Performance Accomplishments]] (strongest driver), [[Vicarious Experience]], [[Verbal Persuasion]], and lower states of [[Emotional Arousal]]. Regulated heavily by [[Cognitive Appraisal]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Behavioral Change]], [[Technology Acceptance]], Increases initiation and persistence of [[Coping Behavior]]; strongly decreases [[Defensive Behavior]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the COM-B framework, [[Self-Efficacy]] functions as both a profound Psychological **Capability** and an internal Reflective **Motivation** driver for [[Sustained Behavioral Change]], [[Self-Regulated Learning]], and [[Technology Acceptance]]. When engineering behavior, enhancing perceived self-efficacy is a primary leverage point for sustaining action. Interventions should focus on shifting motivation by systematically increasing efficacy through structured [[Participant Modeling]] and successful enactive mastery, rather than relying purely on exhortation or manipulating [[Outcome Expectancy]].
- **Leverage Point:** Design graded mastery experiences and micro-habits to incrementally elevate [[Self-Efficacy]], thereby expanding the psychological capability and intrinsic drive needed to sustain [[Behavioral Change]].
- **Leverage Point:** Reduce environmental friction by simplifying initial user interfaces (modifying [[Situational Specificity]]), ensuring that early interactions organically increase perceived [[Self-Efficacy]].
