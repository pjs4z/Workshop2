# Self-Parenting

**Definition:** The active process wherein an adult with [[Attention Deficit Disorder]] consciously provides themselves with the understanding, structure, and unconditional regard originally missing in childhood. It is the mechanism for re-initiating [[Neural Darwinism]] in adulthood.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Compassionate curiosity and the refusal to succumb to [[Shame]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The development of [[Self-Regulation]] and the healing of [[Implicit Memory]].
- **Components:** Includes physical care (sleep/nutrition), environmental structuring (decluttering), and spiritual practice (mindfulness).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Shifts motivation from "trying to fix a broken self" (which triggers shame) to "caring for a developing self."
- **Leverage Point:** Treating the self with the same "unconditional positive regard" one would offer a child, creating the safety needed for the brain to drop its defenses ([[Dissociation]]).
