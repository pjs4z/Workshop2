# Self-Regulation

**Definition:** The developmental capacity to maintain emotional stability, manage impulses, and soothe oneself without reliance on external aids. It is not an innate trait but an acquired skill dependent on healthy brain development and early environmental support.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Self-regulation is the psychological output of healthy neurobiology.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A well-developed [[Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)]] formed through secure [[Attunement]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The ability to resist [[Brain Lock]] and engage in conscious choice ("Free Won't").
- **Deficit:** The [[Addiction Process]] is fundamentally a disorder of self-regulation, where external substances are used to perform the regulating function the brain cannot do itself.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** This is the core psychological skill missing in the addict.
- **Leverage:** Can be cultivated via [[The Four Steps]] and [[Compassionate Curiosity]], shifting reliance from automatic mechanisms to conscious awareness.
