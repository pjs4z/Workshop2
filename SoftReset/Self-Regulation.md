# Self-Regulation

**Definition:** The capacity to direct attention, control impulses, and manage internal emotional states. It is not an innate trait but a skill acquired through external regulation provided by attachment figures ([[Attunement]]), which is then internalized by the [[Orbitofrontal Cortex]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A developed [[Orbitofrontal Cortex]] and adequate [[Dopamine]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The ability to focus, delay gratification, and maintain stable relationships.
- **Failure Mode:** In ADD, the shift from external regulation (parent) to internal regulation (self) is interrupted, leaving the adult dependent on external structures (deadlines, crises) to regulate arousal.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** The primary deficit in ADD.
- **Leverage Point:** **Co-Regulation Scaffolding**—Using therapists, partners, or structural aids to provide the regulation the brain cannot yet provide for itself.
