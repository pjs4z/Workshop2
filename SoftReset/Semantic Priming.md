# Semantic Priming

**Definition:** A neurocognitive process where exposure to a specific stimulus triggers "spreading activation" through the brain's semantic network. This renders related concepts, facts, and schemas more accessible, facilitating the connection of "far away" ideas (creativity).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Semantic Priming acts as a "Catalyst" for the neural network, warming up the architecture required for complex problem-solving.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Exposure to diverse, lateral stimuli (e.g., reading unrelated poetry, reviewing "Oblique Strategies").
- **Downstream (Drives):** Directly fuels [[Lateral Thinking]] and overcomes the "Blank Mind" inertia that often leads to procrastination.
- **Hemispheric Roles:** Engages the *Right Hemisphere* (coarse coding/diffuse associations) to complement the *Left Hemisphere's* focus, essential for the "Incubation Period" of creativity.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Increases the "cue set-size" (richness of available associations). Without priming, the brain relies on predictable, vertical logic.
- **Leverage Point:** Mandate a 15-minute "Associative Warm-up" phase prior to [[Deep Work]] to transition the brain from reactive logistics to generative creation.
