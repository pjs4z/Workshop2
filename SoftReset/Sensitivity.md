# Sensitivity

**Definition:** An innate, hereditary temperament characterized by acute physiological and emotional reactivity to external stimuli and the emotional states of others. It is the genetic precursor which, only when combined with environmental stress, may develop into [[Attention Deficit Disorder]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Sensitivity acts as an amplifier within the developmental system.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Genetic inheritance (the "trait" rather than the "disease").
- **Downstream (Drives):** High susceptibility to [[Shame]] and the necessity for [[Dissociation]] if the environment is painful.
- **Feedback Loop:** The **Dissociation Defense Spiral**—Sensitivity makes environmental stress unbearable, forcing the mind to tune out, which hinders the development of [[Self-Regulation]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** High sensitivity allows for deep creativity and empathy if regulated, but causes paralysis if unregulated.
- **Leverage Point:** Accepting sensitivity as a "gift" that requires specific environmental protections (like [[Self-Parenting]] and nature immersion) rather than suppression.
