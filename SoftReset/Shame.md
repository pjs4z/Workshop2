# Shame

**Definition:** A low-arousal physiological state of the autonomic nervous system triggered by a break in contact or disapproval from an attachment figure. In [[Attention Deficit Disorder]], this becomes a chronic background emotion, leading to lethargy and a depletion of [[Dopamine]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Shame is the physiological opposite of excitement/connection.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Implicit Memory]] of rejection and failure; [[Sensitivity]] to criticism.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Withdrawal, lack of motivation, and compensatory [[Counterwill]] or hyperactivity to escape the painful feeling.
- **Feedback Loop:** **The Shame-Arousal Trap**—Shame lowers dopamine/arousal -> Brain seeks high stimulation to compensate -> Impulsive behavior ensues -> Leads to more shame.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Shame is a paralyzing agent. It destroys the energy needed for change.
- **Leverage Point:** **Compassionate Curiosity**—Replacing self-judgment with inquiry to bypass the shame response and keep the brain in a learning state.
