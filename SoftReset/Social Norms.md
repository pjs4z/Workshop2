# Social Norms

**Definition:** The invisible rules and shared expectations of a group that guide behavior. We are evolutionarily wired to imitate the habits of three groups: The Close (family/friends), The Many (the tribe), and The Powerful (status figures).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Mechanism:** Belonging to a tribe reduces uncertainty and signals safety. Behaviors that help us fit in are attractive.
- **Feedback:** Deviating from norms causes social friction; adhering provides social **Reinforcement**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Evolutionary drive for belonging.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Identity]], [[Atomic Habit]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity (Social):** Join a culture where your desired behavior is the normal behavior.
- **Leverage Point:** New habits seem achievable when we see others doing them (imitating the Many).
