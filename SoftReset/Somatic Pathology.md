# Somatic Pathology

**Definition:** The conceptualization of addiction as an involuntary physical dysfunction or disease state, independent of moral character or spiritual failing. It posits that the locus of authority lies within the biological tissue of the addict, characterized by a loss of agency to a pharmacological agent.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
**Somatic Pathology** functions as a stabilizing feedback loop in societal treatment structures, moving the addict from the penal system to the medical system. However, it can create a reinforcing fatalism loop for the individual.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Historical shifts from theological sin to [[Neuroadaptation]] and the [[Biopsychosocial Framework]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The [[Dual Pathology]] model (AA), institutionalization of care, and the modern **Brain Disease Model of Addiction (BDMA)**.
- **Tension:** Often conflicts with concepts of [[Reciprocal Determinism]] and [[Psychosocial Dislocation]] by over-emphasizing biological inevitability.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Reflective):** drastically reduces *moral* culpability, increasing willingness to seek help.
- **Capability (Psychological):** Can inadvertently reduce [[Self-Efficacy]] by convincing the patient they are powerless over a chronic, progressive condition.
- **Leverage Point:** Utilizing the medical diagnosis to secure resources while preventing the internalization of "brokenness."
