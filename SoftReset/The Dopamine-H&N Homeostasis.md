# The Dopamine-H&N Homeostasis

**Definition:** A balancing feedback loop where future-oriented dopamine and present-oriented H&N neurotransmitters mutually inhibit each other to regulate human behavior between pursuit and satisfaction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop governs the fundamental tension between wanting and liking. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** High [[Dopamine]] levels (triggered by [[Reward Prediction Error]]) drive focus toward the [[Extrapersonal Space]], structurally inhibiting [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** When a goal is accomplished, resulting in [[Mastery]], [[Dopamine]] temporarily shuts down. This removes the inhibition on [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]], allowing them to spike and shift human focus back to the [[Peripersonal Space]] and [[Affiliative Relationship]]s.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
A healthy capability to navigate life requires this loop to remain balanced. If [[Dopamine]] continuously overrides [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]], it leads to burnout, dissatisfaction, or [[Addiction]]. A vital behavioral leverage point is intentionally intervening to reset the loop: enforcing sensory-focused tasks (like physical creation, exposure to nature, or mindfulness) provides the Opportunity to spike [[Here and Now Neurotransmitters]], naturally suppressing dopaminergic anxiety and restoring psychological Capability.
