# The Habit Loop

**Definition:** The four-step neurological feedback cycle (Cue, Craving, Response, Reward) that allows the brain to automate solutions to recurring problems by associating a specific trigger with a desired change in internal state.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop is the engine of [[Automaticity]]. It scans the environment for a **Cue**, predicts a **Reward**, and executes a response to satisfy a **Craving**.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A recurring problem or stressor in the environment.
- **Downstream (Drives):** The formation and reinforcement of an [[Atomic Habit]].
- **Feedback Mechanism:** If the **Reward** is satisfying, the link between the Cue and the Response is strengthened. If unsatisfying, the link weakens (Reinforcement Learning).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop forms the basis of the **Four Laws of Behavior Change**:
1. **Cue (Make it Obvious):** Increases Opportunity.
2. **Craving (Make it Attractive):** Increases Motivation.
3. **Response (Make it Easy):** Increases Capability.
4. **Reward (Make it Satisfying):** Closes the feedback loop.
