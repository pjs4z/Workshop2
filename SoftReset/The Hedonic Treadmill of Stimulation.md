# The Hedonic Treadmill of Stimulation

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where constant dopamine influx increases tolerance, requiring more intense stimuli to achieve the same baseline sensation of reward, ultimately driving chronic overstimulation.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop explains the biological trap of the modern digital environment. As an individual interacts with an [[Open System]], it catalyzes [[Attention Hijacking]], triggering the release of [[Dopamine]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Open System]] exposure and [[Attention Hijacking]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** The constant influx of [[Dopamine]] increases [[Stimulation Tolerance]], which in turn heavily increases [[Overstimulation]]. This drives the individual to seek out even more intense external stimuli to feel satisfied, repeating and strengthening the cycle. It ultimately results in severe [[Procrastination]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop fundamentally hijacks **Motivation**. By continuously raising the threshold for reward, normal tasks lose all motivational pull. Breaking this loop requires a systemic intervention—specifically, a [[Dopamine Detox]] to reset biological tolerance, and the introduction of [[Environmental Friction]] to cut off the supply of cheap stimuli.
