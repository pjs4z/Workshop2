# The Observing Self

**Definition:** A metacognitive construct representing the part of the mind that can monitor thoughts and feelings without fusing with them. It allows for value-aligned decision making by creating a gap between stimulus and response.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The Observing Self utilizes **Cognitive Defusion** to break the automatic link between the [[Desire Circuit]] and behavior.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Mindfulness, ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy), [[Control Circuit]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibition of [[Reactance]], Reduced impulsivity.
- **Mechanism:** "Surfing the urge"—watching a craving rise and fall without acting on it.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Enhances psychological flexibility.
- **Leverage Points:** A primary tool for treating addiction and compulsion. It moves the locus of control from the automatic [[Desire Circuit]] to conscious choice.
