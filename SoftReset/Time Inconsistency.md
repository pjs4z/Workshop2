# Time Inconsistency

**Definition:** The tendency of the human brain to value immediate rewards (instant gratification) more highly than delayed rewards.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
- **Context:** Humans evolved in an **Immediate-Return Environment** but now live in a **Delayed-Return Environment**.
- **Conflict:** Bad habits often have immediate rewards and delayed costs. Good habits often have immediate costs and delayed rewards.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Evolutionary biology.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Plateau of Latent Potential]], [[Commitment Device]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** We need immediate **Reinforcement** to stick to habits that only pay off in the long run.
- **Leverage Point:** Use [[Habit Tracking]] or [[Temptation Bundling]] to pull long-term rewards into the present moment.
