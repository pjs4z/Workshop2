# Timeboxing

**Definition:** The practice of assigning a specific, bounded time slot to every task or activity in one's day, eliminating "white space" on the calendar. It is the only way to differentiate **[[Traction]]** from **[[Distraction]]**.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
**Timeboxing** converts abstract values into concrete time. It acts as a constraint that reduces cognitive load during the day by pre-deciding "what" and "when." It requires regular maintenance through "Schedule Syncing" with stakeholders to align expectations.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Personal values, **[[Indistractable]]** identity.
- **Downstream (Drives):** **[[Traction]]**, reduction of **[[External Trigger]]** efficacy (by signaling unavailability).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** Creates a rigid structure that prevents "liminal moments" (transition times) from becoming distraction traps.
- **Capability:** Shifts focus from "controlling outcomes" (which we can't control) to "controlling inputs" (time spent).
