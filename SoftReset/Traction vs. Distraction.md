# Traction vs. Distraction

**Definition:** The binary classification of all human behavior based on intent. **Traction** is any action that pulls you toward what you want to do; **Distraction** is any action that pulls you away from what you planned to do.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This dichotomy reframes productivity: you cannot call something a distraction unless you know what it is distracting you *from*. Therefore, without **[[Timeboxing]]**, distraction cannot theoretically exist.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** **[[Internal Trigger]]** (the root urge), **[[External Trigger]]** (the environmental cue).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Achievement of values (Traction) or regret/time loss (Distraction).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Opportunity:** **[[Timeboxing]]** provides the clear opportunity to define Traction.
- **Motivation:** Understanding this distinction shifts the goal from "avoiding tech" to "aligning with intent."
