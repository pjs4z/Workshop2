# Triadic Reciprocal Determinism

**Definition:** The dynamic, unfolding process of continuous interaction and mutual influence among personal cognitive factors, enacted behaviors, and environmental or social contexts.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]] serves as the macro-level occurrent that mediates the interaction between internal cognition and external reality. [[Behavioral Change]] acts as a temporal part of this broader sociocultural process. As behaviors are enacted within an environment, the resulting feedback alters the environment and subsequently increases internal [[Self-Efficacy]]. It operates as the central engine of the [[Efficacy-Behavior-Environment Reinforcement]] loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Behavioral Change]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Self-Efficacy]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This phenomenon acts as an overarching **Opportunity** (both physical and social) driver across multiple target behaviors, fundamentally shaping [[Sustained Behavioral Change]] and [[Technology Acceptance]]. 
- **Leverage Point:** Engineer the environment to minimize restrictive [[Situational Specificity]] while deliberately harnessing [[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]] by introducing positive social models and peer-support structures.
- **Leverage Point:** Embed social proof and observational learning features within digital tools to harness [[Triadic Reciprocal Determinism]], actively accelerating widespread sociocultural adoption.
