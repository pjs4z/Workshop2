# Vesting Period of Salience

**Definition:** The temporal capacity of the neurochemical dopamine system to sustain the psychological weight of distal goals within working memory before they degrade due to a lack of immediate stimulus.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This is the "container size" for motivation. In neurotypical architecture, this spans years. In dysregulated systems, it is compressed to hours.

*   **Upstream (Driven by):** Negatively impacted by [[Horizon Compression]] (caused by chronic dopaminergic overstimulation).
*   **Downstream (Drives):** The stability of the [[Inference Substrate]]. If the vesting period is shorter than the task duration, [[Inference Evaporation]] is inevitable.
*   **Feedback Loop:** [[Enforced Systemic Abstinence]] creates a Reinforcing Loop that expands this period, restoring executive function.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
*   **Capability (Physical):** The fundamental biological constraint on long-term planning.
*   **Leverage Point:** This is the primary target for biological intervention. "Psychological reframing" fails here because the container is physically too small to hold the goal.
