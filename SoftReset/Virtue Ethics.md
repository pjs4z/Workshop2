# Virtue Ethics

**Definition:** A framework involving the conscious selection of higher principles (e.g., wisdom, justice, courage, temperance) to guide behavior. It serves as the decision-making logic used by [[Executive Modulation]] to filter impulses.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Acts as the compass that directs the raw energy of [[Dopamine]] toward constructive ends rather than consumption.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Cultural/Religious learning, personal philosophy.
- **Downstream (Catalyzes):** [[Eudaimonic Fulfillment]].
- **Regulation:** Provides the "Why" that enables the "How" of [[Executive Modulation]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation (Reflective):** The "Aristotelian Heuristic." Before acting on an urge, the behavior is checked against these chosen virtues.
- **Leverage Point:** Explicitly writing down and defining one's virtues creates a mental model that is easier to access during moments of high [[Dopaminergic Anticipation]].
