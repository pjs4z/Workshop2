# Withdrawal

**Definition:** The experience of distress when an addictive substance or activity is removed, interpreted in the [[Life Process Program]] as an existential crisis of losing one's primary coping mechanism rather than solely a physiological event.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A critical friction point that often catalyzes [[Relapse]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Cessation of [[Addiction]] or [[Interpersonal Addiction]]; mediated by the [[Opponent-Process]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Relapse]] (to relieve distress); motivation to develop new [[Coping Skills]].
- **Mitigated by:** [[The Placebo Effect]] (belief/expectations) and supportive social settings (e.g., Vietnam veterans returning home).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** The desire to avoid this pain often drives the [[Addictive Cycle]].
- **Leverage Points:** Viewing withdrawal as a temporary skill-building challenge rather than a biological imperative.
