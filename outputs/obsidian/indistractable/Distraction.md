# Distraction

**Definition:** Any action that moves an individual away from their goals, fundamentally serving as an unhealthy escape from psychological discomfort or reality.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Distraction]] acts as the primary negative outflow in the attention system, pulling focus away from intended goals (the opposite of [[Traction]]). It is frequently catalyzed by a [[Liminal Moment]]—a transition period where attention is vulnerable. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Internal Trigger]], [[External Trigger]], [[Phubbing]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[The Psychological Nutrient Depletion Trap]], [[The Cycle of Responsiveness Escalation]] (by reinforcing the norm of constant availability)
- **Inhibited by:** [[Precommitment]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavior design, engaging in distraction (like tech overuse) is driven by a combination of Motivation (the urge to escape the discomfort of an [[Internal Trigger]] or depletion of [[Psychological Nutrients]]) and Opportunity (the ubiquity of an [[External Trigger]] or [[Job Strain]]). 

Key leverage points to engineer this behavior out of a system include:
- **Modifying Motivation:** Disarm the [[Internal Trigger]] by teaching individuals to observe their discomfort rather than suppressing it.
- **Modifying Opportunity:** Hack back [[External Trigger]]s by adjusting device settings, clearing workspaces, and challenging the [[Cycle of Responsiveness]].
- **Modifying Opportunity:** Introduce a [[Social Antibody]] to actively inhibit [[Phubbing]] and dismantle the social contagion loop that normalizes distraction.
