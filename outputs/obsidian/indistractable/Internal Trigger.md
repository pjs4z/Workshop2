# Internal Trigger

**Definition:** Cues originating from within an individual, such as negative emotions or physical discomfort, that prompt them to seek relief through a specific behavior.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
An [[Internal Trigger]] is the root cause of off-task behavior, representing the psychological friction that demands pacification. When individuals attempt mental abstinence to suppress these triggers, they often trigger the [[Ironic Rebound Loop]], where [[Ironic Process Theory]] increases [[Rumination]], making the urge even stronger.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Negativity Bias]], [[Hedonic Adaptation]], [[Rumination]], [[Job Strain]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Distraction]]
- **Inhibited by:** Fulfillment of [[Psychological Nutrients]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Internal Trigger]] serves as the foundational Motivation driver for unwanted behaviors. If individuals lack the capability to manage this discomfort, they will reliably seek out a [[Distraction]]. To successfully intervene, individuals must build the psychological Capability to ride out these triggers rather than fighting them. By observing the urge without judgment, the individual breaks the link between the [[Internal Trigger]] and the subsequent automated escape behavior.
