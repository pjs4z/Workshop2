# Precommitment

**Definition:** A freely made decision designed and intended to bind one's future choices to prevent distraction. These are implemented as effort pacts, price pacts, or identity pacts.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A [[Precommitment]] acts as the final homeostatic boundary in the attention system. It is the core mechanism of [[The Precommitment Protection Loop]], engaging only when other defenses fail to keep an individual on track. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** The anticipation or frequent occurrence of [[Distraction]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Traction]]
- **Inhibits:** [[Distraction]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
A [[Precommitment]] targets both Opportunity and Motivation to lock in focused work. 
- **Opportunity:** Effort pacts physically alter the environment to make [[Distraction]] highly inconvenient (e.g., website blockers).
- **Motivation:** Price pacts and identity pacts attach severe financial or psychological costs to abandoning [[Traction]]. 
Leverage point: These pacts should only be deployed *after* an individual has managed their [[Internal Trigger]] and planned their time via [[Timeboxing]]; otherwise, the friction of the pact will lead to frustration and system bypass.
