# The Psychological Nutrient Depletion Trap

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where a deficit in autonomy, competence, or relatedness increases psychological discomfort, driving individuals toward digital distraction which further isolates and depletes them.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop explains the systemic failure of motivation that drives chronic tech overuse, particularly in children and stressed workers. A deficit in [[Psychological Nutrients]] increases an [[Internal Trigger]] (feelings of inadequacy, loneliness, or lack of control). To escape this discomfort, the individual engages in [[Distraction]] (e.g., scrolling social media, playing video games). This [[Distraction]] consumes time and isolates the individual, further depleting their [[Psychological Nutrients]] and strengthening the next [[Internal Trigger]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Lack of [[Psychological Safety]], [[Job Strain]], overly restrictive environments
- **Downstream (Drives):** Chronic [[Distraction]], [[Phubbing]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop is a systemic Motivation failure. The ultimate leverage point is structural: ensuring that environments (workplaces, schools, homes) fulfill essential [[Psychological Nutrients]] in the real world. For example, fostering [[Psychological Safety]] at work decreases [[Job Strain]], naturally restoring these nutrients so that individuals are no longer driven to seek them through digital escapism.
