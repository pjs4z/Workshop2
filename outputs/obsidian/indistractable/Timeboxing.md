# Timeboxing

**Definition:** The practice of setting an implementation intention by deciding what you are going to do and exactly when you are going to do it, effectively eliminating blank space on a calendar.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Timeboxing]] acts as a structural defense mechanism that clearly demarcates the boundary between intended and unintended actions. Without it, the distinction between [[Traction]] and [[Distraction]] is ambiguous.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** N/A (Self-initiated planning protocol)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Traction]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Timeboxing]] acts as a critical Capability driver for achieving [[Traction]]. It shifts the individual from a state of reactive behavior (responding to every [[External Trigger]]) to reflective planning. By providing a rigid cognitive structure, it eliminates the ambiguity of choice in the moment, making it easier to leverage tools like [[Multichannel Multitasking]] and [[Temptation Bundling]] during predetermined blocks of time.
