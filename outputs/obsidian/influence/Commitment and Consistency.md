# Commitment and Consistency

**Definition:** The deep-seated drive to align one's current and future beliefs, words, and actions with choices, stands, or public declarations previously made.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Consistency is generally adaptive, but when weaponized, it traps individuals in behaviors that run counter to their self-interest.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Initiated and increased by compliance tactics like [[Foot-In-The-Door]] and the [[Low-Ball Tactic]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases reliance on [[System 1 Thinking]] because the individual no longer feels the need to evaluate new information. It is the engine behind [[The Commitment Justification Engine]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Acts as a powerful **Motivation** driver for escalating agreement. 
- **Leverage Points:** To combat the weaponization of consistency, choice architects must structure environments where initial agreements are kept private rather than public, neutralizing the societal pressure to remain consistent. Using the "But you are free" technique during a [[Rejection-Then-Retreat]] sequence restores perceived autonomy and lowers [[Psychological Reactance]].
