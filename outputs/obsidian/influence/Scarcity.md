# Scarcity

**Definition:** The psychological perception that opportunities, items, or information hold greater value when their availability is restricted or dwindling.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Scarcity derives its power from the evolutionary threat of losing access to vital resources. It overrides logical valuation by creating visceral emotional arousal.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed significantly by [[Loss Aversion]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Dramatically increases [[Psychological Reactance]] as individuals feel their behavioral freedoms shrinking, serving as the catalyst for [[The Scarcity-Reactance Arousal Loop]]. 

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Scarcity acts as a profound **Opportunity** driver for [[Automaticity]]. By altering the physical or temporal availability of an item (e.g., deadlines, limited stock), choice architects artificially constrain the target's opportunity space, forcing a hasty reliance on [[System 1 Thinking]].
