# Social Proof

**Definition:** An informational shortcut where individuals determine what is correct or appropriate in a given situation by observing and adopting the beliefs and actions of others.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Social Proof is most effective under conditions of high uncertainty and when the observed group shares high [[Unity]] (similarity/peer-suasion).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increased by [[Unity]] and catalyzed strongly by uncertainty. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases reliance on [[System 1 Thinking]]. It can drive dark societal trends like the [[Werther Effect]] or become trapped in circular logic via [[Pluralistic Ignorance]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Social Proof serves as a powerful **Opportunity** driver. Seeing others perform a behavior signals both validity and feasibility, lowering the psychological friction to act. Conversely, highlighting the prevalence of an *undesirable* behavior accidentally provides Social Proof for that behavior, encouraging it (The Big Mistake).
