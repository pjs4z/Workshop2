# System 1 Thinking

**Definition:** A mode of cognitive processing that is fast, associative, intuitive, and emotionally driven, often guiding behavioral choices via simple heuristics when a person is overloaded or distracted.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This cognitive mode relies on fast heuristics rather than deep analysis. It is highly susceptible to emotional flooding and cognitive fatigue. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Triggered by the presence of [[Authority]], [[Social Proof]], and [[Commitment and Consistency]]. It is also forcefully activated when [[Psychological Reactance]] or high emotional arousal inhibits [[System 2 Thinking]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Directly increases [[Automaticity]] and drives the mechanics behind [[The Pluralistic Ignorance Trap]] and [[The Commitment Justification Engine]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
System 1 Thinking acts as the primary **Capability** driver for mindless compliance. When individuals lack the psychological bandwidth to process complex information, they default to this system. Structuring environments that demand fast decisions intentionally traps targets in System 1, increasing their vulnerability to influence levers.
