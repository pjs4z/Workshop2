# System 2 Thinking

**Definition:** A mode of cognitive processing that is slower, deliberative, analytical, and rational, used for thorough evaluation but easily suppressed by emotional arousal or cognitive fatigue.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
System 2 Thinking is the primary defense mechanism against exploitative influence tactics, but it is a highly limited resource.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Activated by conscious physiological pattern interrupts (e.g., "heart-of-heart signs") that break the momentum of [[System 1 Thinking]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[Automaticity]] and prevents the escalation of loops like [[The Scarcity-Reactance Arousal Loop]]. It is directly inhibited by intense [[Psychological Reactance]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
To protect individuals from unethical compliance practitioners, interventions must design **Opportunity** (time, physical space, reflection prompts) to allow System 2 to activate. Providing "cooling off" periods naturally restores the capacity for System 2 Thinking.
