# The Commitment Justification Engine

**Definition:** A reinforcing sociocultural loop where a minor compliance shifts a person's self-image, causing them to unconsciously invent new rationalizations that permanently anchor the new behavior.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop explains how people become radicalized or deeply loyal to brands, cults, or relationships even after the original incentives are removed.
- **Mechanism:** A small initial compliance technique, such as [[Foot-In-The-Door]], successfully induces an initial state of [[Commitment and Consistency]]. To avoid the cognitive labor of [[System 2 Thinking]], the individual relies on [[System 1 Thinking]] ([[Automaticity]]) to align their self-image with this new stance. This internal justification creates new "legs to stand on," which deepens the level of [[Commitment and Consistency]], making the individual highly vulnerable to further exploitation via the [[Low-Ball Tactic]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop fundamentally rewires internal **Motivation**. Because the target invents their own reasons for the behavior, the behavior persists autonomously. Disrupting this engine requires the target to isolate the *original* reason for the commitment and ask if they would make the same choice today.
