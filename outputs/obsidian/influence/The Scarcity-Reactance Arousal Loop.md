# The Scarcity-Reactance Arousal Loop

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop wherein the perception of scarcity triggers a psychological threat response, flooding the brain with arousal that disables rational thought and amplifies the perceived value of the scarce resource.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop explains why humans make highly irrational, aggressive decisions in competitive environments (e.g., auctions, Black Friday sales).
- **Mechanism:** The presence of [[Scarcity]] triggers [[Psychological Reactance]] as individuals feel their freedoms are restricted. This reactance causes emotional arousal which inhibits deliberative [[System 2 Thinking]] and shifts the brain into [[System 1 Thinking]]. Operating under System 1, the individual fixates solely on the limited availability, which artificially magnifies the perceived [[Scarcity]] of the item, thereby generating even more [[Psychological Reactance]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop is utilized to block a target's **Capability** for rational thought. By introducing competition alongside scarcity, practitioners hyper-accelerate this loop. To defend against it, individuals must use the physiological arousal itself as a cue to step away and force [[System 2 Thinking]].
