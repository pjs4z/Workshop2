# Addictive Process

**Definition:** A dynamic coping mechanism and natural response to unnatural circumstances, functioning as an organism's attempt to soothe unbearable emotional pain and trauma.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Addictive Process]] is not a standalone pathology but an emergent symptom of deep psychological and biological feedback mechanisms. It functions as the central node in both the [[Neurobiological Hijacking Loop]] and the [[Trauma-Escape Futility Cycle]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Self-Escape Mechanism]], [[Developmental Trauma]], [[Bad Choices Paradigm]], [[Disease Paradigm]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Neurobiological Adaptation]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Understanding the [[Addictive Process]] requires analyzing the systemic drivers that maintain it:
- **Capability:** Diminished by [[Neurobiological Adaptation]] (altered brain circuitry undermining voluntary control) and [[Developmental Trauma]] (reduced cognitive bandwidth for processing distress).
- **Opportunity:** Constrained by the [[Bad Choices Paradigm]] (punitive legal environments that exacerbate isolation) and the [[Disease Paradigm]] (clinical models that ignore biographical context).
- **Motivation:** Driven by the [[Self-Escape Mechanism]] (the automatic/reflective drive to seek anesthesia) and the continuous emotional pain stemming from [[Developmental Trauma]].
- **Leverage Points:** 
  - Shift clinical/societal interventions from punitive measures to trauma-informed care protocols.
  - Directly address underlying [[Developmental Trauma]] to reduce the emotional pain driving the [[Self-Escape Mechanism]].
  - Evolve treatment beyond the [[Disease Paradigm]] by utilizing therapies that heal emotional regulation rather than simply managing a chronic biochemical pathology.
