# Developmental Trauma

**Definition:** Early life experiences of severe distress, such as emotional isolation, physical abuse, or family chaos, which inflict deep psychological pain and act as the primary catalyst for later addictive behaviors.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Developmental Trauma]] acts as the foundational stock of emotional pain in the human system. It is continuously exacerbated by larger societal forces and acts as the primary flow catalyzing the need for coping mechanisms in the [[Trauma-Escape Futility Cycle]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Multigenerational Colonial Trauma]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Self-Escape Mechanism]], [[Addictive Process]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Severe trauma fundamentally impairs emotional regulation skills and limits the psychological bandwidth required to process distress natively.
- **Motivation:** Acts as the underlying reservoir of unbearable emotional pain, generating an overwhelming, continuous need for the [[Self-Escape Mechanism]].
- **Leverage Points:** Behavioral remediation must bypass the symptom (the [[Addictive Process]]) and target the trauma itself. Healing the psychological capability to endure and regulate emotions removes the primary motivational driver of addiction.
