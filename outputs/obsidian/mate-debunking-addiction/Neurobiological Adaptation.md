# Neurobiological Adaptation

**Definition:** The long-lasting alteration of the brain's reward apparatus and impulse regulation circuits caused by repeated drug use or compulsive behaviors, which fundamentally undermines voluntary control.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Neurobiological Adaptation]] bridges the psychological and physical domains. As the [[Addictive Process]] flows over time, it increases this adaptation. This creates the [[Neurobiological Hijacking Loop]]: a reinforcing feedback cycle where accumulated neurochemical impairment destroys impulse control, thereby dramatically increasing the drive to repeat the addictive behavior.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Addictive Process]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Addictive Process]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Acts as a severe physical and psychological inhibitor. By compromising the brain's impulse regulation circuitry, it dismantles the individual's physiological capacity for voluntary control (often mischaracterized by the [[Bad Choices Paradigm]] as a moral failing).
- **Leverage Points:** Treatment must recognize this as an impaired state of the organ and utilize therapies focused on neuroplasticity to slowly rebuild the biological capability for self-regulation, rather than demanding sheer willpower.
