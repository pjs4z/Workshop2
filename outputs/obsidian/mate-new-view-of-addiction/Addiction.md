# Addiction

**Definition:** A complex psychological, biological, and social process manifesting through any behavior that provides temporary relief or pleasure, leading to craving, long-term negative consequences, and an inability to stop.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Addiction]] functions as a systemic Occurrent rather than a discrete disease. It is the downstream consequence of unhealed [[Psychological Trauma]] and systemic vulnerabilities introduced by [[Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)]]. The process is heavily regulated by [[Epigenetic Modulation]], which impairs both the [[Endorphin System]] and the [[Incentive-Motivation System]]. As individuals engage in [[Self-Medication]] or a [[Behavioral Addiction]], they trigger a [[Neurochemical Craving Loop]] that reinforces the dependency. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Self-Medication]], [[Behavioral Addiction]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Psychological Trauma]] (via long-term negative consequences)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the COM-B model, [[Addiction]] acts as an overwhelming Automatic Motivation driver. It overrides an individual's conscious, reflective desire to stop by structurally hijacking the brain's dopamine pathways. Because the behavior offers short-term relief, the motivation to engage in it repeatedly outcompetes the abstract awareness of long-term suffering. Escaping this behavior requires shifting away from models of [[Genetic Determinism]] and instead actively addressing the underlying Capability deficits (unprocessed trauma).
