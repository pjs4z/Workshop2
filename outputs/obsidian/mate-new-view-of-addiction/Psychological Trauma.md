# Psychological Trauma

**Definition:** An enduring internal emotional injury resulting from unprocessed, intolerable feelings incurred through adversity. It persists within the individual as a foundational driver for self-medicating behaviors.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Psychological Trauma]] is the central Continuant fueling the compulsion for addictive behaviors. It is catalyzed by environmental and developmental stressors such as [[Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)]]. Because the individual lacks the biological or emotional bandwidth to process these feelings—often worsened by [[Epigenetic Modulation]] stunting the [[Endorphin System]]—they turn to external soothing mechanisms. This creates [[The Trauma-Addiction Cycle]], where the negative fallout of addiction simply generates more trauma.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)]], [[Addiction]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Self-Medication]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Trauma acts as a severe Psychological Capability barrier. Individuals lack the cognitive and emotional ability to process intolerable feelings, often masking this deficit with self-delusion or "happy childhood" blind spots. 
**Leverage Points:**
- **Modify Capability:** Implement trauma-informed therapeutic protocols (such as compassionate inquiry) to help individuals safely process underlying [[Psychological Trauma]]. This increases their emotional bandwidth to tolerate distress naturally, significantly reducing the automatic drive for [[Self-Medication]].
