# Self-Medication

**Definition:** An attempt to cope with and soothe internal trauma or lack of emotional attachment by utilizing external substances or behaviors that provide temporary, targeted psychoactive relief.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Self-Medication]] is the active behavioral process (Occurrent) deployed to manage the pain of [[Psychological Trauma]]. By engaging in substance use or a [[Behavioral Addiction]], the individual forces unnatural spikes in [[Dopamine]], artificially fulfilling the role of a healthy [[Incentive-Motivation System]]. Over time, this behavior becomes a central temporal part of the broader [[Addiction]] process.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Psychological Trauma]], [[Dopamine]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Dopamine]], [[Addiction]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Self-Medication]] is the primary Target Behavior in the addiction process, driven by a combination of Reflective and Automatic Motivation (the persistent drive to find temporary relief or illusory freedom). 
**Leverage Points:**
- **Modify Opportunity:** Engineer digital and physical environments to introduce friction against ubiquitous triggers (e.g., regulating dopamine-exploitative algorithms in apps and pornography), thereby reducing the environmental accessibility of continuous neurochemical spikes.
- **Modify Motivation:** Decouple [[Dopamine]]-driven cravings from harmful [[Self-Medication]] by cultivating environments of healthy social affiliation and attachment. This innately satisfies the [[Incentive-Motivation System]] and reduces the biological urgency for external relief.
