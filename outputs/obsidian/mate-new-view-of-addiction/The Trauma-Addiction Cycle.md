# The Trauma-Addiction Cycle

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where early developmental adversity and unprocessed emotional pain drive self-medicating behaviors, which provide temporary relief but ultimately generate long-term negative consequences that deepen the original pain.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This Reinforcing Loop operates as the core engine of dependency. It begins when [[Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)]] catalyze [[Psychological Trauma]]. To cope with this unprocessed pain, the individual turns to [[Self-Medication]]. While this provides a temporary soothing effect, it becomes embedded in the systemic process of [[Addiction]]. The long-term negative consequences of addiction—such as social ostracization, health deterioration, and familial breakdown—actively increase the individual's baseline of [[Psychological Trauma]], which in turn drives a higher biological and emotional need for further [[Self-Medication]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Escalation of [[Addiction]] and [[Psychological Trauma]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop illustrates how Automatic Motivation (the drive for pain relief) continuously compromises Psychological Capability over time. The primary leverage point requires interrupting the feedback loop by addressing the root Capability barrier: utilizing compassionate inquiry to process the underlying [[Psychological Trauma]], stripping the loop of its primary fuel source before [[Self-Medication]] is triggered.
