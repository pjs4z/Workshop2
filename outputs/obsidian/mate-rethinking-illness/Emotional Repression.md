# Emotional Repression

**Definition:** An automatic, unconscious mechanism that exiles risky or painful feelings to escape overwhelming vulnerability and maintain equilibrium with the ambient world.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Emotional Repression]] acts as a central engine for various psychological pathologies. It is heavily catalyzed by [[Cultural Alienation]], causing the mind to automatically split from the self to survive unbearable tension. Once established, it acts as a primary driver that increases the likelihood of physiological shutdowns like [[Depression]], and escapist adaptations like [[Dissociation]], [[Self-Harm]], and [[Anorexia]]. It is constantly reinforced by behavioral feedback mechanisms such as [[The Anorexic Control Spiral]] and the [[Self-Soothing Homeostasis Trap]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cultural Alienation]], [[The Anorexic Control Spiral]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Depression]], [[Dissociation]], [[Self-Harm]], [[Anorexia]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavioral engineering, [[Emotional Repression]] acts primarily as an **Automatic Motivation** driver. It creates an unconscious urgency to numb emotional pain and sustain physical deprivation or harm to avoid confronting underlying trauma. 
- **Leverage Points:** Addressing this requires introducing somatic experiencing and emotional-awareness protocols to gradually build the psychological capability to tolerate discomfort, disrupting the reliance on repression without overwhelming the nervous system.
