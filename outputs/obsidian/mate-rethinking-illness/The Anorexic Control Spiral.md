# The Anorexic Control Spiral

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where a chaotic environment strips individual agency, driving extreme bodily control strategies that further sever emotional connection and necessitate stricter deprivation.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A chaotic environment forces an individual into [[Emotional Repression]], which strips away a sense of identity, self-worth, and agency. This profound lack of agency catalyzes [[Anorexia]] as a desperate attempt to seize bodily control. While the disorder temporarily restores a false sense of control, the strict physical deprivation and social isolation further sever the individual's connection to their true feelings. This increases [[Emotional Repression]] and necessitates even stricter adherence to [[Anorexia]] to maintain the fragile illusion of control.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Emotional Repression]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Anorexia]], [[Emotional Repression]] (Reinforced)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop underpins the behavior of **Disordered Eating (Anorexic restriction and meticulous food tracking)**.
- **Capability:** Characterized by a low psychological capacity to tolerate ambient emotional chaos, paradoxically coupled with extremely high cognitive discipline utilized for meticulous tracking and restriction.
- **Opportunity:** Fostered in emotionally unstable or boundary-lacking environments where [[Cultural Alienation]] validates body-centric perfectionism.
- **Motivation:** Driven by a desperate, reflective need to seize agency and establish a sense of control, paired with an automatic drive to numb emotional pain through starvation.
- **Leverage Points:** Restructure the individual's social ecosystem to provide healthy, non-body-centric domains of control and agency (e.g., leadership roles, creative autonomy) to safely satisfy the reflective motivation for control.
