# Biological Determinism

**Definition:** A reductionist medical paradigm and cultural narrative that attributes complex mental distress primarily to DNA-dictated chemical imbalances, effectively obscuring underlying trauma and absolving society of responsibility.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Biological Determinism]] serves as the dominant ideological framework in modern psychiatry, driving the systemic over-reliance on the [[Psychiatric Diagnosis]] construct. By framing distress as an innate biological defect, it actively diverts attention away from systemic failures and historical trauma.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** A societal and clinical need to absolve guilt regarding [[Childhood Adversity]], as well as systemic reliance on the [[Psychiatric Diagnosis]] for medical billing.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases the assignment of a [[Psychiatric Diagnosis]] while simultaneously inhibiting the exploration and repair of [[Relational Connectedness]]. 

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In clinical settings, [[Biological Determinism]] functions as a powerful **Motivation** driver. It provides reflective motivation for both doctors and patients to bypass the "enormous work" of trauma recovery, offering a simple, time-efficient narrative that absolves all parties of guilt. 
- **Leverage Point:** Institute clinical intake architectures that mandate comprehensive screening for [[Childhood Adversity]] and relational history prior to applying any [[Psychiatric Diagnosis]], effectively breaking the automatic default to biological/pharmacological explanations.
