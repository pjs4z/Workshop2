# Non-Neediness

**Definition:** A psychological baseline where an individual places a higher priority on their own self-perception than on the perceptions and validation of others, manifesting as authentic, uninhibited behavior.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Non-Neediness]] acts as the central node for authentic attraction, fundamentally opposing both [[Neediness]] and [[Narcissism]]. When an individual lowers their [[Emotional Investment]] in the validation of others, they naturally increase their [[Non-Neediness]]. This state is fundamentally fueled by [[Vulnerability]] and the alignment of one's environment through [[Honest Living]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Vulnerability]], [[Honest Living]], successfully navigating [[The Pain Period]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Polarization]], [[Honest Action]], [[Honest Communication]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
As a core Capability driver, [[Non-Neediness]] is the prerequisite for initiating both [[Honest Action]] and [[Polarization]]. It is engineered not by avoiding fear, but by building psychological capacity through incremental social exposure during [[The Pain Period]]. This strips away the motivation for external validation and mitigates the risk of [[Creepiness]].
