# Behavioral Activation

**Definition:** An intervention aimed at combating depression by progressively scheduling and engaging in values-based activities that are inherently rewarding or important, thereby breaking the cycle of withdrawal and low mood.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Behavioral Activation]] directly combats the "Depressive Withdrawal Loop." When individuals withdraw from life, they are deprived of rewarding experiences, which validates negative [[Core Belief]]s. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Decreased by [[Major Depressive Disorder]]; catalyzed heavily by [[Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreases [[Major Depressive Disorder]] and naturally strengthens the [[Social Support Network]] by forcing interpersonal engagement.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Depression severely limits *Capability* (low energy, poor concentration) and provides strong *Automatic Motivation* to rest and withdraw for short-term relief. 
- **Leverage Points:** To engineer [[Behavioral Activation]], one must bypass low motivation using "Mise en place" task breakdown—dividing overwhelming tasks into highly specific, micro-sub-tasks. 
- **Opportunity:** Engineered via strict calendar scheduling (Activity Scheduling) to assign specific days and times to valued activities, entirely removing in-the-moment decision fatigue.
