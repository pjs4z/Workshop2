# Behavioral Avoidance

**Definition:** The process of escaping or staying away from anxiety-provoking stimuli, which provides short-term relief but negatively reinforces the behavior and perpetuates long-term fear.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Behavioral Avoidance]] is the central engine of the "Avoidance Spiral." By running away from distress, the individual experiences immediate relief, but loses the chance to encounter disconfirming evidence about the feared situation. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Anxiety Disorder]], [[Panic Attack]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Core Belief]] (strengthens and solidifies maladaptive schemas)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This behavior is driven by a powerful *Automatic Motivation* to escape perceived threats and alleviate immediate discomfort. To overcome [[Behavioral Avoidance]], interventions must target the individual's *Capability* to tolerate [[Sympathetic Nervous System Arousal]] and remove the *Opportunity* to flee. A prime leverage point is the engineering of social accountability (e.g., sharing plans with a [[Social Support Network]]) to increase the social cost of avoidance, making [[Exposure Therapy]] the path of least resistance.
