# Cognitive Distortion

**Definition:** Biased, inaccurate, or irrational thought events that negatively misinterpret reality, significantly contributing to the initiation and maintenance of anxiety and depression.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A [[Cognitive Distortion]] is the real-time manifestation of a negative schema and plays a central role in the "Panic-Arousal Spiral." For instance, catastrophizing a normal bodily sensation immediately triggers physiological fear.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Core Belief]], [[Panic Attack]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Sympathetic Nervous System Arousal]] and maintains [[Anxiety Disorder]] and [[Major Depressive Disorder]]. Decreased by the practice of [[Mindfulness]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Distortions operate automatically, requiring specialized *Capability* (metacognitive awareness) to catch oneself in the act of biased thinking. 
- **Opportunity:** Created through real-time thought logging—mandating the use of structured CBT forms immediately following a mood dip to capture the [[Cognitive Distortion]] before memory biases alter it. 
- **Leverage Points:** Integrating [[Mindfulness]] allows the individual to observe intrusive thoughts as mental events rather than absolute truths, short-circuiting the "What if?" loops that drive anxiety.
