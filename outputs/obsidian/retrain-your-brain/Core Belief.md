# Core Belief

**Definition:** A deeply ingrained, foundational assumption or schema about oneself, others, or the world that automatically generates and reinforces specific maladaptive thoughts and emotional responses.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A [[Core Belief]] serves as the gravitational center for both anxiety and depressive feedback loops. Because the belief acts as a filter, positive evidence is discarded, and ambiguous evidence is interpreted as catastrophic.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increased/reinforced by [[Behavioral Avoidance]] and the illusion of protection from a [[Safety Behavior]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases the frequency and severity of a [[Cognitive Distortion]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Maladaptive [[Core Belief]]s exert intense *Automatic Motivation*, pulling the mind toward negative self-evaluation ("I am a failure") or threat overestimation ("The world is dangerous"). 
- **Leverage Points:** Directly attacking a [[Core Belief]] requires building metacognitive *Capability*. One highly effective intervention is the "Double Standard" technique: asking, "What would I tell someone I love if they had this thought?" This bypasses the automatic harsh self-judgment, creating an *Opportunity* for cognitive restructuring.
