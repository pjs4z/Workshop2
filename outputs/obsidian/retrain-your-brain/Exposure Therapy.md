# Exposure Therapy

**Definition:** A systematic therapeutic process of intentionally and progressively confronting feared stimuli or situations without employing safety behaviors, facilitating new learning and the eventual reduction of anxiety.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Exposure Therapy]] acts as a critical balancing loop (Exposure Habituation) against the self-reinforcing cycles of anxiety. By intentionally provoking [[Sympathetic Nervous System Arousal]] while strictly inhibiting [[Behavioral Avoidance]] and any [[Safety Behavior]], the brain habituates to the stimulus. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy]] (catalyzes the process)
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreases [[Behavioral Avoidance]], [[Safety Behavior]], and maladaptive [[Core Belief]]s. 
*Note: A specialized sub-type involves [[Interoceptive Exposure]] to deliberately induce physiological sensations like dizziness or racing heart to treat panic.*

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Engaging in [[Exposure Therapy]] requires significant *Reflective Motivation*—the conscious decision (courage) that achieving life goals is more important than avoiding fear. 
- **Capability:** Requires psychological and physiological tolerance of arousal. 
- **Opportunity:** Engineered by constructing a progressive exposure hierarchy, providing a structured, step-by-step environment to face fears safely. 
- **Leverage Points:** A critical intervention is deliberately eliminating "magic feathers" (i.e., dropping a [[Safety Behavior]]) during exposure to forcefully disconfirm catastrophic predictions.
