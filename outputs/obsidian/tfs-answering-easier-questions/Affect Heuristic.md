# Affect Heuristic

**Definition:** A cognitive heuristic in which an individual's preexisting emotional likes and dislikes determine their factual beliefs, conclusions, and assessments of risks and benefits.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Affect Heuristic]] flips the standard model of rational deduction; it forces conclusions to dictate arguments. It acts as the core engine of the **Affective Coherence Loop**, establishing an emotional preference that catalyzes [[WYSIATI]] (What You See Is All There Is). 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increases in [[WYSIATI]] (which reinforces the emotional bias by suppressing contradictory evidence)
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes [[WYSIATI]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Affect Heuristic]] operates as a primary **Motivation** driver for heuristic decision-making. People are motivated to maintain emotional coherence.
- **Leverage Points:** Mitigating the [[Affect Heuristic]] requires breaking the feedback loop of emotional confirmation. Interventions include forcing explicit risk/benefit tradeoff exercises, or utilizing standardized, emotion-blind rubrics that prevent [[Intensity Matching]] from quietly translating a "dislike" into a "high-risk/low-benefit" factual assessment.
