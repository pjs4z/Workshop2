# Intensity Matching

**Definition:** An automatic capability of System 1 to translate and map responses across different quantitative or qualitative scales, such as equating the intensity of an emotion to a specific dollar amount.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Once the [[Mental Shotgun]] produces an answer to a [[Heuristic Question]], that answer is rarely in the correct format for the original [[Target Question]]. [[Intensity Matching]] is the translation mechanism. It allows [[System 1]] to intuitively align the subjective weight of an emotion (like anger or affection) to a corresponding value on a completely different scale (like dollars, prison sentences, or political success). 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Substitution]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Intensity Matching]] provides the **Capability** for seamless heuristic jumping. 
- **Leverage Points:** To short-circuit [[System 1]]'s ability to use [[Intensity Matching]] to map emotional states onto factual or quantitative judgments, engineers can target **Opportunity/Motivation** by decoupling emotion from the task. Implementing blind evaluations or standardized scoring rubrics breaks the subjective mapping process, preventing the [[Affect Heuristic]] or [[Mood Heuristic]] from dominating the decision.
