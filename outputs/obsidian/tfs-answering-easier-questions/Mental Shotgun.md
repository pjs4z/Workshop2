# Mental Shotgun

**Definition:** An automatic cognitive process characterized by imprecise control over targeted responses, causing the mind to compute more information than is strictly intended or required.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Mental Shotgun]] is deployed automatically when the brain attempts to process information. Because [[System 1]] cannot precisely aim its computational power, it inevitably activates a wide array of associations, generating off-the-shelf answers that form the basis of a [[Heuristic Question]]. This excessive computation is a primary catalyst in the [[Cognitive Least Effort Loop]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases the availability of a [[Heuristic Question]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Mental Shotgun]] acts as a deep-seated **Capability** driver for Cognitive [[Substitution]]. 
- **Leverage Points:** Because we cannot turn off the [[Mental Shotgun]], behavioral interventions must focus on **Opportunity**. Restructuring the environment—such as altering the sequence of information or breaking a complex [[Target Question]] down into objective sub-queries—prevents priming effects and starves the [[Mental Shotgun]] of easily accessible, biasing contextual cues.
