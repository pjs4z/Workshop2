# Substitution

**Definition:** The automatic cognitive operation of answering a simpler, related question in place of a difficult or computationally demanding target question.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Substitution]] is the core mechanism by which [[System 1]] handles overwhelming computational demands. When faced with a complex [[Target Question]], the mind utilizes the [[Mental Shotgun]] and [[Intensity Matching]] to quickly generate an answer to a simpler [[Heuristic Question]]. Temporal variations of this process include the [[3-D Heuristic]] and [[Mood Heuristic]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Target Question]], [[Heuristic Question]], [[Intensity Matching]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[System 2]] (bypassing critical analysis)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavioral design, [[Substitution]] is the target behavior representing a failure of critical reasoning. 
- **Opportunity Drivers:** Environmental structures that present complex a [[Target Question]] alongside easily accessible but misleading cues (like the [[3-D Heuristic]]).
- **Motivation Drivers:** The inherent laziness of [[System 2]], driven by the desire for Cognitive Ease.
- **Leverage Points:** To prevent unwanted [[Substitution]], introduce deliberate "cognitive friction" (e.g., disfluent formatting or mandatory justification steps). This disrupts cognitive ease and forces [[System 2]] to transition from a lazy apologist to an active critic, effectively inhibiting the heuristic shortcut.
