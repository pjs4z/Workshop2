# Associative Activation

**Definition:** A rapid, mostly unconscious process where an evoked idea triggers a spreading cascade of electrical and associative activity in the brain, activating multiple related concepts simultaneously across the semantic network.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Associative Activation]] functions as the core kinetic mechanism of [[System 1]]. When a stimulus is encountered, it acts upon nodes within [[Associative Memory]], causing a ripple effect of related thoughts, memories, and physiological states. This process is highly sensitive to the [[Priming Effect]] and forms the foundation for downstream behavioral changes.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]], [[Priming Effect]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Ideomotor Effect]], [[Associative Coherence]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the COM-B framework, this acts as a powerful, unconscious **Motivation** driver. Because the activation happens automatically, behavioral engineers can manipulate the environment to trigger specific associative cascades. 
- **Leverage Point:** By carefully managing environmental cues (e.g., removing money-themed imagery in collaborative spaces), you can prevent the [[Associative Activation]] of individualism, thereby naturally maintaining baseline prosocial behavior and helpfulness.
- **Leverage Point:** Embed subtle primes aligned with task goals (e.g., academic or financial motifs) to unconsciously drive self-reliance and increase the time individuals spend persevering on difficult tasks.
