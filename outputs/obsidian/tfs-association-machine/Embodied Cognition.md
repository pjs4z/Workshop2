# Embodied Cognition

**Definition:** The principle that the physical body actively participates in the interpretation of events, meaning thinking and emotional processing are enacted through physiological and bodily responses rather than isolated entirely within the brain.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Embodied Cognition]] provides the biological hardware and architectural constraints that make phenomena like the [[Ideomotor Effect]] and [[Reciprocal Priming]] possible. Because the brain and body act as a coupled system, physical expressions (smiling, nodding) are inextricably linked to the semantic meaning of those expressions within [[Associative Memory]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Biological and evolutionary physiological structures
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Reciprocal Priming]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Acts as a fundamental **Capability** driver. It explains *how* individuals possess the physical-to-psychological hardware necessary for affective state shifts.
- **Leverage Point:** Implement somatic interventions in high-stress environments. For instance, requiring users to hold a posture or perform a facial expression (like the "pencil-in-mouth" exercise to force a smile) instantly initiates a self-reinforcing cascade of [[Associative Coherence]] that measurably improves subjective mood and message acceptance.
