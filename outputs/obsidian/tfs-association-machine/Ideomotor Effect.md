# Ideomotor Effect

**Definition:** A specific subset of priming wherein an unconsciously activated concept or idea directly influences and alters physical actions and physiological states without conscious awareness or intent.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Ideomotor Effect]] acts as the critical bridge between mental representation and physical execution. It takes cognitive inputs from [[Associative Activation]] and translates them into bodily movements (e.g., walking slower, frowning, or physical cleansing impulses seen in [[The Lady Macbeth Effect]]). Once the physical action is initiated, it feeds back into the mind via [[Reciprocal Priming]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Associative Activation]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Reciprocal Priming]], [[The Lady Macbeth Effect]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This effect provides the unconscious **Motivation** and physical execution pathway for primed behaviors. 
- **Leverage Point:** Target this effect to drive product consumption by juxtaposing digital or retail environments with specific media contexts. For example, presenting ads for soap or mouthwash immediately following news of moral violations directly capitalizes on the specific physical cleansing impulse of [[The Lady Macbeth Effect]].
- **Leverage Point:** Design physical protocols or user interfaces that require specific physical movements (e.g., vertical scrolling to simulate nodding) to leverage the physical-to-mental loop and unconsciously increase user acceptance of presented information.
