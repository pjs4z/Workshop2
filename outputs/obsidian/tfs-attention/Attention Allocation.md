# Attention Allocation

**Definition:** The sophisticated, evolutionarily honed process by which the brain selectively and precisely distributes limited mental capacity, protecting the most critical activities while assigning spare capacity to secondary tasks.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Attention Allocation]] is a protective occurrent mechanism deployed during cognitive overload. It is the core driver in the **Selective Overload Protection** feedback loop. When a [[Task Set]] demands extreme [[Mental Effort]], [[Attention Allocation]] caps systemic load by inducing [[Inattentional Blindness]] to secondary external stimuli. This prevents new information from entering [[Working Memory]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Mental Effort]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Inattentional Blindness]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This process is the primary Capability driver for the target behavior of maintaining selective attention and resisting external distractions.
- **Capability:** Automatically deployed to protect the primary task, but vulnerable if the environment is overly chaotic.
- **Leverage Points:** Modify Opportunity (Stimulus Control). Design work environments with minimal visual and auditory clutter during high-demand cognitive tasks. This reduces the systemic burden on [[Attention Allocation]] to actively filter out irrelevant stimuli, preserving capacity for the primary task.
