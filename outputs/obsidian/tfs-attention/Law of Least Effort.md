# Law of Least Effort

**Definition:** A general principle asserting that when multiple ways exist to achieve the same goal, individuals will naturally gravitate to the least cognitively or physically demanding course of action.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Law of Least Effort]] serves as a critical balancing feedback mechanism in the **Economy of Cognitive Action**. As [[System 2]] applies [[Executive Control]] to maintain a [[Task Set]], [[Mental Effort]] escalates. Once [[Mental Effort]] reaches an intolerable peak, the [[Law of Least Effort]] forces the system to find a less demanding path, automate the task, or abandon it entirely, thereby decreasing [[Mental Effort]] and restoring cognitive homeostasis.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** High [[Mental Effort]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreases [[Mental Effort]], shifts control to [[System 1]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This law is the fundamental baseline of human Motivation. It dictates that behaviors requiring high cognitive exertion will extinguish over time.
- **Motivation:** Acts as a constant negative driver against sustained [[System 2]] engagement.
- **Leverage Points:** Do not fight the law; design for it. Modify Opportunity by restructuring complex tasks into multiple, sequential, low-friction steps. Removing artificial time pressure also prevents forced "mental sprints," aligning the task with the user's natural drive for cognitive economy.
