# Mental Effort

**Definition:** The active investment of limited cognitive resources required to perform complex mental operations, characterized by a rapid buildup during demanding tasks and a gradual relaxation as short-term memory unloads.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Mental Effort]] is a dynamic process (Occurrent) that fluctuates based on the demands placed on [[Working Memory]]. It participates heavily in the **Memory Decay and Rehearsal Cycle** loop: as a [[Task Set]] is established, time pressure causes memory to decay, forcing continuous rehearsal that exponentially increases [[Mental Effort]]. Cross-domain, this effort directly increases [[Pupillary Dilation]] (a biological index). When [[Mental Effort]] reaches its peak, it triggers [[Attention Allocation]] and the [[Law of Least Effort]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 2]], [[Working Memory]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Pupillary Dilation]], [[Attention Allocation]], [[Inattentional Blindness]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
High [[Mental Effort]] acts as a severe motivational friction point.
- **Motivation:** Because humans are wired to avoid sustained [[Mental Effort]], interventions requiring it often fail unless intrinsic or extrinsic reflective drives are extremely high.
- **Leverage Points:** Modify Capability via Training. Increase task familiarity through repeated practice to automate cognitive operations. This transfers the cognitive load from [[System 2]] to [[System 1]], permanently reducing the [[Mental Effort]] required for the behavior.
