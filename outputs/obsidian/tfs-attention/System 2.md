# System 2

**Definition:** The deliberate, effortful, and inherently lazy cognitive system capable of following rules, comparing objects, making deliberate choices, and overriding the impulses of [[System 1]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 2]] acts as the central governor of conscious thought, requiring the [[Prefrontal Area]] to execute its functions. It actively inhibits the automatic responses of [[System 1]] and catalyzes [[Mental Effort]] by using [[Executive Control]] to establish and maintain a [[Task Set]]. Because its capacity is strictly limited, it is deeply governed by the [[Law of Least Effort]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Prefrontal Area]], [[Executive Control]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Mental Effort]], inhibits [[System 1]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of sustaining complex cognitive tasks, [[System 2]] engagement is the primary target behavior. 
- **Capability:** Relies heavily on the capacity of [[Working Memory]]. 
- **Motivation:** Inherently fights against the [[Law of Least Effort]], which constantly seeks to minimize cognitive exertion.
- **Leverage Points:** To optimize [[System 2]] function, modify the Opportunity environment: externalize [[Working Memory]] demands by breaking complex tasks into sequential, easy steps, and use physical tools (like paper) to prevent cognitive overload.
