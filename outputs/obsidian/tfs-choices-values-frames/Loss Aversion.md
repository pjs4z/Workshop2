# Loss Aversion

**Definition:** The psychophysical property wherein the subjective severity of a loss is experienced as significantly greater than the subjective attractiveness of an objectively equivalent gain.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Loss Aversion]] is a direct structural consequence of the steepness of the [[Value Function]] in the negative domain. It is the primary cognitive engine behind behavioral inertia and irrational attachment.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Value Function]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Endowment Effect]], [[Dead-loss Effect]], [[Status Quo Entrenchment Loop]], [[Sunk-Cost Escalation Loop]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** A severe motivational driver where the psychological pain of giving up an endowed asset or abandoning a sunk cost looms much larger than the pleasure of acquiring a new asset or cutting losses.
- **Leverage Points (Opportunity/Environment Design):** Modifying systemic default options (e.g., utilizing opt-out rather than opt-in choice architectures) ensures that the desired behavioral state inherently becomes the reference point. This effectively weaponizes [[Loss Aversion]] in favor of the desired outcome.
- **Leverage Points (Motivation Modification):** Psychologically reframe the rejection of a new alternative as a definitive "loss" of its unique advantages, artificially triggering [[Loss Aversion]] against remaining in the status quo.
