# Mental Accounting

**Definition:** The cognitive process by which individuals intuitively organize, track, and evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of economic transactions relative to specific reference states.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Mental Accounting]] explains why resources are often treated as non-fungible in the human mind. Individuals create narrow cognitive brackets to evaluate specific transactions rather than looking at total absolute assets.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed by a [[Topical Account]] (a specialized mental frame evaluating financial consequences exclusively in relation to an immediate context).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes [[Decision Value]], enables the [[Dead-loss Effect]], drives the [[Sunk-Cost Escalation Loop]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** A psychological capability mechanism. The tendency to narrowly isolate transactions into a [[Topical Account]] makes individuals vulnerable to poor aggregate decision-making (e.g., enduring continuous negative [[Experience Value]] to justify a prior unrecoverable expense).
- **Motivation:** By categorizing an expense as an ongoing cost rather than an uncompensated loss, it artificially inflates [[Decision Value]] to psychologically offset negative experiences.
- **Leverage Points:** Prompt the individual to shift from a narrow [[Topical Account]] to a comprehensive account, cognitively decoupling the past sunk cost from the immediate activity to break the accounting linkage.
