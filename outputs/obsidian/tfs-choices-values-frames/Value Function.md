# Value Function

**Definition:** An S-shaped psychophysical model mapping objective outcomes to subjective value. It is concave in the domain of gains, convex in the domain of losses, and significantly steeper for losses than for equivalent gains.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Value Function]] serves as the baseline evaluation mechanism for human decision-making under risk and uncertainty. Its asymmetrical shape dictates that objective outcomes are judged relative to a neutral reference point rather than absolute states of wealth. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Objective outcomes, Reference point assignment
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Risk Aversion]] (in the domain of gains), [[Risk Seeking]] (in the domain of losses), [[Loss Aversion]], [[Status Quo Entrenchment Loop]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Motivation:** Acts as a primary automatic motivational driver. People automatically rely on the nonlinearity of the [[Value Function]], which dictates their risk preferences depending on whether a situation is structurally framed as a gain or a loss. 
- **Leverage Points:** To shift behavior, change the assigned reference point in the environment so that the target behavior is evaluated as a gain rather than a loss, or vice versa depending on the desired risk profile.
