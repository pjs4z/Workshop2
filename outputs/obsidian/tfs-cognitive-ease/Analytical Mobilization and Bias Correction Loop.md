# Analytical Mobilization and Bias Correction Loop

**Definition:** A balancing feedback system wherein environmental or cognitive friction induces mental discomfort, triggering the brain's analytical framework to verify impressions and correct intuitive biases.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This balancing loop is the brain's primary defense mechanism against the over-application of [[System 1]] heuristics. 
- **Mechanism:** Unmet demands or dysfluent stimuli increase [[Cognitive Strain]]. High [[Cognitive Strain]] catalyzes the mobilization of [[System 2]] to transition the individual from casual thinking to an engaged, analytic mode. Once active, [[System 2]] evaluates [[System 1]]'s impressions, thereby decreasing both the [[Illusion of Truth]] and the [[Illusion of Remembering]]. This critical evaluation resolves the initial mental conflict, eventually balancing the level of [[Cognitive Strain]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Dysfluent environmental inputs, [[Cognitive Strain]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Engages [[System 2]], inhibits [[Cognitive Ease]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Understanding this loop helps behavior designers manipulate **Opportunity** (environmental legibility) to influence **Capability** (which cognitive system is active).
- **Leverage Points:** Altering the environmental context to reduce comfort (avoiding excessive positive affect or using hard-to-read text) disrupts the [[Intuition of Coherence]] and prevents the brain from defaulting to lazy, superficial processing.
