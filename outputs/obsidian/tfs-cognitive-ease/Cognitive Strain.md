# Cognitive Strain

**Definition:** A state of mental effort or discomfort triggered by unmet demands, poor legibility, or complex problems, which mobilizes analytical thinking and increases vigilance.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Cognitive Strain]] is the functional opposite of [[Cognitive Ease]]. It acts as an internal alarm bell signaling that [[System 1]]'s automatic heuristics are insufficient for the current environment. This friction awakens the brain's analytical machinery.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]] (when encountering dysfluent stimuli, complex problems, or degraded visual environments)
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes the mobilization of [[System 2]], decreases reliance on [[Associative Memory]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Cognitive Strain]] provides the **Motivation** (via a feeling of mental friction or suspicion) and necessitates the **Capability** (mental bandwidth) to mobilize analytical reasoning to detect and correct errors.
- **Leverage Points:** To force individuals out of a casual intuitive mode, introduce "desirable friction." Using barely legible fonts or complex presentation formats artificially induces [[Cognitive Strain]], which forces the engagement of [[System 2]] and effectively reduces intuitive errors like the [[Illusion of Truth]].
