# Illusion of Truth

**Definition:** A cognitive bias where falsehoods are accepted as factual because factors like repetition, highly legible fonts, or simple language create a sense of cognitive ease indistinguishable from factual validity.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Illusion of Truth]] is a direct byproduct of the brain misinterpreting processing fluency as historical accuracy. It plays a critical role in the *Familiarity-Truth Illusion Loop*, where believed information becomes further integrated into [[Associative Memory]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cognitive Ease]], [[Mere Exposure Effect]], [[System 1]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreased by [[System 2]] (when engaged)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This phenomenon outlines the target behavior of *accepting a message without critical verification*. 
- **Opportunity:** Exploits high-contrast visual environments, repetitive exposure, and simplified language.
- **Capability:** Relies on low [[System 2]] engagement and high [[System 1]] automatic processing.
- **Leverage Points:** Marketers and communicators can repeatedly expose audiences to a core message to trigger the [[Mere Exposure Effect]], seamlessly converting novel or even false information into a feeling of familiar truth.
