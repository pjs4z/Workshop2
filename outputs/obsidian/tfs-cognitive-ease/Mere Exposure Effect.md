# Mere Exposure Effect

**Definition:** An adaptive psychological phenomenon where repeated exposure to an arbitrary, non-threatening stimulus inherently increases an organism's preference and affection for it.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Mere Exposure Effect]] operates entirely beneath conscious awareness. It conditions [[System 1]] to treat previously encountered, non-harmful stimuli as "safety signals," which fundamentally rewires affective responses.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Environmental repetition, Unconscious priming
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Cognitive Ease]], Increases [[Illusion of Truth]], Increases [[Illusion of Remembering]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
As an unconscious driver of **Motivation**, the [[Mere Exposure Effect]] automatically generates preference without requiring rational justification or conscious memory of the exposure. 
- **Leverage Points:** Frequent repetition of brand names, visuals, or statements—even if masked or presented too quickly for conscious processing—will reliably induce [[Cognitive Ease]] and foster unearned trust or liking in the target audience.
