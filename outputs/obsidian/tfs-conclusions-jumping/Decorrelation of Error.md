# Decorrelation of Error

**Definition:** A procedural intervention designed to aggregate collective intelligence by ensuring individual judgments remain isolated from one another, thereby preventing shared biases and allowing localized errors to average out.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Decorrelation of Error]] is the primary regulatory mechanism within the *Institutional Error Mitigation* feedback loop. By isolating individuals, it prevents the interpersonal transmission of cognitive biases.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Institutional protocols, [[System 2]] deliberation at a systemic or organizational level.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Actively inhibits the [[Halo Effect]] and [[Confirmation Bias]] across cross-domain boundaries, subsequently reducing group [[Overconfidence]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
It functions as a structural **Opportunity** driver that promotes the target behavior of *Independent Evidence Evaluation*. 
- **Leverage Points:** Implement mandatory asynchronous judgment gathering (e.g., forcing committee members to write down their positions prior to open discussion). This prevents the social transmission of [[Confirmation Bias]] and stops early, assertive speakers from dictating group consensus.
