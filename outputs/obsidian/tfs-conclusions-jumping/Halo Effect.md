# Halo Effect

**Definition:** A cognitive bias where an initial emotional response or observed trait fundamentally shapes and dictates the interpretation of a subject's subsequent or completely unobserved characteristics.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Halo Effect]] is a central component of the *Halo Integration Loop*. An initial judgment establishes a stock of this bias, which then filters subsequent information to perfectly align with the first impression.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Sequential presentation of information (initial emotional responses).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Confirmation Bias]], which in turn catalyzes the [[Suppression of Doubt]]. Actively inhibited by [[Decorrelation of Error]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Acts as an automatic **Motivation** driver for jumping to conclusions, propelling the mind toward exaggerated emotional coherence and cognitive ease.
- **Leverage Points:** Physically disrupt the **Opportunity** for the [[Halo Effect]] to take hold by isolating sequential tasks (e.g., grading exams by question rather than by student). This breaks the associative chain and forces independent evaluation.
