# Suppression of Doubt

**Definition:** The automatic cognitive process by which ambiguous information is resolved into a single, definite interpretation, functioning without retaining or tracking rejected alternative meanings.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This occurrent process smooths over gaps in perception to maintain cognitive ease. It operates continuously within the *Coherence-Confidence Reinforcement* loop until interrupted by effortful cognition.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed by [[System 1]] and increased by [[Confirmation Bias]]. It is actively inhibited by the effortful process of [[Unbelieving]] executed by [[System 2]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Directly increases [[Overconfidence]] by preventing alternative interpretations from surfacing.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Suppression of Doubt]] serves as an automatic **Motivation** driver (yielding the internal reward of cognitive ease) that encourages jumping to conclusions.
- **Leverage Points:** To combat this process, one must trigger the balancing *Cognitive Intervention Loop* by activating [[System 2]] to initiate [[Unbelieving]], which demands higher working memory capability and a tolerance for subjective discomfort.
