# System 1

**Definition:** An automatic, fast-acting cognitive system that efficiently jumps to conclusions, resolves ambiguity without conscious awareness, and constructs coherent interpretations from limited available information.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 1]] acts as the foundational engine for rapid decision-making, trading accuracy for cognitive ease. It inherently relies on [[WYSIATI]] to construct coherent narratives and avoid the subjective discomfort of uncertainty. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Ambiguous environmental inputs, high-speed information processing demands.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes the [[Suppression of Doubt]], increases [[Confirmation Bias]], and serves as the primary driver in the closed *Coherence-Confidence Reinforcement* loop.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of the target behavior *Jumping to conclusions*, [[System 1]] acts as a primary **Capability** driver. It processes associations at high speeds without consciously tracking rejected alternatives. Its influence is particularly dominant when [[System 2]] is depleted, lazy, or busy.
- **Leverage Points:** Modify the Opportunity landscape by restructuring the presentation of information (e.g., implementing [[Decorrelation of Error]]) or protect [[System 2]] bandwidth to ensure individuals have the cognitive capacity for deliberate [[Unbelieving]].
