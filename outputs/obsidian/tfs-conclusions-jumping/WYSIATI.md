# WYSIATI

**Definition:** An essential design feature of [[System 1]] characterized by radical insensitivity to the quality or quantity of missing evidence, wherein judgments are made solely based on currently activated ideas to quickly achieve a coherent narrative.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[WYSIATI]] is the structural limitation that enables [[System 1]] to function rapidly. By completely ignoring missing data, it bypasses the need for complex evidence evaluation and active doubt.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Overconfidence]], catalyzes [[Framing Effects]], and catalyzes [[Base-rate Neglect]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[WYSIATI]] functions as a severe **Capability** constraint, locking the mind into relying only on activated ideas. This structurally drives the rapid, biased decision-making associated with jumping to conclusions.
- **Leverage Points:** Overcome [[WYSIATI]] by modifying the **Opportunity** environment: present alternative frames (e.g., survival rates vs. mortality rates) or statistical base rates simultaneously rather than sequentially, forcing [[System 2]] to reconcile the complete picture.
