# Experienced Well-Being

**Definition:** The moment-by-moment emotional quality of an individual's life as it dynamically unfolds over time. It fluctuates based on immediate activities, social interactions, and attention, operating independently from overall [[Life Evaluation]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Experienced Well-Being]] operates as a real-time gauge of emotional state, continuously monitored by the [[Experiencing Self]]. It is highly sensitive to [[Focal Attention]] and psychological absorption states like [[Flow]]. Interestingly, while climbing toward the [[Income Satiation Threshold]] improves this metric, exceeding the threshold can artificially cap it (via the Hedonic Satiation Loop), as individuals lose the ability to savor small pleasures. At a population scale, increasing this well-being naturally forces a reduction in the [[U-index]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Experiencing Self]], [[Focal Attention]], [[Flow]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[U-index]] (decreases)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of motivation, the inherent dopaminergic drive toward [[Experienced Well-Being]] functions as an Automatic Motivation driver for behaviors like socializing and active leisure. To engineer environments that protect this state, leverage points include friction-based environmental design (e.g., tech-free zones) that forcefully eliminates multitasking triggers and sustains single-task engagement.
