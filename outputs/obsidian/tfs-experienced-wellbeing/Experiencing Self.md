# Experiencing Self

**Definition:** The aspect of human cognition that lives in the present moment and directly undergoes emotional states in real-time. It is evaluated by measuring momentary affective states rather than overarching retrospective judgments.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Experiencing Self]] is the operational engine behind [[Experienced Well-Being]]. Unlike the [[Remembering Self]], which compiles narratives to formulate a [[Life Evaluation]], the [[Experiencing Self]] is strictly bound to the present reality. When coupled with [[Focal Attention]], it frequently enters a state of [[Flow]], locking into the Cognitive Absorption Loop that continually reinforces present-moment awareness and spikes daily positive affect.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Human Cognition
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Experienced Well-Being]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Experiencing Self]] represents a core Psychological Capability for executing Single-Task Sustained Engagement. It requires the cognitive bandwidth to remain anchored in present sensory and emotional inputs without distraction. Enhancing this capability relies heavily on designing physical and digital environments that minimize abrupt situational stressors (like sudden noises or digital notifications) to protect the immediate experiential state.
