# Income Satiation Threshold

**Definition:** A specific socioeconomic boundary beyond which further increases in wealth yield no additional improvement in experienced well-being. Earning beyond this level increases life evaluation but may reduce an individual's ability to savor smaller, everyday pleasures.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Income Satiation Threshold]] acts as a critical bifurcation point between the [[Experiencing Self]] and the [[Remembering Self]]. It anchors the Hedonic Satiation Loop: as individuals accumulate wealth past this threshold, it reliably increases the [[Life Evaluation]] generated by the [[Remembering Self]]. Concurrently, it inhibits [[Focal Attention]] toward small, everyday pleasures, effectively capping and preventing any further increases in [[Experienced Well-Being]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Wealth Accumulation
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Life Evaluation]] (increases), [[Experienced Well-Being]] (inhibits further growth)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Achieving the [[Income Satiation Threshold]] provides the physical and financial Opportunity (COM-B) necessary for Active Daily Time Reallocation—affording individuals the flexibility to alter work hours or outsource high [[U-index]] chores. However, to counteract the negative side effects of this threshold (the loss of ability to savor small pleasures), individuals require deliberate Reflective Motivation to manually force Single-Task Sustained Engagement, utilizing friction-based environmental designs to artificially protect their [[Focal Attention]].
