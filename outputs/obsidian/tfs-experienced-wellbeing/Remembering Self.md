# Remembering Self

**Definition:** The aspect of human cognition responsible for retrospectively evaluating one's life as a whole. It relies on overarching narratives and memories rather than the continuous reality of moment-to-moment experiences.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Remembering Self]] is strictly responsible for generating an individual's overarching [[Life Evaluation]]. It is fundamentally disconnected from the daily emotional reality felt by the [[Experiencing Self]]. Wealth accumulation past the [[Income Satiation Threshold]] strongly satisfies the [[Remembering Self]], even though it does little to nothing to improve the day-to-day [[Experienced Well-Being]] of the individual.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Income Satiation Threshold]] (increases status/narrative evaluation)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Life Evaluation]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Remembering Self]] acts as a primary Capability and Reflective Motivation driver for Active Daily Time Reallocation. Because the [[Remembering Self]] can retrospectively review a day and identify sources of distress (often via tools like the [[Day Reconstruction Method]]), it enables the planning and restructuring of future schedules. Interventions should focus on providing individuals with clear visualizations of their personal [[U-index]] to bridge the gap between present pain and future schedule optimization.
