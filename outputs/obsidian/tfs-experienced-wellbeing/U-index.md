# U-index

**Definition:** An objective, time-based statistical metric representing the percentage of time a population or individual spends in a predominantly unpleasant emotional state. It is utilized to quantify societal suffering and optimize public policy.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[U-index]] serves as the primary gauge in the Societal Suffering Reduction Loop. It is calculated utilizing the [[Day Reconstruction Method]], which aggregates the emotional distress of the [[Experiencing Self]] across populations. A high [[U-index]] highlights systemic friction points (such as commutes or lack of child care) and ideally catalyzes social policy interventions. When these interventions succeed, they increase overall [[Experienced Well-Being]], inherently driving the [[U-index]] down.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Day Reconstruction Method]] (measurement), [[Experienced Well-Being]] (decreases)
- **Downstream (Drives):** Societal and Policy Interventions

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Minimizing an individual's [[U-index]] is the core Reflective Motivation behind Active Daily Time Reallocation. People consciously aim to reduce time spent in suffering. Leverage points include systemic choice architecture—where corporate and public policy environments (e.g., subsidized child care, flexible work) passively reduce the friction of engaging in low [[U-index]] activities, bypassing the need for individuals to flawlessly optimize their own schedules.
