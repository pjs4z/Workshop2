# Hindsight Bias

**Definition:** The inability to accurately reconstruct past states of knowledge, leading individuals to underestimate their surprise at an event and falsely believe they predicted it beforehand.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
`[[Hindsight Bias]]` is the result of a memory replacement process. The occurrent of `[[Substitution]]` overwrites prior uncertainty with current knowledge once an outcome is revealed. This makes the past look highly predictable and knowable, acting as a crucial component of `[[The Retrospective Validation Cycle]]`.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Substitution]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Outcome Bias]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
`[[Hindsight Bias]]` functions as an automatic **Motivation** to penalize decision-makers for unforeseeable bad outcomes, under the false pretense that the risk was obvious.
- **Leverage Point:** Modify **Opportunity/Capability** by instituting a mandatory "Decision Journal" or pre-mortem protocol. Requiring decision-makers to document available information, assumed probabilities, and unknown variables exactly at the moment of the decision creates a physical anchor that blocks the psychological occurrence of `[[Substitution]]` and prevents `[[Hindsight Bias]]`.
