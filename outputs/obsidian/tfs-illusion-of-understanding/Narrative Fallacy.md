# Narrative Fallacy

**Definition:** A cognitive disposition to construct simple, coherent, and concrete causal stories of the past that exaggerate the role of talent, intentions, and skill while heavily minimizing the role of luck.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The `[[Narrative Fallacy]]` is a central artifact of human cognition, deeply embedded within `[[The Narrative Coherence Engine]]`. It acts as the structured output of our automatic `[[Sense-making]]` processes when confronted with random or complex historical data. It is strongly reinforced by the `[[Halo Effect]]`, which artificially aligns an entity's traits to match the valence of a known outcome.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Sense-making]]`, `[[Halo Effect]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Illusion of Understanding]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In organizational behavior, the `[[Narrative Fallacy]]` acts as a powerful **Motivation** driver. It fuels the reflective need for comfort and the reassuring message that actions have appropriate, predictable consequences. This drives executives to blindly adopt "recipes for success" from outlier companies. 
- **Leverage Point:** Redesign strategy reviews to artificially supply unseen information. Mandating the presentation of "Base Rates" and "Control Groups" (e.g., analyzing firms that adopted the exact same management practices but failed) breaks the coherent narrative and directly counteracts the `[[WYSIATI]]` heuristic that makes the fallacy possible.
