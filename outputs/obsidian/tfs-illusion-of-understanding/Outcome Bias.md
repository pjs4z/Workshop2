# Outcome Bias

**Definition:** The tendency to evaluate the quality of a decision based on its eventual outcome—whether good or bad—rather than assessing the soundness of the decision-making process at the time it was made.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Operating hand-in-hand with `[[Hindsight Bias]]`, `[[Outcome Bias]]` projects the valence of a result backward onto the actor. If a wild gamble succeeds, the outcome bias naturally catalyzes the `[[Halo Effect]]`, branding a reckless leader as a "visionary." 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Hindsight Bias]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Halo Effect]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
`[[Outcome Bias]]` is deeply embedded in the social and legal **Opportunity** environment (e.g., malpractice litigation, corporate performance reviews). It heavily penalizes bad outcomes regardless of ex-ante risk calculus, driving decision-makers toward bureaucratic risk aversion or rewarding reckless gamblers who happen to get lucky.
- **Leverage Point:** Restructure organizational evaluation metrics to reward strict adherence to mathematically/logically sound Standard Operating Procedures. Explicitly decouple compensation and performance reviews from the influence of luck-driven outcomes to short-circuit this bias.
