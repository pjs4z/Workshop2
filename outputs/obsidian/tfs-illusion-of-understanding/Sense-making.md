# Sense-making

**Definition:** The continuous, automatic cognitive process driven by System 1 that adjusts an individual's mental model of the world to accommodate surprises and maintain a tidy, simple, and coherent worldview.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
As an active occurrent, `[[Sense-making]]` is the engine of rationalization. When a system undergoes unpredictable shifts—such as `[[Regression to the Mean]]` disrupting a previously successful entity's performance—`[[Sense-making]]` immediately scrambles to restore psychological equilibrium by inventing new causal reasons (e.g., "the CEO became complacent"). 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[WYSIATI]]`, `[[Regression to the Mean]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Narrative Fallacy]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This occurrent serves as a core automatic **Motivation** driver. The brain inherently rejects unresolved randomness. When surprise occurs, `[[Sense-making]]` compels the individual to rapidly construct a new `[[Narrative Fallacy]]` rather than sitting with statistical uncertainty.
- **Leverage Point:** To disrupt flawed `[[Sense-making]]`, introduce explicit statistical training (Capability intervention) that reframes extreme success and failure as a combination of average skill plus extreme luck, establishing a default baseline expectation of `[[Regression to the Mean]]` before the mind attempts to invent a causal story.
