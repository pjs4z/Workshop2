# WYSIATI

**Definition:** A cognitive heuristic meaning "What You See Is All There Is," where the mind constructs the best possible coherent story using only the limited information immediately available, while ignoring unknown or missing variables.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
`[[WYSIATI]]` acts as the restrictive filter for human perception. By artificially limiting the data pool to only what is highly salient, it forces the mind to connect unrelated dots, acting as the primary catalyst for our `[[Sense-making]]` routines. The false confidence generated by the `[[Illusion of Understanding]]` further entrenches our reliance on this heuristic.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Illusion of Understanding]]` (Feedback loop reinforcement)
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Sense-making]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
`[[WYSIATI]]` represents a fundamental **Capability** limitation. It prevents individuals from natively processing randomness, statistical noise, or "nonevents" (calamities that didn't happen). 
- **Leverage Point:** Modify the **Opportunity** environment by forcing the visualization of invisible data. When evaluating a competitor's success, implementing a mandatory "Luck Discount" or structurally requiring the documentation of unseen variables prevents the mind from defaulting to a simplistic, visible-only causal chain.
