# Basic Assessment

**Definition:** An evolutionarily shaped, continuous evaluation of the environment determining threat levels, normalcy, and approach-avoidance responses, effortlessly generated without specific intention.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Basic Assessment]] operates as the continuous monitoring function of [[System 1]]. It provides rapid, low-effort evaluations of stimuli (like facial dominance or trustworthiness) which are then co-opted for complex decision-making in modern environments. This process is central to the **Biological Judgment Reinforcement** loop: rapid assessments generate immediate cognitive ease, which decreases the engagement of [[System 2]], thereby reinforcing reliance on biological heuristics for non-survival choices.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Threat Detection Mechanism]] (catalyzes), [[System 1]] (increases), [[Mental Shotgun]] (increases)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Judgment Heuristic]] (acts as a temporal part of)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of electoral behavior (e.g., voting based on superficial facial traits), [[Basic Assessment]] acts as an unconscious **Motivation** driver. Because the assessment is automatic and continuous, it provides an easily accessible substitute for complex political evaluation. 
- **Leverage Point (Choice Architecture):** Blind the [[Threat Detection Mechanism]] and prevent the [[Basic Assessment]] by removing candidate photographs from ballots and replacing them with standardized, text-based policy matrices (Opportunity intervention).
