# Category Prototype

**Definition:** A typical, highly evocative exemplar used by automatic cognitive systems to represent entire categories, facilitating the rapid computation of averages while causing the systemic neglect of aggregate sums.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
A [[Category Prototype]] is central to the **Prototype Neglect Loop**. When faced with large-scale or systemic data, [[System 1]] rapidly generates a prototype (e.g., a single emotional image of a drowning bird). The overwhelming salience of this [[Category Prototype]] actively inhibits the calculation or emotional weighting of a [[Sum-like Variable]]. This failure decreases the likelihood of [[System 2]] engaging in corrective quantitative reasoning, reinforcing unchecked reliance on the prototype.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Sum-like Variable]] (inhibits evaluation of)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In scenarios requiring financial resource allocation or damage valuation, the [[Category Prototype]] acts as an overwhelming **Motivation** driver that exploits a **Capability** deficit (System 1's inherent inability to compute scale). 
- **Leverage Point (Data Visualization Intervention):** To counter prototype dominance, redesign environments to visually translate [[Sum-like Variable]] data into spatial or temporal representations (e.g., infinitely scrolling icons). This forces [[System 1]] to perceive aggregate scale instinctively, bypassing the need for laborious [[System 2]] mathematical effort (Opportunity/Capability intervention).
