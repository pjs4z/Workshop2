# Intensity Matching

**Definition:** The cognitive ability to translate and intuitively align values across fundamentally different dimensions (e.g., predicting a GPA from a child's reading age) based on an underlying scale of intensity.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Intensity Matching]] is a specialized operation of [[System 1]] that allows for the rapid, intuitive mapping of severities, volumes, or ranks across domains. Like [[Basic Assessment]], it serves as a core temporal component of the [[Judgment Heuristic]], allowing complex, multi-dimensional predictive tasks to be bypassed in favor of simple intensity alignments.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 1]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Judgment Heuristic]] (is a temporal part of)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Intensity Matching]] functions as a **Capability** driver. Because it is highly fluent and natural to [[System 1]], it frequently overrides the statistically sound (but laborious) calculations of [[System 2]]. When assessing ambiguous futures or disparate variables, individuals are driven to match intensities because it provides high cognitive ease.
- **Leverage Point (Cognitive Anchoring):** In environments requiring statistical or cross-domain predictions, force users to ground their assessments in base-rates before allowing them to compare variables, effectively blocking the intuitive intensity translation.
