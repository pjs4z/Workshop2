# Mental Shotgun

**Definition:** The involuntary triggering of excess cognitive computations and associative evaluations when attempting to focus on or evaluate a specific, narrow attribute.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Mental Shotgun]] represents a precision failure within human cognition. When [[System 2]] forms an intention to answer a highly specific query, it cannot aim perfectly; instead, it catalyzes a scattershot of [[Basic Assessment]] computations. This excess information generation directly fuels the substitution process found in a [[Judgment Heuristic]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 2]] (catalyzed by deliberate intention/querying)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Basic Assessment]] (increases flow of computations)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Mental Shotgun]] acts as an internal **Motivation** and **Capability** constraint. It makes humans highly susceptible to conflating attributes in ambiguous environments (e.g., judging a company's financial soundness based on liking their product). 
- **Leverage Point (Task Disaggregation):** Redesign complex evaluation forms (e.g., performance reviews, financial assessments) into strictly isolated, single-variable questions (Opportunity intervention). This structural change prevents the [[Mental Shotgun]] from overlapping irrelevant assessments into the target judgment.
