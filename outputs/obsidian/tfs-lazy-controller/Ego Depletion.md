# Ego Depletion

**Definition:** A temporary loss of motivation and reduced capacity for self-control following a prior exertion of cognitive, emotional, or physical willpower. It represents a sequential drainage of a shared pool of mental energy, distinct from the simultaneous interference of cognitive load.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Ego Depletion]] is the psychological manifestation of biological [[Glucose Depletion]]. It is the core mechanism in the *Metabolic Ego Depletion Loop*: as [[System 2]] exerts effort, glucose is drawn down, resulting in [[Ego Depletion]]. This state drastically reduces the motivation and capacity of [[System 2]] to exert further effort, increasing the behavioral dominance of [[System 1]] and forcing a reversion to the [[Law of Least Effort]]. Unlike [[Cognitive Load]], which is a simultaneous bottleneck, [[Ego Depletion]] is a sequential exhaustion. It is uniquely bypassed or inhibited by the state of [[Flow]].

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 2]] (prior exertion of willpower/cognition), [[Glucose Depletion]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Decreases [[System 2]] capacity, Increases [[System 1]] dominance, Increases susceptibility to [[Law of Least Effort]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Ego Depletion]] acts as a profound **Capability** barrier to *Sustaining Self-Control*. 
- **Leverage Points:**
  - **Nutritional Interventions:** Because ego depletion maps to nervous system glucose consumption, ingesting glucose can act as a rapid, temporary physical capability restorer.
  - **Strategic Scheduling:** Mandate that demanding cognitive, emotional, or unselfish tasks are not sequenced back-to-back without restorative physiological breaks.
