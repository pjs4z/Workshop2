# Law of Least Effort

**Definition:** A behavioral principle dictating that individuals naturally avoid demanding mental computation and frequent task switching. People gravitate toward actions and cognitive paths that demand the lowest possible expenditure of physical or mental energy.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Law of Least Effort]] represents the energetic baseline of human cognition. Whenever [[System 2]] is weakened by [[Cognitive Load]] or [[Ego Depletion]], the system defaults to this law. It is the underlying gravity that [[Rationality]] and active cognitive checking must constantly fight against. When fatigued or hungry, this law drives behavior toward the path of least resistance (e.g., parole judges denying parole rather than doing the hard work of evaluating a case).

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Ego Depletion]], Mental/Physical Fatigue, Hunger
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[System 2]], Empowers default/superficial outputs of [[System 1]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This law acts as an omnipresent negative **Motivation** driver, constantly pulling behavior away from deliberate cognitive checking and sustained self-control.
- **Leverage Points:**
  - **Choice Architecture Defaults:** Because individuals naturally follow the path of least effort, systems should be designed so that the "default" choice is the most beneficial or optimal one.
  - **Flow Induction:** By redesigning tasks to trigger [[Flow]], you align the task with intrinsic motivation, bypassing the subjective friction of effort and effectively short-circuiting the individual's desire to seek an easier path.
