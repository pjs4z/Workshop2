# System 1

**Definition:** An automatic, intuitive, and impulsive mental processing system that functions without conscious effort or voluntary control. It exerts greater influence on decision-making and behavior when conscious self-control resources are busy or depleted.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 1]] is the default operating mode of the human mind, generating superficially plausible but often incorrect intuitive impulses. It is normally monitored and inhibited by [[System 2]] via the *Intuition Override Loop*. However, when the mind experiences [[Cognitive Load]] (simultaneous tasking) or [[Ego Depletion]] (sequential exhaustion), the inhibitory power of [[System 2]] fails. Left unchecked, [[System 1]] drives the *Impulsive Glucose Restoration Loop*: because it has a "sweet tooth" and favors immediate gratification, it naturally drives the individual to consume sugary treats, which biologically reverses [[Glucose Depletion]] and eventually restores self-control.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cognitive Load]], [[Ego Depletion]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Generates Intuitive Errors, Drives Impulsive Behaviors (e.g., consumption of sugar)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[System 1]] acts as a powerful, often counterproductive **Motivation** driver, pulling individuals toward immediate gratification, biased default judgments, and the avoidance of mental strain. 
- **Leverage Points:** 
  - **Cognitive Unloading:** Restructure decision environments to remove simultaneous working memory demands when unbiased, virtuous choices are required, preventing [[System 1]] from taking over.
  - **Strategic Scheduling:** Ensure high-stakes decisions occur post-meal, preventing [[System 1]] from defaulting to easy but negatively impactful choices (like parole denial) when the body is hungry.
