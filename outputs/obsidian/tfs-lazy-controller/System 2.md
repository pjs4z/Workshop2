# System 2

**Definition:** A mental processing system responsible for effortful computation, deliberate self-control, and the active monitoring of intuitions. It possesses a limited budget of mental energy and often defaults to a low-effort state, accepting superficial answers unless actively engaged.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 2]] acts as the conscious governor of the mind, fundamentally opposing the automatic impulses of [[System 1]]. When engaged, it actively checks, modifies, or inhibits flawed outputs through the *Intuition Override Loop*. However, engaging this system requires significant metabolic energy, directly increasing [[Glucose Depletion]]. This metabolic drain triggers the *Metabolic Ego Depletion Loop*, causing [[Ego Depletion]], which subsequently weakens the capacity of [[System 2]] and returns the mind to the low-energy state governed by the [[Law of Least Effort]]. 

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Rationality]] (increases engagement), [[Intelligence]] (catalyzes capacity), Explicit Incentives
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[System 1]], Increases [[Glucose Depletion]], Increases [[Ego Depletion]] (sequentially)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the COM-B model, [[System 2]] is the primary **Capability** driver for the target behavior of *Deliberate Cognitive Checking*. 
- **Leverage Points:** 
  - **Pacing and Environmental Design:** Remove simultaneous physical exertion or time pressures to provide the temporal and physical Opportunity for [[System 2]] to function. 
  - **Incentive Insertion:** Apply strong explicit external incentives to temporarily override the motivational deficit of [[Ego Depletion]].
  - **Cognitive Capability Training:** Implement attention-control training to durably improve baseline executive function and algorithmic intelligence.
