# Duration Neglect

**Definition:** A cognitive phenomenon wherein the temporal length of an episode or an entire lifespan exerts almost no influence on its overall retrospective assessment.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Triggered by `[[Prototype Substitution]]`, `[[Duration Neglect]]` explains why the `[[Remembering Self]]` treats a 6-day vacation and a 3-day vacation identically if their concluding moments are of equal quality. It strips away the dimension of time, prioritizing the "story" over the lived reality, which heavily reinforces the structural rules of a `[[Life Narrative]]`.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Prototype Substitution]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Life Narrative]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
`[[Duration Neglect]]` serves as a cognitive **Capability** limitation. Individuals lack the intuitive capability to accurately weight the cumulative length of real-time discomfort against a brief concluding peak, leading them to repeatedly choose painful or grueling experiences for the sake of a story.
- **Leverage Point:** **Capability Intervention** - Redesign choice architecture (e.g., on travel booking websites or medical consent forms) to explicitly visualize the cumulative duration of discomfort versus the brevity of the "peak," deliberately counteracting the mind's tendency to ignore time.
