# Experiencing Self

**Definition:** The cognitive subsystem that exists entirely in the continuous present, undergoing real-time sensory and emotional states without participating in long-term retrospective evaluation.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The `[[Experiencing Self]]` is highly vulnerable to the dictates of the `[[Remembering Self]]`. In the **Experiential Savoring Trade-off**, the pursuit of a curated `[[Life Narrative]]` directly degrades the present moment. Because the `[[Experiencing Self]]` lacks a voice in future decision-making, it is frequently subjected to real-time pain or neglected via `[[Memory Commodification]]` (e.g., tourists experiencing a destination entirely through a camera lens rather than their senses).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Inhibited by `[[Memory Commodification]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** N/A (Does not store memories or drive future decisions)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The suppression of the `[[Experiencing Self]]` acts as a **Capability** barrier to present-moment savoring. Because cognitive bandwidth is monopolized by documenting the present for the future, real-time sensory processing is severely inhibited.
- **Leverage Point:** **Opportunity Intervention** - Design "device-free" environments or temporal zones in tourist destinations to physically remove the means of `[[Memory Commodification]]`, forcing the re-engagement of the `[[Experiencing Self]]`.
