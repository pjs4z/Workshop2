# Memory Commodification

**Definition:** The sociocultural phenomenon where environments, experiences, and behaviors are systematically optimized for the construction and collection of future memories rather than present-moment enjoyment.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
`[[Memory Commodification]]` represents the external, societal manifestation of the internal drive for a `[[Life Narrative]]`. Driven by the demands of the `[[Remembering Self]]`, entire industries (like tourism) are structured to help people collect memories. In the **Narrative Memory Optimization Loop**, this process reinforces the importance of the `[[Life Narrative]]` while simultaneously serving as an upstream inhibitor of the `[[Experiencing Self]]` (the **Experiential Savoring Trade-off**).
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Life Narrative]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Life Narrative]]` (Reinforces), `[[Experiencing Self]]` (Inhibits)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This phenomenon acts as a pervasive physical and social **Opportunity** driver for behaviors like frenetic picture-taking. Smartphones, social media, and tourist infrastructure afford the constant documentation of reality, making it structurally difficult to just "be in the moment."
- **Leverage Point:** Restructuring the physical environment by implementing friction against documentation (e.g., camera-free resorts or social incentives for unplugging) disrupts the **Opportunity** to commodify the memory, thus forcing individuals to default to their `[[Experiencing Self]]`.
