# Peak-End Effect

**Definition:** An evaluative heuristic in which the retrospective judgment of an experience or life is disproportionately determined by its most intense moments (peaks) and its conclusion (end).

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The `[[Peak-End Effect]]` is a direct result of `[[Prototype Substitution]]`, wherein the `[[Remembering Self]]` evaluates an event based on a prototypical snapshot rather than a cumulative sum. By heavily weighting the "peak" and the "end," this occurrent enables the construction of a compelling `[[Life Narrative]]`. It actively works alongside `[[Duration Neglect]]` to distort objective reality in favor of memory storage.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** `[[Prototype Substitution]]`, `[[Remembering Self]]`
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Life Narrative]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The `[[Peak-End Effect]]` functions as an automatic **Motivation** driver. It creates a powerful, impulsive urge to secure tangible proof of an emotional "peak" (e.g., frantically taking a photo of a sunset instead of watching it). 
- **Leverage Point:** **Capability Intervention** - Provide mindfulness or grounding prompts (e.g., guided audio tours focusing on real-time sensory details) just as individuals approach a "peak" experience to shift cognitive bandwidth away from the `[[Remembering Self]]` and reduce the perceived urgency to document the moment.
