# Remembering Self

**Definition:** The cognitive subsystem responsible for retrospectively evaluating episodes, composing them into structured narratives, and storing them to guide future decision-making.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The `[[Remembering Self]]` is the central psychological mechanism that forces humans to interpret their existence as a `[[Life Narrative]]`. To optimize its retrospective evaluations, it catalyzes `[[Prototype Substitution]]`, entirely bypassing cumulative real-time experience in favor of easily stored heuristics like the `[[Peak-End Effect]]` and `[[Duration Neglect]]`. This creates the **Narrative Memory Optimization Loop**, where the psychological dominance of the `[[Remembering Self]]` drives the sociocultural phenomenon of `[[Memory Commodification]]`, ultimately at the expense of the `[[Experiencing Self]]`.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** N/A (Innate cognitive subsystem)
- **Downstream (Drives):** `[[Prototype Substitution]]`, `[[Life Narrative]]`

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In behavioral engineering, the `[[Remembering Self]]` acts as a primary **Motivation** driver. It creates an anticipatory reflective drive to satisfy the future retrospective self, often leading individuals to choose grueling or uncomfortable real-time experiences (e.g., jungle treks) solely for their future memory value. 
- **Leverage Point:** To shift behavior away from narrative obsession, interventions must reframe non-narrative, restorative choices to appeal to the `[[Remembering Self]]` (e.g., marketing a relaxing vacation as a "supreme self-care achievement" to align with its narrative goals).
