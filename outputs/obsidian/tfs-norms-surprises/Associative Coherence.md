# Associative Coherence

**Definition:** The automatic cognitive operation by which disparate fragments of knowledge, events, or sensory inputs are seamlessly linked into a plausible, non-contradictory narrative or causal story.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Associative Coherence]] is the active sense-making process deployed by the brain to resolve incongruities. It sits at the center of the *Causal Intuition Reinforcement* loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed by [[System 1]] and triggered by [[Surprise]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Directly increases the [[Perception of Causality]] and actively updates the [[Personal World Model]] by cementing associative links as definite causal relationships rather than random occurrences.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
As a **Motivation** driver, the brain's craving for [[Associative Coherence]] creates a powerful pull toward cognitive ease, pushing individuals to intuitively explain away complex statistical realities. It relies on the **Opportunity** provided by the environmental presentation of sequential events.
- **Leverage Point (De-biasing Information Architecture):** To prevent false causal narratives, redesign information dashboards, news feeds, or reports to deliberately separate sequential events temporally or visually. Breaking the proximity of events interrupts [[System 1]]'s ability to seamlessly synthesize them via [[Associative Coherence]].
