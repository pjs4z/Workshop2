# Perception of Causality

**Definition:** An innate, automatic cognitive process wherein physical or intentional cause-and-effect relationships are directly perceived from sequential events, independent of logical or statistical reasoning.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Unlike formal logic, the [[Perception of Causality]] is an immediate, almost sensory experience generated by our neural wiring. It reinforces the *Causal Intuition Reinforcement* loop, deeply embedding causal narratives into our baseline reality.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increased by [[Associative Coherence]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Strongly inhibits [[Statistical Reasoning]]. By providing a satisfying, immediate "why" for an event, it suppresses the engagement of [[System 2]] that would otherwise evaluate base rates or random variance.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This phenomenon acts as an innate **Capability** that drives the specific target behavior: *Applying intuitive causal explanations to complex or statistical events*. Because humans are born prepared to make intentional attributions, the absence of an immediate causal narrative feels unnatural.
- **Leverage Point (System 2 Activation):** Because the [[Perception of Causality]] inhibits logical evaluation, environments must be engineered to bridge the capability gap. By mandating structured analytical techniques or explicitly teaching [[Statistical Reasoning]] as a distinct skill, organizations can mitigate the over-application of this innate cognitive illusion.
