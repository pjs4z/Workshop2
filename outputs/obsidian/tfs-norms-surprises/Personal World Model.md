# Personal World Model

**Definition:** A dynamic mental representation of normality constructed through strengthened associative links between circumstances, events, actions, and outcomes that regularly co-occur.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Personal World Model]] represents a person's baseline for reality, deeply informed by broader sociocultural [[Categorical Norms]]. It governs what is considered normal and what triggers an anomaly response. This entity is the core stock in the *Normative Adaptation Homeostasis* balancing loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Catalyzed by [[System 1]] and constantly increased/updated by [[Associative Coherence]]. 
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases [[Passive Expectation]] (the latent cognitive state where events are not consciously awaited but don't shock us). The model's strength continually inhibits and decreases the likelihood of future [[Surprise]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
A heavily populated, causally-linked [[Personal World Model]] acts as an internal **Opportunity** structure. When anomalies repeat in the exact same physical or situational context, the model quickly updates, eliminating the capacity for surprise. 
- **Leverage Point (Anomaly Documentation Protocols):** To prevent the dangerous normalization of hazards in high-reliability organizations, individuals must explicitly document and rationally explain anomalies (engaging [[System 2]]) immediately upon the first occurrence. This prevents the [[Personal World Model]] from quietly integrating the hazard as a new, unexamined norm.
