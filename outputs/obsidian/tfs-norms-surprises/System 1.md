# System 1

**Definition:** An automatic cognitive agent and associative network that rapidly processes information to maintain a mental model of the world, detect anomalies, and intuitively infer causality without conscious effort.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 1]] acts as the continuous, low-metabolic-cost engine of human cognition. It is structurally opposed to, yet integrated with, [[System 2]] (the conscious, deliberate cognitive self). 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Innate evolutionary architecture and continuous sensory input from the environment.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes [[Associative Coherence]] to make sense of the world and continuously builds and updates the [[Personal World Model]]. It is the primary generator of the [[Perception of Causality]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of COM-B, [[System 1]] acts as an immense **Capability** driver. Its ability to rapidly recruit past episodes from memory makes it highly capable of normalizing anomalies to suppress future [[Surprise]]. It also drives the motivation to apply intuitive causal explanations to complex statistical events to achieve cognitive ease. 
- **Leverage Point:** Because [[System 1]] operates automatically, behavioral interventions require **System 2 Activation**—implementing mandatory "statistical checklists" or decision-making pause points that force evaluating ensemble/category base rates (i.e., [[Statistical Reasoning]]) before a causal narrative generated by [[System 1]] is accepted.
