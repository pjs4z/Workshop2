# Attention Allocation

**Definition:** The deliberate, continuous distribution of a strictly limited cognitive resource required to sustain the effortful mental operations of System 2.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Attention Allocation]] is the currency of conscious thought. It is directly increased by [[System 2]] in response to challenging tasks or [[Cognitive Conflict]]. As it increases, it drives two major downstream effects: physically, it causes [[Cognitive-Autonomic Arousal]] (muscle tension, blood pressure spikes), and psychologically, it creates a **Task Absorption and Perceptual Filtering** loop by sharply increasing [[Inattentional Blindness]]. This blindness acts as a reinforcing mechanism, blocking out the automatic orienting responses of [[System 1]] to keep the mind shielded from external distractions (such as a gorilla walking through a basketball game).

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[System 2]], [[Cognitive Conflict]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Cognitive-Autonomic Arousal]], [[Inattentional Blindness]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
As a psychological **Capability**, the strict limits of the [[Attention Allocation]] budget must be respected in behavioral design. To foster **Sustaining effortful attention**, environments must be engineered to be "clean" and free of salient, unexpected stimuli (like loud noises or pop-up notifications) that could allow [[System 1]] to involuntarily hijack the user's attention. Additionally, establishing sociocultural "allowances" (e.g., quiet hours, "do not disturb" norms) can protect an individual's attention budget from interpersonal depletion.
