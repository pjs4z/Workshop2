# Executive Control and Conflict Resolution

**Definition:** A balancing feedback loop wherein cognitive strain mobilizes conscious attention to suppress disruptive automatic impulses, thereby restoring mental equilibrium and goal-directed behavior.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop governs the interaction between the two primary cognitive agents. The mechanism unfolds as follows: [[System 1]] generates automatic, prepotent impulses that clash with logical rules or intentions. This clash catalyzes [[Cognitive Conflict]]. The rising level of [[Cognitive Conflict]] alerts and mobilizes the normally lazy [[System 2]]. In response, [[System 2]] aggressively increases [[Attention Allocation]] to exert deliberate self-control. This targeted [[Attention Allocation]] forcefully inhibits the disruptive automatic outputs of [[System 1]], which consequently decreases the [[Cognitive Conflict]] and successfully resolves the interference.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cognitive Conflict]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[System 1]] (via inhibition)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop is the systemic engine of **Exercising Cognitive Self-Control** (a core psychological **Capability**). To optimize this loop, interventions should focus on capability-building for [[System 2]]. By pre-programming the conscious mind through specialized training, individuals can learn to recognize specific environmental cues—such as the charismatic charm of a manipulative person—as distinct triggers that instantly kickstart this loop, mobilizing attention before an automatic error occurs.
