# System 1

**Definition:** A conceptual cognitive agent representing automatic, fast, and effortless mental processing that continuously generates impressions, intuitions, and impulses without voluntary control.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 1]] acts as the default mode of human cognition, running continuously in the background. It draws upon [[Cultural Normative Knowledge]] to rapidly interpret social cues and environmental stimuli. While highly efficient, its prepotent responses frequently catalyze [[Cognitive Illusion]]s (systematic errors in intuitive thinking) and generate [[Cognitive Conflict]] when its outputs clash with logical rules or deliberate intentions. When this conflict becomes too high, it triggers the intervention of [[System 2]]. Through the [[Executive Control and Conflict Resolution]] loop, its impulsive outputs can be actively inhibited by directed conscious effort.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cultural Normative Knowledge]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Cognitive Illusion]], [[Cognitive Conflict]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of the COM-B model, [[System 1]] primarily functions as an automatic **Motivation** driver. It generates immediate desires, intuitive jumps, and fast reactions that often bypass conscious evaluation. When designing interventions for **Exercising Cognitive Self-Control**, a key leverage point is to implement mandatory delays or environmental "friction." This artificially slows down the decision-making process, preventing [[System 1]] from instantly executing an action and providing the slower [[System 2]] adequate time to detect errors and override the impulse.
