# System 2

**Definition:** A conceptual cognitive agent representing the conscious, reasoning self that allocates limited attention to effortful mental activities, complex computations, and behavioral self-control.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[System 2]] is typically in a comfortable, low-effort mode until it is mobilized by surprise or rising [[Cognitive Conflict]]. Once activated, it functions as the executive controller by dramatically increasing [[Attention Allocation]]. This surge in focused mental effort allows [[System 2]] to inhibit the automatic, error-prone impulses of [[System 1]]. However, sustaining [[System 2]] operations triggers a **Cognitive-Autonomic Depletion** loop, driving up [[Cognitive-Autonomic Arousal]] (e.g., increased heart rate, pupil dilation), which acts as a physical constraint on how long this system can remain engaged. To maintain deep focus, it often leverages [[Inattentional Blindness]] to filter out distracting stimuli.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Cognitive Conflict]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Attention Allocation]], [[System 1]] (inhibits)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[System 2]] acts as the core **Capability** driver for complex behaviors like **Sustaining effortful attention** and overriding biases. Because it relies on a highly limited budget of [[Attention Allocation]], it is easily depleted. Behavioral leverage points include externalizing working memory demands (e.g., using checklists or digital trackers) to mitigate physiological strain, and pre-programming [[System 2]] through training to recognize specific environmental cues as red flags that require immediate analytical skepticism.
