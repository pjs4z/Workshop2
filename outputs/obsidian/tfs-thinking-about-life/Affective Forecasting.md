# Affective Forecasting

**Definition:** The cognitive process of predicting one's future emotional state or well-being in response to significant life changes, such as marriage, relocation, or major purchases.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Affective Forecasting]] is inherently flawed because it relies on incomplete mental models of the future. It is a critical processing step where cognitive biases are translated into expectations.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Focusing Illusion]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Miswanting]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Affective Forecasting]] serves as a core **Motivation** driver for the target behavior of making long-term life commitments and purchases. Because individuals expect a sustained peak in their [[Hedonimeter Total]], they make sub-optimal commitments.
- **Leverage Point:** Counteract erroneous forecasting and [[Duration Neglect]] by implementing a "time-use forecasting" protocol before major commitments. This forces individuals to map out precisely how a new purchase or life change will alter their hour-by-hour daily routine, shifting their focus away from the [[Remembering Self]]'s transitional peak toward the [[Experiencing Self]]'s continuous reality.
