# Focusing Illusion

**Definition:** A cognitive bias wherein an individual assigns excessive importance to a specific aspect of life simply because their attention is currently directed toward it, ignoring other determinants of well-being.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Focusing Illusion]] acts as a primary cognitive distorting lens when evaluating life satisfaction. It is heavily influenced by how human attention anchors on novel or transitioning states while ignoring long-term routine. Over time, the effects of the illusion are naturally neutralized by [[Adaptation]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[WYSIATI]], [[Duration Neglect]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Affective Forecasting]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of evaluating global life satisfaction and making long-term life commitments, the [[Focusing Illusion]] acts as an outsized **Motivation** driver. 
- **Leverage Point:** To mitigate the illusion in decision-making or well-being assessments, design choice-architecture environments that mandate joint evaluation. By evaluating multiple [[Aspirational Goals]] or contrasting alternatives simultaneously, the localized attention on a single novel pursuit is diluted, aligning expectations with inevitable [[Adaptation]]. Additionally, introducing a "domain-broadening" prompt (requiring individuals to rate diverse life domains explicitly) neutralizes the illusion's impact.
