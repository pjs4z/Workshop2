# Miswanting

**Definition:** The process of making suboptimal life choices and commitments due to flawed affective forecasting, typically caused by exaggerating the long-term emotional impact of initially exciting goods or circumstances.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Miswanting]] is the ultimate behavioral consequence of human cognitive biases failing to account for time. It occurs when individuals pursue [[Aspirational Goals]] based on exaggerated anticipated utility, ultimately experiencing a disconnect when the [[Experiencing Self]] lives out the mundane reality.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Affective Forecasting]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Triggers the feedback mechanism detailed in [[The Cycle of Miswanting]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Miswanting]] is the primary target behavior to be reduced in life-planning interventions. 
- **Capability Drivers:** Enabled by [[WYSIATI]], [[Duration Neglect]], and the narrative constructions of the [[Remembering Self]].
- **Opportunity Drivers:** Facilitated by societal [[Aspirational Goals]] and the invisible nature of [[Adaptation]].
- **Motivation Drivers:** Fueled by [[Affective Forecasting]], the [[Focusing Illusion]], and baseline [[Temperamental Disposition]].
- **Leverage Point:** Defeat miswanting by explicitly modeling daily workflows before making commitments. Moving away from abstract predictions to concrete, time-based simulations strips the novelty from the anticipated event.
