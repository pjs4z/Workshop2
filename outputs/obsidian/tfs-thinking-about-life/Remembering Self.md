# Remembering Self

**Definition:** The facet of human cognition that evaluates past episodes by constructing narratives based on critical moments, such as peaks and endings, while systematically ignoring time and duration.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Remembering Self]] is the dominant system used when individuals are asked to evaluate their global life satisfaction. It acts as a heuristic engine, often substituting complex realities for simple narrative peaks. It exists in constant tension with the [[Experiencing Self]], which actually inhabits the moment-to-moment [[Hedonimeter Total]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Temperamental Disposition]], [[Aspirational Goals]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Duration Neglect]], [[Substitution]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The [[Remembering Self]] operates as a **Capability** driver (a cognitive mechanism) for evaluating global life satisfaction, but its evaluations are deeply flawed by [[Substitution]] and [[WYSIATI]].
- **Leverage Point:** To obtain accurate metrics of human happiness, shift well-being measurement protocols away from retrospective surveys governed by the [[Remembering Self]] and toward ecological momentary assessment (experience sampling). Periodically pinging individuals in their natural environment captures a more accurate accounting of well-being grounded in the [[Experiencing Self]], bypassing the cognitive distortions of memory.
