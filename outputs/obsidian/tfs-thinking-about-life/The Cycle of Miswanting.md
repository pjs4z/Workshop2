# The Cycle of Miswanting

**Definition:** A reinforcing systemic feedback loop where cognitive biases lead to poor emotional forecasting and suboptimal life choices, which subsequently fail to sustain happiness due to hedonic adaptation, prompting further flawed forecasting and new pursuits.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop represents the macroeconomic and personal treadmill of desire and disappointment:
1. [[Duration Neglect]] and [[WYSIATI]] catalyze the [[Focusing Illusion]] by exaggerating the anticipated peak or transitional moment of a novel experience.
2. This inflates erroneous [[Affective Forecasting]], which in turn increases [[Miswanting]] as the individual pursues new [[Aspirational Goals]] or major purchases. 
3. Once the goal is attained, [[Adaptation]] inevitably decreases the attention paid to the new state. 
4. Because the novelty wanes, the change fails to sustain an elevated [[Hedonimeter Total]] for the [[Experiencing Self]]. 
5. The resulting baseline return (anchored by [[Temperamental Disposition]]) registers as dissatisfaction to the [[Remembering Self]], prompting the individual to focus on the *next* novel pursuit, reinforcing the [[Focusing Illusion]] and generating further episodes of [[Miswanting]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Breaking this reinforcing loop requires interventions that target both the **Capability** to evaluate time accurately and the **Motivation** to seek novelty. 
- **Leverage Point:** Interrupt the cycle at the forecasting stage. Implement 'time-use forecasting' before allowing the [[Remembering Self]] to finalize a decision. By forcing the cognitive system to explicitly map out how a new commitment interacts with the [[Experiencing Self]] on a mundane Tuesday, the loop loses its reinforcing affective charge.
